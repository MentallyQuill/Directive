# The Black Current Campaign

## Status

This is the implementation-facing campaign baseline for the Directive campaign **The Black Current**. It contains full spoilers and is intended for authors, implementers, Mission Director logic, package-schema work, and playtest preparation.

## Identity baseline

- Campaign title: `The Black Current`
- Campaign package: U.S.S. Serein
- Ship class: Steamrunner-class
- Opening stardate: `53672.1`
- Opening year: 2376
- Primary theater: the Vanta Wake
- Player role: newly promoted Commander/XO, regional recovery commander, and Acting Captain after the prelude
- Runtime architecture: `directive.openWorldCampaign.v2`
- Expected campaign length: 22–35 sessions

## Campaign promise

The Dominion War is over, but the Vanta Wake continues to deliver its wreckage. A migrating subspace current releases damaged vessels, live ordnance, bodies, records, and survivors months after the battles that trapped them. The player commands the Serein's recovery operations while deciding who owns what returns, which people are still legally alive, and whether the machinery causing the Wake can be exposed without destabilizing the corridor.

## Campaign thesis

**The dead do not return to the same world that buried them.**

The campaign tests whether rescue can remain rescue after law, property, memory, intelligence, security, and command begin claiming the recovered. Survival is not the only success axis. The player must decide what institutions deserve authority over people and objects displaced by war, and what forms of evidence are worth preserving when preservation itself creates danger.

## Opening baseline and succession

The player has been aboard the Serein for three days, newly promoted into the Executive Officer billet after the previous XO was killed during the war. Captain Anika Lorne chose an officer with enough independent judgment to command recovery teams, but neither she nor the crew has yet observed the player handling several simultaneous mass-casualty priorities.

At Wreckfall Alpha, the remains of three ships emerge inside an active civilian salvage operation: a hospital transport breaking apart, a weapons carrier with unstable torpedoes, and a powered Starfleet hull transmitting an obsolete challenge. A current surge throws the Serein against a tractored wreck and injures Lorne. The player assumes the conn, determines what can be saved, and prevents the emergence from cascading into Lydian traffic.

Lorne survives with neurological and spinal trauma. Starfleet confirms the player as Acting Captain while preserving the player's separate mandate as regional recovery commander. Lorne can brief, advise, contest, recover, return to limited duty, retire, or eventually resume command according to established medical and command state. Her existence never reduces the player to a caretaker; the player owns the operational record and remains principal mission commander.

## Starting directives

1. Protect civilian life and freedom of navigation in the Vanta Wake.
2. Coordinate rescue, triage, hazardous towing, and survivor transfer.
3. Secure live ordnance and classified technology without prejudicing lawful grave, evidence, or salvage claims.
4. Establish auditable evidence custody and determine the physical cause of the Wake.
5. Reopen safe routes without treating every local responder as subordinate to Starfleet.
6. Preserve command continuity while Captain Lorne remains medically unavailable.
7. Avoid permanent Starfleet civil rule where a legitimate regional recovery system can function.

These directives agree in principle and conflict in nearly every consequential case.

## Ship and mission fit

The Steamrunner-class Serein is a medium escort and route-security vessel adapted for convoy protection, hazardous recovery, and rapid response. Reinforced tractor control, modular cargo bays, high-capacity transporters, workbees, and a wake-analysis overlay allow it to protect a site and tow one major hull. It cannot tow several wrecks, house every survivor, maintain quarantine and morgue capacity indefinitely, or control the entire corridor alone.

The campaign therefore makes civilian tugs, Cardassian identification teams, local medical berths, legal institutions, and shared navigation infrastructure operational necessities rather than decorative allies. A larger explorer would weaken the premise by allowing Starfleet to absorb too much regional capacity; a smaller patrol ship would lack the endurance and credibility to coordinate the Wake.

## Fixed Director-only truths

- The Vanta Wake is a natural subspace fault whose present compression and release cycle was intensified by **Project Mooring**, a classified wartime Starfleet-Cardassian attempt to pin transit paths and redirect Dominion movements.
- Project Mooring used four anchor platforms. Three failed, fragmented, or were deliberately scuttled; **Anchor Seventeen** remains connected to the current and continues accumulating path stress.
- The Wake does not produce time travel. Vessels experience uneven subjective duration because compressed subspace paths isolate them from normal transit and sensor reference.
- The final Black Tide is a convergent release of accumulated hulls and path energy. Its composition derives from known wartime losses, current pressure, anchor disposition, and earlier recovery decisions.
- Commander Elian Ro has the fullest surviving institutional record and a standing covert priority to recover or erase specific Mooring evidence before civilian or Cardassian authorities obtain it.
- Former Mooring engineer Niko Saren survived and is stranded near the Mooring Grave. He possesses incomplete but decisive technical knowledge; he is not a single exposition source and may be reached, lost, discredited, or protected.
- No character or faction begins with the complete truth.

## Story arcs

### The Living Dead

Returned people confront legal death, disrupted families, reassigned careers, and institutions that have already distributed their property or benefits. First Manifest, Forty-Seven Hours Late, and The Hospital Hull can be pursued in any order after Wreckfall. Their facts and precedents establish whether the region treats returned people as patients, witnesses, claimants, crew, citizens, or security objects.

### Claims of the Wake

Rescue labor, salvage title, grave custody, evidence, medical privacy, weapons control, and ship command are deliberately separated. The player can negotiate a recovery compact, recognize Freehold first-response standing, build neutral escrow, centralize Starfleet control, or allow conflict to harden into seizure and work stoppage.

### The Arsenal Below

Weapons emerge inside civilian claims and memorial sites. The player can destroy, disarm, secure, document, or distribute military systems. Tactical certainty reduces immediate danger but may destroy survivors or Project Mooring evidence; exhaustive caution can expose inhabited facilities to drifting ordnance.

### Anchor Seventeen

Technical baselines, testimony, recovered components, classified route records, and Blackmouth expeditions gradually expose Project Mooring. The player may approach the anchor early at higher uncertainty or delay until allies and evidence improve. Repair, shutdown, venting, shared control, or destruction each changes the Black Tide.

### Black Tide

The finale draws from the actual emergence queue, current and anchor state, delegated assets, regional charter, survivor capacity, ordnance disposition, route condition, faction posture, evidence custody, ship damage, and crew strain. The Serein cannot personally cover every site. Earlier institutions and relationships become operational systems under pressure.

## Command Bearing moments

- The opening triple emergence requires a rescue doctrine before complete information exists.
- A returned Starfleet captain is alive, competent, and legally dead while another officer holds her former command.
- Marr recommends destroying a weapons wreck that may contain people behind shielded compartments.
- Freehold crews seize a hull before Starfleet evidence teams arrive but are also the only responders able to stabilize it.
- Ro presents a classified lawful-looking order to recover a Mooring asset ahead of a civilian transport.
- An officer recognizes former shipmates in an emerging wreck and must be supported, reassigned, or retained under pressure.
- Lorne requests return to duty before Sen can provide full medical clearance.
- The Black Tide forces the player to cut loose or abandon a visible wreck to answer a larger distress call.

Command Bearing should appear only at eligible pauses where routine competence is insufficient and a value-laden command judgment is required. Prewritten choices are examples, never limits.

## Core ending families

### Safe Channel — strong combined success

The mass emergence is contained, survivor care functions, Anchor Seventeen is neutralized or legitimately governed, and a durable recovery compact recognizes rescue labor, grave rights, evidence, returned-person status, and Cardassian participation. Project Mooring is documented through evidence that survives institutional review.

### Recovery State — operational success, civic failure

Starfleet secures major hazards and stabilizes the corridor but centralizes custody, marginalizes Freehold crews and returned persons, and leaves cooperation dependent on enforcement. The Serein succeeds as a recovery ship while the region loses political legitimacy.

### Free Wake — autonomy success, security weakness

Local actors govern recovery and resist institutional seizure. Incomplete controls leave some weapons, fraudulent claims, or unstable infrastructure. The region remains resilient, but its safety depends on voluntary coordination and may be vulnerable to a future crisis.

### Cleared by Fire — safety through destruction

The player destroys wrecks, corridors, or Anchor Seventeen to prevent catastrophic release. Immediate casualties may be limited, but survivors, evidence, cultural remains, and technical knowledge are lost. This can be a defensible emergency outcome without becoming a full success.

### Black Tide — severe failure

The convergence overwhelms the regional system, destroys or forces evacuation of a major hub, and leaves several rescue zones abandoned. The campaign concludes through evacuation, inquiry, survivor accounting, and persistent obligations rather than reset or noncanonical game-over.

## Independent command outcome

Regional outcome and command outcome are separate. Lorne may resume command with the player as XO; remain legal captain while the player continues acting command; medically retire while the player is confirmed; enter a negotiated transition; or face review alongside the player. The result follows medical reality, candor, crew confidence, law, and the player's decisions rather than a hidden morality score.

## Mission direction rules

- Situation on rails, solution off rails.
- Every emergence has a causal parent in documented wartime movement, current geometry, anchor activity, elapsed time, or a prior committed decision.
- The Wake is a persistent region; locations, wrecks, survivors, factions, and claims continue outside the foreground quest.
- Calm scientific, memorial, maintenance, and personal content remains possible and need not connect to Project Mooring.
- Factions act between scenes from goals, knowledge, access, and capability rather than simply countering the player.
- Campaign-critical facts have multiple routes: testimony, manifests, telemetry, components, relays, institutional records, and physical examination.
- Senior-staff disagreement remains domain-focused. The bridge is not a council that votes on every order.
- The player can refuse, postpone, delegate, withdraw, negotiate, surrender, or invent an unanticipated solution.
- The Director never writes the player's dialogue, thoughts, feelings, or decisions.

## Failure and recovery

No single failed check ends a mission. Failure produces cost, uncertainty, delay, damage, injury, exposure, altered custody, lost leverage, degraded evidence, transformed objectives, or a harder route. Major losses remain campaign state: a wreck can be destroyed, a survivor can refuse cooperation, an ally can walk away, Ternion can fail, the Serein can be crippled, and Lorne can suffer a setback without invalidating the campaign.
