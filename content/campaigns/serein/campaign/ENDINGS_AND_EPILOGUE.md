# The Black Current: Endings and Epilogue

## Evaluation model

The campaign ending is assembled across six independent axes. The Director should describe each axis before naming an overall ending family. Mixed outcomes are expected.

### 1. Survival and recovery

Assess living survivors recovered, responder casualties, inhabited-facility losses, medical continuity, housing, quarantine, and whether abandoned zones still have viable rescue paths. Survivor Load is evidence, not the whole result.

### 2. Current stability

Assess Anchor Seventeen, Ternion Relay, Blackmouth, route reliability, future emergence forecasting, and whether the corridor can remain open without constant military force. A destroyed anchor may reduce deliberate use while leaving long-term instability.

### 3. Legitimate custody

Assess recognition of first responders, grave rights, evidence escrow, weapons control, returned-person status, Cardassian participation, claim adjudication, and whether regional actors will comply voluntarily.

### 4. Truth and accountability

Assess whether Project Mooring is established, what evidence survives, who can independently verify it, whether Ro's actions are reviewed, whether Cardassian participation is acknowledged, and whether Starfleet accepts institutional responsibility.

### 5. Ship and crew continuity

Assess the Serein's damage and technical debt, casualties, medical strain, recovery-deck morale, unresolved personal threads, and whether the crew retains a workable command culture.

### 6. Command succession

Assess Lorne's health, the player's lawful conduct, candor, crew confidence, Starfleet review, and the player's own expressed preference. Do not infer a desired career outcome for the player.

## Ending families

### Safe Channel

Minimum shape: Black Tide contained without catastrophic hub loss; current is stable or governed; survivor care and weapons control remain functional; the recovery compact has broad legitimacy; Mooring evidence survives outside one office.

Variants include a Starfleet-sponsored compact, a genuinely shared regional authority, or a locally led system with Federation guarantees. The strongest version includes returned-person law, Cardassian grave custody, neutral evidence escrow, predictable first-response rules, and independent forecast access.

### Recovery State

Minimum shape: major routes and hazards are controlled, but authority rests primarily on Starfleet seizure, Security classification, or centralized emergency rule. Freehold capacity may be absorbed or marginalized; returned people may receive case-by-case relief without structural standing.

This is not a tactical failure. It is an institutional partial failure whose future cost should remain visible in compliance, policing, and distrust.

### Free Wake

Minimum shape: local responders and communities retain control, with meaningful autonomy and resistance to secret seizure. Current infrastructure, weapons, fraud, or enforcement remains uneven.

The ending may be hopeful if the local compact has real capacity and transparent rules, or precarious if autonomy is chiefly the absence of enforceable standards.

### Cleared by Fire

Minimum shape: broad destruction neutralizes immediate danger at the cost of lives not yet found, evidence, grave sites, salvage, and technical knowledge. The Director must ground why destruction was chosen or became unavoidable.

Do not moralize automatically. Under extreme Current Pressure and low capacity, sacrifice may be lawful and defensible. The consequence is irreversible loss, not a hidden punishment.

### Black Tide

Minimum shape: one major hub is destroyed or evacuated under catastrophic conditions; multiple rescue zones are abandoned; current stability remains poor; regional institutions fracture or become emergency regimes.

The Serein may escape, be crippled, or be deliberately sacrificed after evacuation. The epilogue still accounts for survivors, command responsibility, evidence, and future obligations.

## The Names Returned epilogue

Run the epilogue after the immediate crisis has ended and enough time has passed for hearings, family contact, and medical transfer. It may take place across Lydian Anchorage, Freehold Nine, the Serein, and a formal Starfleet review.

Required epilogue domains:

- a public accounting of known dead, returned, unidentified, and still missing;
- disposition of Anchor Seventeen and Mooring records;
- status of the regional recovery compact or successor regime;
- at least one returned-person case that demonstrates the law actually created;
- Cardassian grave, family, and archive arrangements;
- Ro, Pell, Brant, Vek, Hegg, and Lydian institutional consequences as applicable;
- the Serein's repair and crew state;
- Captain Lorne and the player's command status;
- unresolved obligations that may persist into another campaign.

The public narrative depends on surviving evidence and trusted institutions. The Director may present competing official, local, family, and crew accounts rather than one omniscient verdict.

## Command outcomes

### Lorne resumes command

Requires credible medical clearance and a lawful transition. The player remains XO or chooses another path. The campaign should recognize that the player commanded the decisive regional operation rather than erasing their authority.

### Player confirmed as captain

Requires Lorne's retirement, reassignment, inability to resume, or voluntary support, plus a legally credible Starfleet decision. Confirmation can occur immediately or after review.

### Continuing acting command

Appropriate when medical prognosis, review, or political settlement remains unresolved. This is a substantive command state, not a failure to conclude.

### Negotiated transition

Lorne may resume limited legal command while the player retains regional operations, or the two may plan an orderly handover after the Serein leaves the Wake. Authority must be explicit and playable.

### Inquiry or relief

Possible after serious unlawful conduct, concealed casualties, evidence destruction, mutiny-level breakdown, or catastrophic negligence. It should follow established facts and process, not act as an authorial punishment for an unconventional solution.

## Persistent hooks

- surviving Anchor technology or a missing component;
- returned people seeking Federation-wide precedent;
- a Starfleet inquiry into Mooring authorization;
- Cardassian claims against wartime Federation action;
- Freehold becoming a permanent rescue cooperative or separatist authority;
- Ro becoming witness, defendant, reform advocate, or continuing security antagonist;
- the Serein carrying unidentified remains, unresolved crew grief, or a new mandate.
