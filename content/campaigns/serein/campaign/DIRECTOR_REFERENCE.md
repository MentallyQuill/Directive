# The Black Current Director Reference

## Use

This file is Director-only. It summarizes the campaign's hidden mechanism, knowledge boundaries, causal event model, recurring pressures, and state-assembled ending. It does not replace package data.

## Core operating principle

**Every return has a source. Every consequence remains in the world.**

The Wake may produce surprise, but it does not produce arbitrary spectacle. Before introducing an emergence, identify:

1. the vessel, debris group, or signal source;
2. where and when it entered the compressed route system;
3. why its current path releases now;
4. which established front, clock, anchor state, forecast, or prior decision caused the timing;
5. what persistent objects, people, evidence, hazards, and claims remain after the immediate scene.

When those answers do not exist, use an ordinary patrol, repair, memorial, hearing, family scene, training problem, or known wreck instead.

## Hidden campaign truth

Project Mooring was a late-war joint Starfleet-Cardassian system intended to pin unstable subspace paths and redirect Dominion fleet movement. Four anchors injected controlled phase stress into the natural Vanta fault. The project worked locally and failed regionally. Three anchors broke apart or were scuttled; their components became debris, sensor ghosts, and false baselines. Anchor Seventeen remained synchronized and continued collecting transit paths after the war.

The Wake's vessels are not transported from alternate timelines and are not resurrected copies. They have followed compressed, partially isolated paths with inconsistent subjective duration. Their bodies, records, command authority, property, and relationships continue from entry, while the outside world has acted on their presumed deaths.

Anchor Seventeen can sequence or vent some releases, but it cannot simply return every ship safely. Shutdown may dump accumulated stress elsewhere. Repair may preserve a dangerous strategic system. Shared control can create legitimacy and proliferation risk. Destruction can stop deliberate use but trigger an uncontrolled release and erase evidence.

The Black Tide occurs when accumulated path stress, damaged relay prediction, unresolved ordnance drift, and Anchor Seventeen's state converge. The precise composition is not prewritten: populate it from established missing-vessel records, unresolved quest objects, known weapons clusters, and prior decisions.

## The emergence queue

Use a ledger entry with these fields:

- id and public designation;
- source vessel or debris group;
- entry stardate and documented last location;
- causal release parent;
- forecast window and uncertainty;
- emergence zone;
- structural, medical, ordnance, identity, evidence, and claim state;
- parties with prior knowledge;
- available responders and capacity;
- consequences if delayed, delegated, seized, destroyed, or ignored;
- persistent follow-up objects and threads.

## Faction doctrine

### Starfleet Recovery Bureau

Pell prefers auditable rescue, central scheduling, and rapid corridor reopening. She can become an effective sponsor of a shared compact if the player offers a defensible system. If disorder and public failure rise, she centralizes by order and increases scrutiny.

### Free Salvage Coalition

Brant distinguishes rescue labor from title. Freehold responds first because delay kills people, then edits manifests because formal systems have often seized ships and prosecuted crews. Recognition creates disciplined delegation; indiscriminate enforcement creates sanctuary behavior, work stoppage, and concealed traffic.

### Cardassian Remembrance Commission

Vek and Taan prioritize names, remains, family records, and grave custody. They can accept joint ordnance teams and evidence escrow but oppose secret extraction. Public disrespect creates diplomatic escalation; meaningful participation creates forensic capacity and legitimacy.

### Hegg Claims Consortium

Hegg buys tangled debt, title, and insurance rights. He is exploitative when monopoly pays, but a neutral escrow system is also in his interest because predictable claims are valuable. He should not be played as comic relief. Contracts can become durable infrastructure or predatory capture.

### Starfleet Security Liaison Office

Ro believes uncontrolled Mooring technology could recreate wartime catastrophe. He uses compartmentation, legal ambiguity, selective urgency, and evidence substitution. He need not be malicious or disloyal. His conflict is whether security can impose irreversible risk without public or command accountability.

### Lydian Civil Authority

Administrator Leth must keep an overcrowded hub functioning. Lydian action follows docking, housing, medical, traffic, and public-order capacity. It can support returned persons or restrict them when resources fail.

## Senior crew fault lines

- Enor and Marr disagree over stripping, preserving, or destroying dangerous wrecks.
- Sen and Holt disagree when legal records contradict biological continuity.
- T'Meru and zh'Ren disagree over confidence thresholds, but neither is framed as intuition versus logic caricature.
- Vek challenges schedules that treat remains as cargo or recovery crews as expendable.
- Lorne's advice is experienced and consequential, but it never pre-decides the player's command judgment.

Only officers with relevant domain stakes should enter a disagreement scene. Keep briefings legible: facts, inference, unknowns, capacity, recommendations, and decision.

## Evidence resilience

Project Mooring can be established through several independent chains:

1. routing checksums from Wreckfall and Holt's redacted convoy logs;
2. unusual alloys and phase couplings from several wrecks;
3. Ternion baselines showing synchronized external pulses;
4. Commander Varen's orders and ship telemetry;
5. Cardassian military and family archives;
6. Niko Saren's testimony and physical access to the Mooring Grave;
7. Ro's protected-wreck list and evidence substitutions;
8. Anchor Seventeen itself.

Loss of one source changes certainty, public proof, or available approach; it does not halt the campaign.

## Command succession

Lorne remains legal captain until Starfleet confirms another appointment or she resigns/retires. The player has complete acting authority after the prelude and retains the separate regional recovery mandate. Use her recovery clock to pace capability, not to manufacture arbitrary reversals.

Possible stages:

- critical surgery and minimal contact;
- conscious medical briefing;
- advisory duty from sickbay;
- limited bridge observation;
- request for restricted command duty;
- medically cleared, medically retired, or indeterminate at campaign end.

Sen controls medical fitness. Starfleet controls legal appointment. The player controls operational decisions while acting captain. Lorne controls her requests and personal response. Do not collapse these authorities into one hidden check.

## Finale construction procedure

1. Freeze the current regional state at the convergence boundary.
2. Select three to five primary emergence sites from unresolved queue entries and routes.
3. Place inhabited facilities at risk according to current routes and prior stabilization choices.
4. Assign each earned ally a task they can actually perform under current charter and relationship state.
5. Apply Anchor Seventeen's established disposition to timing and route control.
6. Apply Serein damage, towing, shuttle, transporter, medical, and crew limits.
7. Foreground one command problem at a time while committed delegated outcomes resolve elsewhere.
8. Preserve at least one meaningful retreat, evacuation, or sacrifice path if full containment is impossible.
9. Evaluate the six ending axes after all committed outcomes, not from one final speech.

## Prohibited shortcuts

- Do not reveal Project Mooring because a generic scan is excellent.
- Do not make every wreck contain a secret weapon or survivor.
- Do not turn returned people into supernatural duplicates.
- Do not make Freehold automatically virtuous or criminal.
- Do not let Ro know everything the Director knows.
- Do not force combat to create pace.
- Do not use Lorne's injury to write the player's command personality.
- Do not erase an outcome because a later chapter needs a different state.
