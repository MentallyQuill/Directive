# Vanta Wake Recovery Charter Reference

## Purpose

The recovery charter is an evolving campaign object. It may begin as Serein standing orders, become a temporary interagency protocol, and end as a durable compact, centralized Starfleet regime, commercial escrow system, Freehold authority, or fractured patchwork. The player is never required to adopt all clauses.

## Separate authorities

A workable charter distinguishes these domains rather than assigning every recovered object to the first armed vessel on scene.

### First response

Defines who may cross a claim perimeter to save life or prevent immediate catastrophe, what logs must be kept, and when emergency access ends. Recognition of Freehold first-response status improves coverage but requires safety standards and auditable handoff.

### Medical custody and consent

Living persons remain patients and rights-bearing individuals. Quarantine and emergency treatment may be imposed under established law, but testimony, intelligence debriefing, family contact, and public identification require appropriate consent or lawful process.

### Returned-person legal status

Provides provisional continuity of personhood, identity documents, pay, citizenship, command standing, property access, and appeal while conflicts are adjudicated. It should avoid making administrative delay a second civil death.

### Remains and grave custody

Creates identification, family notification, cultural practice, evidence preservation, and disturbance rules. Cardassian and other family representatives require meaningful participation rather than ceremonial consultation.

### Salvage and labor

Separates compensation for stabilization and rescue from final title. A responder may earn documented labor claims without owning the vessel, its people, personal property, classified systems, or remains.

### Weapons safety

Allows immediate neutralization when danger is imminent while requiring documentation, compartment search where feasible, and review of irreversible destruction. Tactical custody does not decide final ownership.

### Evidence and archives

Defines chain of custody, neutral escrow, duplicate records, privacy, classification challenge, and independent verification. A strong charter prevents one office from becoming the only custodian of Mooring evidence.

### Navigation and forecasts

Defines Ternion authentication, forecast publication, pilot access, restricted channels, and emergency route priority. Forecast control is political power and must be treated accordingly.

### Claims adjudication

Provides temporary possession rules, conflict-of-interest boundaries, appeals, family representation, commercial finance, and emergency relief. Hegg can support this system, capture it, or be excluded.

### Delegated command

Defines who can command a site when the Serein is elsewhere, what standing orders apply, what decisions require confirmation, and how an independent responder records an emergency deviation.

## Charter qualities tracked through play

- **Breadth:** how many factions and communities accept it.
- **Capacity:** whether recognized responders, berths, tugs, courts, forecasts, and identification teams can execute it.
- **Auditability:** whether logs and evidence survive review.
- **Rights protection:** whether patients, returned persons, families, and crews can contest decisions.
- **Security:** whether weapons and dangerous technology are controlled.
- **Resilience:** whether it functions when the Serein is absent or damaged.

Claims Legitimacy is a compact signal for these qualities, not a substitute for the actual clauses and assets established.

## Sample partial settlements

- Starfleet controls weapons and classified systems; Freehold holds first-response authority; a mixed court decides title.
- Cardassian teams hold remains and family archives; Starfleet and Lydian medics share patient custody; Hegg provides bonded escrow.
- Ternion forecasts are public, but Anchor control requires a multi-party command quorum.
- Freehold operates most rescue sites while Starfleet guarantees external security and appeal.
- Recovery Bureau centralizes the system under emergency law with independent observers but no local veto.

## Failure states

A charter can fail through noncompliance, lack of capacity, exclusion, secret exceptions, unaffordable requirements, corrupt escrow, inability to control weapons, or rules that exist only while the Serein is present. The Director should show which clause fails and why rather than treating legitimacy as abstract approval.
