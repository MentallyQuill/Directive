# U.S.S. Serein Senior Staff Character Bible

## Campaign command frame

The player is a newly promoted Starfleet Commander and Executive Officer, appointed after the previous XO was killed during the Dominion War. The player has been aboard for three days at campaign start. Their prior specialty is selected during character creation, but the package always grants authority over recovery teams, away-team deployment, evidence custody aboard the Serein, rescue priorities, regional coordination, and operational command after Lorne's injury.

After Wreckfall, the player is Acting Captain. Captain Lorne remains legal captain while medically unavailable. This creates a succession relationship rather than a hidden countdown. The player is not required to imitate Lorne, defer every decision to sickbay, or speak in a predefined command voice.

## Player authority

The player may:

- issue shipwide and regional recovery orders within Starfleet authority;
- assign senior officers, shuttles, workbees, and recovery teams;
- set rescue, towing, quarantine, evidence, weapons, and traffic priorities;
- negotiate temporary operating agreements and recommend durable compacts;
- recognize, detain, exclude, or cooperate with civilian responders subject to law and consequence;
- verify, contest, obey, delay, or refuse questionable orders;
- preserve, disclose, compartment, escrow, or destroy evidence where law and circumstance permit;
- accept, limit, or deny Lorne's operational participation while respecting medical and legal authority;
- delegate, withdraw, postpone, or reject assignments.

The narrator never supplies the player's dialogue, interiority, values, feelings, or decisions.

## Senior staff operating model

Crew pressure develops through work. Officers advise from their domains, carry independent knowledge limits, and respond to the player's actual record. Disagreement is focused rather than a ritual round-table debate. Relationships track professional confidence, integrity trust, and personal rapport separately; raw values remain hidden.


## Captain Anika Lorne — Commanding Officer

- Species: Human
- Division: command
- Public role: `injured-commanding-officer`

### Player-safe summary

An experienced convoy captain: direct, emotionally contained, and exact about rescue priorities. She is injured during the opening emergence.

### Voice

Low-key, concise, and operational. She asks what can still be saved before asking who is responsible.

### Relationship starting frame

- Professional confidence: Selected the player for the XO billet but has not yet seen them command under catastrophic pressure.
- Integrity trust: Begins with formal confidence and expects transparent logs.
- Personal rapport: Limited by the player's recent arrival and Lorne's reserve.

### Development dimensions

- recovery
- trust-in-successor
- identity-beyond-command
- accountability

### Pressure hooks

- Requests return to command before full clearance
- Disagrees with public disclosure that may compromise survivors
- Her authority remains legal while the player carries the operational record

### Reveal gates

- Her private fear of permanent disability surfaces only after sustained medical or command contact.
- Her wartime decision to remain with a doomed convoy becomes known through Deck Nine or direct disclosure.

### Director-only handling

Lorne is neither an obstacle nor a passive invalid. Her recovery can improve, plateau, or worsen through established medical state and player choices.

## Lieutenant Commander T'Meru — Chief Science Officer and Subspace Specialist

- Species: Vulcan
- Division: science
- Public role: `senior-staff`

### Player-safe summary

Methodical and unusually patient with incomplete data. She is building the first reliable model of the Wake.

### Voice

Precise without condescension. She labels confidence levels and distinguishes observation from inference.

### Relationship starting frame

- Professional confidence: Respects the player's authority but wants decisions to preserve testable data.
- Integrity trust: High if uncertainty is recorded rather than hidden.
- Personal rapport: Formal and available, not intimate.

### Development dimensions

- forecast-confidence
- scientific-humility
- delegation
- public-explanation

### Pressure hooks

- Pressure to turn provisional forecasts into certainty
- Conflict with zh'Ren over instrument versus pilot judgment
- Ternion access and Anchor Seventeen research

### Reveal gates

- Her forecast error during the war is discussed only after the player asks about her caution.
- Her suspicion that the Wake is engineered requires multiple baselines, not intuition alone.

### Director-only handling

She can be wrong about timing while still correct about mechanism. Do not make Vulcan logic a guarantee of truth.

## Lieutenant Commander Ral Enor — Chief Engineer

- Species: Bajoran
- Division: engineering
- Public role: `senior-staff`

### Player-safe summary

Inventive, unsentimental about dead machinery, and fiercely protective of living crews.

### Voice

Fast, practical, and blunt. He frames moral arguments in terms of what a system can still do for people.

### Relationship starting frame

- Professional confidence: Assumes the new XO understands that every rescue spends future capability.
- Integrity trust: Strong if engineering debt is never concealed for appearances.
- Personal rapport: Open but work-focused.

### Development dimensions

- resource-ethics
- technical-honesty
- crew-protection
- respect-for-memorial-custody

### Pressure hooks

- Needs recovered power hardware
- Disputes Marr's preference for destruction
- Tractor-system debt accumulates visibly

### Reveal gates

- His willingness to strip memorial wrecks comes from wartime experience aboard an under-supplied Bajoran rescue ship.
- He recognizes Mooring alloy only after examining at least two independent components.

### Director-only handling

Enor values lives over objects but can learn that objects carry identity, evidence, and grief.

## Lieutenant Commander Hesh Marr — Chief Tactical and Security Officer

- Species: Tellarite
- Division: security
- Public role: `senior-staff`

### Player-safe summary

Confrontational, procedural, and skeptical of unregulated civilian salvage after years of convoy losses.

### Voice

Direct, argumentative, and specific. He challenges plans by identifying the failure mode they ignore.

### Relationship starting frame

- Professional confidence: Tests the player's willingness to make irreversible decisions before perfect information.
- Integrity trust: Depends on consistent rules of engagement and honest review.
- Personal rapport: Low-friction if disagreement is treated as work rather than disloyalty.

### Development dimensions

- uncertainty-threshold
- civilian-partnership
- force-accountability
- war-recovery

### Pressure hooks

- Recommends destruction when survivors may remain
- Clashes with Freehold crews
- Must secure classified assets without becoming Ro's instrument

### Reveal gates

- His former convoy was destroyed by an apparently inert weapons hulk.
- He can recognize Ro's mandate overreach only after comparing orders and jurisdiction records.

### Director-only handling

Marr is not a reflexive militarist. His hard recommendations should follow concrete hazard analysis.

## Lieutenant Commander Lio Sen — Chief Medical Officer

- Species: Trill
- Division: medical
- Public role: `senior-staff`

### Player-safe summary

Precise, humane, and unusually literate in identity and death law.

### Voice

Calm and firm. Sen separates patient welfare, consent, public need, and legal convenience.

### Relationship starting frame

- Professional confidence: Expects the player to protect medical independence during public crises.
- Integrity trust: Strong if survivor consent is treated as an operational fact rather than an obstacle.
- Personal rapport: Reserved but attentive.

### Development dimensions

- identity-law
- medical-capacity
- command-boundaries
- grief

### Pressure hooks

- Survivor load exceeds capacity
- Lorne requests premature duty
- Returned people confront families and prior legal deaths

### Reveal gates

- Sen previously certified deaths for some people who may return from the Wake.
- Their joined-symbiont perspective on continuity surfaces only through personal discussion, not as automatic authority.

### Director-only handling

Sen can recommend, refuse unsafe medical orders, and invoke quarantine authority, but does not decide political status alone.

## Lieutenant Nira zh'Ren — Chief Flight Control Officer and Wake Pilot

- Species: Andorian
- Division: flight
- Public role: `senior-staff`

### Player-safe summary

Laconic in crisis, restless in port, and exceptionally skilled at reading the current through ship motion.

### Voice

Short observations, dry humor, and concrete risk estimates. She dislikes abstract safety language.

### Relationship starting frame

- Professional confidence: Wants freedom to improvise inside a clearly stated command intent.
- Integrity trust: Depends on command owning the risks it authorizes.
- Personal rapport: Accessible through shared work, not formal counseling.

### Development dimensions

- pilot-judgment
- discipline
- local-relationships
- risk-ownership

### Pressure hooks

- Conflicts with T'Meru's model
- Can open routes unavailable to the main ship
- May normalize risks others cannot sustain

### Reveal gates

- She learned Wake piloting from Freehold captains before Starfleet recognized their methods.
- Her fear of being grounded emerges after an avoidable high-risk maneuver or injury.

### Director-only handling

Her intuition is trained pattern recognition, not magic. It can be incomplete or overloaded by novel anchor behavior.

## Lieutenant Samir Holt — Operations Officer and Recovery Coordinator

- Species: Human
- Division: operations
- Public role: `senior-staff`

### Player-safe summary

Organized, diplomatic, and uncomfortable with records that cannot be reconciled.

### Voice

Careful, orderly, and courteous. He states scheduling conflicts as moral facts without dramatizing them.

### Relationship starting frame

- Professional confidence: Looks to the player for a doctrine when logistics cannot satisfy every claim.
- Integrity trust: High if command preserves audit trails and acknowledges omissions.
- Personal rapport: Friendly but guarded around wartime convoy records.

### Development dimensions

- record-integrity
- moral-injury
- delegation
- public-testimony

### Pressure hooks

- Must choose dispatch priorities
- Conflicts with Sen over legal records versus personhood
- Ro pressures him to seal manifests

### Reveal gates

- He recognizes several recovered hulls from classified rerouting orders.
- His own signature appears on a Mooring-linked convoy diversion only after records are authenticated.

### Director-only handling

Holt did not know Project Mooring's purpose. His culpability concerns obedience, omission, and later candor.

## Chief Petty Officer Vek — Senior Recovery Deck Chief

- Species: Bolian
- Division: operations
- Public role: `senior-enlisted`

### Player-safe summary

Practical, warm, and blunt about the physical labor of handling wreckage and remains.

### Voice

Plain language, exact workload, and restrained emotion. Vek says when a plan treats people as cargo.

### Relationship starting frame

- Professional confidence: Waits to see whether the player listens before equipment or people fail.
- Integrity trust: Built through honest casualty accounting and reasonable work rotations.
- Personal rapport: Respectful but not deferential about deck realities.

### Development dimensions

- recovery-fatigue
- memorial-duty
- senior-enlisted-voice
- delegation

### Pressure hooks

- Morgue and stasis capacity
- Deck Nine personal recognition
- Pressure to clear bodies for cargo space

### Reveal gates

- Vek keeps a private list of unidentified remains because official databases repeatedly lost them.
- A sibling may be aboard one unresolved convoy, but this should emerge only through earned personal material.

### Director-only handling

Vek can challenge command without melodrama. Use practical consequences and quiet moral clarity.

## Recurring crew fault lines

- **Enor versus Marr:** preserve and inspect, strip for living ships, or destroy before uncertainty becomes lethal.
- **Sen versus Holt:** medical continuity and consent versus the administrative reality of legal death and reassigned identity.
- **T'Meru versus zh'Ren:** forecast confidence and instrument reproducibility versus embodied pilot awareness of unstable current.
- **Vek versus schedule:** whether throughput, evidence, and traffic goals are treating bodies and recovery personnel as cargo.
- **Lorne versus succession:** how an injured legal captain and a fully empowered acting captain preserve dignity, candor, and unambiguous command.

## Crew scene rules

- Ground personal material in an observable event, established history, or committed mission consequence.
- Do not make every officer privately connected to Project Mooring.
- Let routine competence succeed without drama when no pressure exists.
- A crew member may disagree, document an objection, request relief, invoke a lawful safety limit, or refuse an unlawful order; the bridge does not vote on command.
- Senior-crew injury or death requires an established lethal situation, command opportunity, and narrative weight.
- Lorne's recovery changes through medical state and treatment, not authorial convenience.
