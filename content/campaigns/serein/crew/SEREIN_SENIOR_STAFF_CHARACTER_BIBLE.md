# U.S.S. Serein Senior Staff Character Bible

## Campaign command frame

The player is a newly promoted Starfleet Commander and Executive Officer, appointed after the previous XO was killed during the Dominion War. The player has been aboard for three days at campaign start. Their prior specialty is selected during character creation, but the package always grants authority over recovery teams, away-team deployment, evidence custody aboard the Serein, rescue priorities, regional coordination, and operational command after Lorne's injury.

After Wreckfall, the player is Acting Captain. Captain Lorne remains legal captain while medically unavailable. This creates a succession relationship rather than a hidden countdown. The player is not required to imitate Lorne, defer every decision to sickbay, or speak in a predefined command voice.

## Player authority

The player may:

- issue shipwide and regional recovery orders within Starfleet authority;
- assign senior officers, shuttles, workbees, and recovery teams;
- set rescue, towing, quarantine, evidence, weapons, and traffic priorities;
- negotiate temporary operating agreements and recommend durable compacts;
- recognize, detain, exclude, or cooperate with civilian responders subject to law and consequence;
- verify, contest, obey, delay, or refuse questionable orders;
- preserve, disclose, compartment, escrow, or destroy evidence where law and circumstance permit;
- accept, limit, or deny Lorne's operational participation while respecting medical and legal authority;
- delegate, withdraw, postpone, or reject assignments.

The narrator never supplies the player's dialogue, interiority, values, feelings, or decisions.

## Senior staff operating model

Crew pressure develops through work. Officers advise from their domains, carry independent knowledge limits, and respond to the player's actual record. Disagreement is focused rather than a ritual round-table debate. Relationships track professional confidence, integrity trust, and personal rapport separately; raw values remain hidden.


## Captain Anika Lorne — Commanding Officer

- Species: Human
- Division: command
- Public role: `injured-commanding-officer`

### Player-safe summary

An experienced convoy captain: direct, emotionally contained, and exact about rescue priorities. She is injured during the opening emergence.

### Voice

Low-key, concise, and operational. She asks what can still be saved before asking who is responsible.

### Relationship starting frame

- Professional confidence: Selected the player for the XO billet but has not yet seen them command under catastrophic pressure.
- Integrity trust: Begins with formal confidence and expects transparent logs.
- Personal rapport: Limited by the player's recent arrival and Lorne's reserve.

### Development dimensions

- recovery
- trust-in-successor
- identity-beyond-command
- accountability

### Pressure hooks

- Requests return to command before full clearance
- Disagrees with public disclosure that may compromise survivors
- Her authority remains legal while the player carries the operational record

### Reveal gates

- Her private fear of permanent disability surfaces only after sustained medical or command contact.
- Her wartime decision to remain with a doomed convoy becomes known through Deck Nine or direct disclosure.

### Director-only handling

Lorne is neither an obstacle nor a passive invalid. Her recovery can improve, plateau, or worsen through established medical state and player choices.

## Lieutenant Commander T'Meru — Chief Science Officer and Subspace Specialist

- Species: Vulcan
- Division: science
- Public role: `senior-staff`

### Player-safe summary

Methodical and unusually patient with incomplete data. She is building the first reliable model of the Wake.

### Voice

Precise without condescension. She labels confidence levels and distinguishes observation from inference.

### Relationship starting frame

- Professional confidence: Respects the player's authority but wants decisions to preserve testable data.
- Integrity trust: High if uncertainty is recorded rather than hidden.
- Personal rapport: Formal and available, not intimate.

### Development dimensions

- forecast-confidence
- scientific-humility
- delegation
- public-explanation

### Pressure hooks

- Pressure to turn provisional forecasts into certainty
- Conflict with zh'Ren over instrument versus pilot judgment
- Ternion access and Anchor Seventeen research

### Reveal gates

- Her forecast error during the war is discussed only after the player asks about her caution.
- Her suspicion that the Wake is engineered requires multiple baselines, not intuition alone.

### Director-only handling

She can be wrong about timing while still correct about mechanism. Do not make Vulcan logic a guarantee of truth.

## Recurring crew fault lines

- **Enor versus Marr:** preserve and inspect, strip for living ships, or destroy before uncertainty becomes lethal.
- **Sen versus Holt:** medical continuity and consent versus the administrative reality of legal death and reassigned identity.
- **T'Meru versus zh'Ren:** forecast confidence and instrument reproducibility versus embodied pilot awareness of unstable current.
- **Vek versus schedule:** whether throughput, evidence, and traffic goals are treating bodies and recovery personnel as cargo.
- **Lorne versus succession:** how an injured legal captain and a fully empowered acting captain preserve dignity, candor, and unambiguous command.

## Crew scene rules

- Ground personal material in an observable event, established history, or committed mission consequence.
- Do not make every officer privately connected to Project Mooring.
- Let routine competence succeed without drama when no pressure exists.
- A crew member may disagree, document an objection, request relief, invoke a lawful safety limit, or refuse an unlawful order; the bridge does not vote on command.
- Senior-crew injury or death requires an established lethal situation, command opportunity, and narrative weight.
- Lorne's recovery changes through medical state and treatment, not authorial convenience.
