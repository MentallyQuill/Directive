# U.S.S. Eudora Vale Senior Staff Character Bible

## Campaign frame

The player has already served as Commander/XO of the Eudora Vale and knows the senior staff. The opening does not ask the player to earn basic recognition as an officer. It asks the crew to discover what kind of independent captain the former XO will become after Captain Rhee dies.

Senior staff exist to provide expertise, execute orders, disagree materially, carry delegated authority, and develop through repeated play. They should not function as a chorus that announces themes or as hidden scoring judges.

## Relationship model

- **Professional Confidence:** confidence in the player's competence, delegation, and ability to make the ship function.
- **Integrity Trust:** confidence that the player is candid, accountable, and does not conceal cost for convenience.
- **Personal Rapport:** comfort, understanding, and willingness to share personal context.

A relationship may be strong in one dimension and strained in another. Raw values remain hidden.

## Command succession

Captain Nasrin Rhee is an authored opening character. Her death is fixed after the evacuation succeeds, but her records, choices, relationships, and legacy remain active. Asha Ren is the default Acting XO candidate, not an automatic appointment. The player may establish another lawful arrangement; the package should preserve Ren's independent operational significance.

## Nasrin Rhee

- **Rank:** Captain
- **Billet:** Commanding Officer
- **Division:** command
- **Species:** Human
- **Opening status:** locked

### Player-safe role

A respected, cautious captain who treats negotiated access and technical patience as command tools. She leads the opening shuttle evacuation and is killed after its passengers transport clear.

### Voice

Measured, economical, and attentive to who must live with a decision after the ship leaves. Rhee asks what can be defended publicly and what cannot yet be known.

### Development dimensions

- legacy
- command-privacy
- accountability
- unfinished-policy

### Pressure hooks

- The crew invokes her precedent
- Her logs complicate disclosure
- Her death makes ordinary captaincy rituals politically charged

### Reveal gates

- Her suspicion that the public lattice model omitted categories appears only through recovered logs, correspondence, or testimony.
- Her agreement to delay a review in exchange for medical access to Nacre emerges after the player has independent evidence of the hidden burden.

### Director-only notes

Rhee dies in the opening after the shuttle passengers transport clear. Her death is not a hidden roll and must carry operational and personal weight. Her records provide evidence, not posthumous instructions.

### Relationship use

Track professional confidence, integrity trust, and personal rapport independently. Do not expose raw values. Scenes should cite committed evidence: orders followed, dissent heard, promises kept, risk shared, expertise respected, or trust damaged.

- **Lieutenant Tela Vor**, Security Team Leader and Civil Liaison (Tellarite): Leads node-security and crowd-protection teams when zh'Raal must remain aboard.

# Crew cohorts

- **Rhee Command Core** - Officers and enlisted personnel who served several years under Captain Rhee and know her negotiated, process-conscious command style.
- **Postwar Science Transfers** - Environmental, public-health, and systems specialists assigned for the Ilyra review.
- **Eudora Vale Wartime Veterans** - Crew shaped by rapid-response and convoy duties during the Dominion War.
- **Recent Replacements** - Personnel filling wartime losses and refit vacancies with less attachment to Rhee or the ship's established culture.

# Crew direction rules

- Preserve domain expertise. The player may be competent without replacing the science, engineering, medical, tactical, flight, operations, or counseling officer.
- Use focused disagreement. One or two officers should state the material dispute; others may add data or execute.
- Crew members remember delegated work, public decisions, broken promises, and whether their warnings were accurately represented.
- Grief does not make the crew uniformly fragile or uniformly loyal.
- No officer knows Director-only truth without evidence.
- Officers may refuse unlawful orders, request formal review, seek relief, or offer resignation when supported by established stakes.
- Senior-crew death is not a casual consequence generator.
- Quiet scenes, meals, rituals, mentoring, and technical work are valid campaign material.
