# The Broken Accord Endings and Epilogue

## Ending method

The campaign ending is assembled from six axes: system survival, burden justice, political legitimacy, ecological continuity, Starfleet role, and captaincy. The finale quest records an ending family, but the epilogue must qualify that family with actual state, casualties, migrations, assets, unresolved obligations, and faction posture.

No ending should be presented as a hidden correct branch. The Director should explain why it became available, what prior work made it possible, and what costs remain.

## A Common Climate

All five populations retain viable futures, Nacre's permanent burden ends, shared authority is lawful, and redundant infrastructure exists. This is difficult, not pristine; name reparations, maintenance, and continuing ecological limits.
## Reformed Accord

The system survives under a legitimate but unequal transition with hard caps, independent telemetry, rotation or review rules, and enforceable exit mechanisms.
## Five Separate Skies

Shared political authority dissolves while warning, navigation, emergency aid, and controlled decommissioning remain coordinated. Sovereignty is gained at the cost of shortages, migration, and lower collective capacity.
## Peace by Allocation

A Starfleet or Secretariat regime limits casualties through technical control without durable consent. Operational success and political failure must both remain visible.
## The Fifth World Falls

Other worlds survive while Nacre suffers mass evacuation or ecological collapse. Do not describe this as victory; record survival, culpability, displaced communities, and the institutional narrative used afterward.
## Broken Worlds

Multiple nodes collapse and at least two populations face catastrophic loss. The campaign concludes through evacuation command, triage, inquiry, and long-term obligations rather than a game-over abstraction.
## Command Outcome

Captaincy disposition is separate from the regional ending. Confirmation, acting status, censure, relief, or voluntary transition follows crew confidence, candor, lawful process, ship risk, and Starfleet scrutiny.

## Minimum success conditions

A campaign may reasonably be called a success when:

- A mass-casualty lattice cascade is contained or transformed into controlled decommissioning.
- At least four world populations retain viable survival and transition paths.
- Nacre's burden is publicly acknowledged and no longer treated as local accident.
- A credible authority or separation protocol can coordinate emergency infrastructure.
- The campaign record preserves evidence and continuing obligations.

This definition permits serious losses and does not guarantee Federation approval.

## Strong success conditions

The strongest outcome additionally requires:

- Viable futures for all five populations.
- An end to Nacre's permanent unconsented sink role.
- Independent telemetry and enforceable burden limits.
- Redundant processing or controlled separation capacity.
- Meaningful representation for Nacre, engineers, and any recognized excluded communities.
- A public reparations and health response.
- Crew and ship capacity sufficient to execute the final plan without concealed catastrophic cost.

## Partial success families

### Operational success, political failure

The lattice is stabilized by Starfleet trusteeship, Secretariat control, or coercive quotas. Casualties are limited, but legitimacy declines and later resistance is likely.

### Political success, material contraction

The worlds reach a legitimate separation or federal arrangement but accept migration, lower habitability, rationing, and lost industrial capacity because alternatives were not built in time.

### Truth without transition

The hidden rotation becomes public, but infrastructure and authority are too weak for a safe change. The epilogue centers on emergency relief, anger, and unfinished duty.

### Survival without justice

Most worlds survive while Nacre continues carrying disproportionate burden under improved limits. This can be a temporary Reformed Accord only if caps, review, reparations, and an exit path are real; otherwise it trends toward Peace by Allocation or Fifth World Falls.

## Failure families

### Fifth World Falls

Nacre suffers mass evacuation or ecological collapse while the other worlds survive. Record this as a system failure even if Starfleet prevented greater total casualties. The Nacre diaspora, evidence, culpability, and the narrative used by surviving authorities remain active.

### Broken Worlds

Multiple nodes fail and at least two populations face catastrophic loss. The Eudora Vale becomes an evacuation and relief command. The campaign concludes with triage, not a reset.

### Institutional failure

A technically survivable result may still produce no credible successor authority, destroyed evidence, militarized infrastructure, or private climate control. These consequences should persist beyond the epilogue.

## Command review

The player begins as an established XO but a new captain. The final review considers:

- Lawful succession and documentation.
- Protection of civilian life.
- Candor about risk and uncertainty.
- Treatment of local authority and technical conscience.
- Ship risk and resource stewardship.
- Evidence custody and public disclosure.
- Crew confidence and dissent.
- Results, including harms the player accepted openly.
- Whether exceptional authority was bounded and relinquished.

Possible command outcomes:

- Confirmed captain of the Eudora Vale.
- Acting Captain pending inquiry.
- Censured but retained.
- Relieved with substantial crew confidence and a defensible record.
- Relieved after serious command failure.
- Voluntary transition after completing the emergency.

The review does not overwrite the regional ending. A player may save the system and lose the chair, or retain command after a compromised result because Starfleet needs continuity and the inquiry remains open.

## Epilogue obligations

The final session should address:

- Immediate relief and migration.
- Nacre health records and reparations.
- Infrastructure ownership and technical governance.
- Accountability for concealment, false flag, sabotage, and negligent certification.
- The future of the Union and technical conscience protections.
- Habitat Six representation if engaged.
- Rhee's legacy and the crew's memorial practice.
- The Eudora Vale's repairs and next orders.
- The player's command status and personal choice, without writing their interiority.
