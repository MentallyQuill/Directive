# The Broken Accord Director Reference

This document is a compact spoiler reference for Mission Director behavior. Package JSON remains the runtime source.

## Non-negotiable truths

1. Captain Rhee dies only after the opening evacuation succeeds. The event is authored, visible, and not a hidden roll.
2. The player has genuine command agency as Acting Captain and may refuse, delay, delegate, withdraw, recognize local authority, or contest Starfleet direction.
3. Nacre's burden is real and deliberately hidden through administrative categories.
4. Abrupt shutdown is physically lethal across all five worlds.
5. The original rotation was mandatory and was not honored.
6. There is no single saboteur behind the campaign.
7. The Eudora Vale can understand and bridge the lattice but cannot replace it.
8. Campaign success and captaincy confirmation are separate axes.

## Knowledge boundaries

- **Rhee:** suspects omitted categories and knows her own bargain; does not know the complete original record before the opening.
- **Osei:** knows the review is politically sensitive and Rhee requested medical access; does not know the hidden rotation or false flag at start.
- **Vorr:** knows public categories were curated and that past governments avoided rotation; he does not know every technical consequence or sabotage action.
- **Senn and Nacre nodekeepers:** know lived burden and many local records; they do not begin with authenticated system-wide proof.
- **Kesh Var and the Union:** know technicians were pressured and reports narrowed; membership has mixed knowledge and culpability.
- **Cael Orin:** knows security is preparing for node conflict; he does not automatically know the false-flag team's entire action.
- **Fenn:** knows his old certification scope; he does not recognize the curated category problem until evidence connects it.
- **The crew:** knows the ship's data and witnessed decisions, not Director-only truth.

## Causal incident ledger

Maintain separate parents for major incidents:

- Crown Station opening surge: aged hardware, Nacre protective reroute, and curated telemetry interacting.
- Aurelia humidity failure: deferred maintenance plus emergency demand.
- Pelagos oscillation: obsolete census and sustained export schedule.
- Ferrum heat failure: industrial priority and failing exchangers.
- Viridia pulse: accumulated compounds and a local industrial test.
- Nacre relay sabotage: one direct-action cell acting without full Assembly authority.
- Accord Security attack: limited false flag intended to justify centralization.
- Spindle component rupture: ordinary material fatigue.
- Contractor pressure: Helioform exploits scarcity through lawful but constraining terms.

Do not retroactively assign all incidents to a revealed faction.

## Briefing discipline

Every consequential briefing should answer:

- What is known?
- How is it known?
- What is inferred?
- What remains unknown?
- What process continues if command waits?
- What can the Eudora Vale and local partners actually do?
- Who is authorized to decide?
- What decision is required now?

Only officers with a material disagreement should speak. Others can supply facts, execute orders, or surface later.

## Command Bearing moments

- **Assume the Chair:** After Rhee's death, the player must issue lawful standing orders, define the Acting XO arrangement, and make the command transition visible without narrating the player's private grief.
- **The First Sacrifice:** The opening load shed must name the population, system, or ship capability carrying the cost. Do not hide it in generic technical success.
- **Publish the Model:** When raw telemetry can be released, pause on timing, custody, explanation, and transition capacity rather than reducing disclosure to honest versus dishonest.
- **Patients Are Not Evidence:** During Nacre medical work, command must decide how health data supports public truth without treating patients as involuntary evidence repositories.
- **Recognize or Retake:** A Nacre node seizure is simultaneously essential service, political self-government, treaty breach, and potential safety risk. Force is not the default answer.
- **Refuse the Single Villain:** When factions demand one culprit, command may insist on incident-specific findings even when a simpler public story would be useful.
- **Sign the Interim Allocation:** Any temporary allocation must identify review dates, safety limits, affected populations, enforcement authority, and a path to exit or revision.
- **Cross the Engineers' Line:** Life-safety work, strikebreaking, technical conscience, and command authority must be separated explicitly before Starfleet personnel enter a stopped facility.
- **Who May Give the Order:** The player can recognize, reform, guarantee, temporarily administer, or decline to create a successor institution. The choice must specify authority and limits.
- **Open Rhee's Quarters:** Mission relevance does not erase privacy. Pause on witnesses, scope, family process, emergency authority, and how the crew understands the succession.
- **Accept or Contest Replacement:** If Starfleet offers a permanent replacement, present lawful options: accept, request delay, contest through review, define a transition, or demonstrate why continuity is operationally necessary.
- **Distribute Command:** The finale requires the player to allocate ship systems, senior staff, shuttles, technical teams, and allies across simultaneous fronts. Delegation must use earned competence and authority.

Command Bearing should recognize judgment, not adherence to an answer key. Eligible moments occur when the player names risk, creates bounded authority, preserves dissent, accepts review, or makes a costly decision visible.

## Clue resilience

The original rotation may be established through:

- Umbra Manifold control cores.
- The Cold Library.
- Rhee's mission records.
- Retired auditor Ines Quill.
- Union certification chains.
- Nacre public-health and maintenance records.
- Physical load behavior that contradicts public categories.

The security false flag may be established through:

- Forensic signatures.
- Contradictory deployment orders.
- Security communications.
- Node witness testimony.
- Component custody.
- Actor confession or internal inquiry.

Losing one site changes certainty, names, timing, and leverage; it does not erase all truth.

## Faction autonomy

At each travel, rest, quest-resolution, and delegation boundary, review fronts and actors. Select only actions supported by knowledge, access, goals, and capability. Avoid adversarial omniscience. Factions may cooperate, misread events, exploit opportunities, split internally, or act too slowly.

## Ending discipline

Do not calculate an ending from one score. Read all axes and committed facts. A technically stable ending can remain unjust or illegitimate. A politically legitimate separation can carry severe material loss. A strong regional result can coexist with Starfleet censure. Name what survives, who pays, what authority remains, and what work continues.
