# Ilyra Lattice Allocation System

## Purpose

This document defines the campaign-specific simulation language for the five-world terraforming lattice. It is an authoring model, not a hard-science claim or a player-visible numeric minigame.

## What the lattice moves

The lattice is a network of orbital processors, atmospheric collectors, heat exchangers, biological catalysts, subspace synchronization nodes, and local climate infrastructure. It redistributes five broad load families:

1. **Water vapor and precipitation potential.** Pelagos is the largest source. Aurelia is the largest predictable consumer.
2. **Waste heat and thermal reserve.** Aurelia and industry export heat; Ferrum depends on imported heat during cold cycles.
3. **Atmospheric compounds.** Viridia's biosphere and processors break down compounds that would otherwise accumulate elsewhere.
4. **Chemical and particulate burden.** Nacre receives concentrated byproducts, corrosive weather, and atmospheric waste.
5. **Instability and timing.** The Harmonic Spindle synchronizes when loads arrive. A safe average can still be lethal when timing or location is wrong.

## World roles

### Aurelia

Political and agricultural center. Receives water vapor and climate stability, exports waste heat, and treats food security as the most legible shared good. Its central risk is converting population and food scale into permanent priority.

### Pelagos

Ocean world supplying water vapor and biological catalysts. Exports change sea level, salinity, buoyancy, storm energy, and deep-water ecology. Official population data undercounts newer floating cities.

### Viridia

Forested world whose biosphere and processors scrub atmospheric compounds. Lattice accounting treats ecological complexity as processing capacity, making a numerically equal allocation capable of irreversible ecological loss.

### Ferrum

Industrial world providing fabrication and heavy maintenance. Its climate depends on imported heat, while political leaders can threaten to withhold the material needed to build alternatives.

### Nacre

The permanent environmental sink. Sealed cities, corroded maintenance zones, chronic illness, and unstable weather are not merely local geology. Nacre's technicians keep the system running while being denied authority over the burden they carry.

## Physical rules for play

- Load cannot disappear because a scene ends.
- Reducing burden in one location must identify where it goes, what system absorbs it, or what consumption stops producing it.
- Emergency storage is temporary and creates a later deadline or maintenance duty.
- The Eudora Vale can bridge timing or synchronization, not erase mass balance.
- Repairs improve margin; they do not settle who may order the system.
- Local shutdown can be lawful and still cause external emergency.
- Ecological injury can be delayed and nonlinear.
- A public model may be numerically correct within curated categories and still conceal injustice.

## Campaign tracks

### Lattice Integrity

Physical ability to absorb changes without linked failure. High integrity lengthens windows and supports controlled separation. Low integrity links emergencies and accelerates the cascade.

### Distribution Equity

Whether burdens and benefits are visible, bounded, and shared through credible rules. This is not equal arithmetic; it includes consent, health, ecology, review, and ability to exit.

### Public Legitimacy

Whether communities and technicians accept authority and process even when decisions hurt. It is shaped by representation, candor, procedural fairness, and whether commitments are implemented.

### Nacre Secession Pressure

The degree to which remaining in the system appears less safe or dignified than unilateral control. Recognition and enforceable limits may lower it; coercion and hidden costs increase it.

### Resource Reserves

Ship feedstock, processors, catalysts, local fabrication, trained labor, spare parts, evacuation capacity, and usable infrastructure.

### Crew Command Confidence

The senior crew's confidence that the player can exercise independent captaincy, delegate, hear dissent, and account for consequences.

### Starfleet Scrutiny

Institutional concern regarding legal authority, public stability, ship risk, disclosure, and the acting command. Scrutiny is not a simple morality score.

### Ecological Continuity

Long-term habitability and biological resilience across all worlds. Immediate survival can still lower continuity.

## Allocation decision template

For any major allocation, the Director should establish:

1. Requested benefit.
2. Immediate recipient population or system.
3. Exported cost and expected arrival time.
4. Confidence and missing data.
5. Available buffer or substitute.
6. Authority requesting the change.
7. People responsible for implementation.
8. Review, limit, and exit conditions.
9. What is recorded publicly.
10. What new obligation persists.

## Closed-cycle alternatives

The system can reduce involuntary transfer through orbital processing near Umbra and the Harmonic Spindle, local world upgrades, consumption reduction, habitat expansion, or managed separation. No single device is sufficient. A strong closed-cycle network requires combinations of:

- Ferrum fabrication.
- Pelagic water buffers.
- Viridian catalysts or substitutes.
- Nacre maintenance knowledge.
- Union labor and safety authority.
- Transparent telemetry.
- Legitimate command coordination.
- Eudora Vale analysis and temporary synchronization.
- Habitat Six transfer and shelter capacity.

Failure to earn one component should alter capacity and cost rather than make the entire path impossible.

## Finale use

During A Common Climate, assign simultaneous pressures only where state supports them. The player distributes command rather than personally solving each subsystem. Every delegated front should identify the actor, asset, route, authority, and risk supporting it. The final configuration may be cooperative, separated, privatized, trustee-administered, unequal, or catastrophic, but it must follow from accumulated state.
