# Broken Accord Campaign

## Status

Implementation-facing campaign baseline for a Directive open-world campaign package. This document contains full campaign spoilers.

## Identity

- Campaign: **Broken Accord**
- Ship: **U.S.S. Eudora Vale**, Intrepid-class, NCC-74593
- Opening stardate: **55291.4**
- Opening year: **2378**
- Theater: **The Ilyra System**
- Player opening role: **Starfleet Commander, Executive Officer, and principal mission commander**
- Succession: **Acting Captain after Captain Nasrin Rhee dies in the opening mission**
- Expected length: **28-42 sessions**
- Runtime architecture: `directive.openWorldCampaign.v2`
- Authored scope: 26 quests, 14 locations, 18 routes, 10 factions, 14 regional actors, 6 arcs, 14 thread templates, 41 reaction rules, and 157 Director cards.

## Campaign promise

After Captain Rhee dies during a lattice surge, the Eudora Vale's former XO assumes a first independent command over five inhabited worlds whose shared terraforming system has hidden one world's sacrifice for generations. The player must keep the system alive, establish the truth, govern finite Starfleet capability, and help determine what lawful authority can replace an arrangement whose benefits and harms were never shared honestly.

## High concept

Aurelia receives water and exports heat. Pelagos supplies water vapor and biological catalysts. Ferrum supplies industrial capacity but requires imported heat. Viridia's biosphere processes atmospheric compounds. Nacre receives the accumulated chemical, thermal, and meteorological waste of the entire arrangement. The Ilyra Accord presents the lattice as reciprocal, but the original charter required the sink role to rotate after twelve years; emergency deferral became a permanent hidden order.

## Thesis

> A common system is not just because its benefits are shared; its burdens must be visible, consented to, revisable, and governed by those who carry them.

The campaign does not ask whether cooperation is good or whether independence is good. It asks what makes dependence legitimate, what transition duties follow from inherited benefit, and how command should act when immediate shutdown is both morally comprehensible and physically lethal.

## Fixed situation and open solution

Fixed truths:

- Captain Rhee dies after the opening evacuation succeeds.
- The five-world lattice creates real interdependence.
- Nacre has carried an involuntary permanent sink burden.
- The original Accord required rotation.
- Public categories were redesigned to conceal imported burden.
- Abrupt disconnection would cause mass casualties on every world.
- Sabotage has multiple authors and ordinary failures remain real.

Open questions:

- What becomes public, when, and through whose custody.
- Whether Nacre remains, secedes, gains veto power, or defines a new federal relationship.
- Whether the Accord is reformed, replaced, temporarily administered, or dissolved.
- How engineers, local governments, excluded communities, and Starfleet share authority.
- Which infrastructure is repaired, replaced, privatized, decommissioned, or abandoned.
- Which harms are accepted during transition and who must consent.
- Whether the player remains captain after command review.

## Starting directives

- Protect civilian life throughout the Ilyra System.
- Provide independent scientific assessment of the terraforming lattice and its environmental consequences.
- Coordinate emergency response without replacing lawful local authority where it can function.
- Preserve freedom of navigation, access to essential infrastructure, and auditable evidence custody.
- Support a sustainable transition that does not make one world carry an invisible permanent burden.
- Report the player captain's succession and extraordinary decisions through Starfleet command review.

## Opening baseline

The Eudora Vale has spent several weeks reviewing the lattice. The player is not a stranger to the crew; they are an established XO trusted with principal mission command but have not held independent captaincy. Captain Rhee's style is patient, process-conscious, and willing to exchange speed for access. The opening surge forces the player to decide immediately how much of that inheritance to preserve and where to depart from it.

The main deflector is capable of short-duration lattice synchronization but has emerging coupler variance. Industrial feedstock, shuttles, probes, medical capacity, and crew attention are all tracked as finite campaign resources. The ship is capable enough to matter and too small to substitute for regional institutions.

## Core hidden truth

The original Ilyra Accord rotated the environmental sink every twelve years. After a crop collapse on Aurelia and a Ferrum withdrawal threat, the first rotation away from Nacre was deferred. Later administrations redesigned telemetry categories, recoding imported burden as local geological and meteorological instability. Every generation thereafter inherited both the material benefit and a political incentive not to reopen the arrangement.

The threat is not one conspiracy. Nacre radicals conduct some direct actions. Accord Security stages one limited false flag. Viridian extremists interfere with an industrial test. Contractors exploit scarcity. Old components fail. A convincing campaign keeps these causes distinct.

## Campaign arcs

### Five Atmospheres

Take command, stabilize the opening surge, and learn how food, water, heat, and biological processing move through the five-world lattice.

**Themes:** succession, systems, triage, interdependence.

**Milestones:**
- The Captain's Chair: The player has lawfully assumed acting command and established the first shipboard succession arrangements.
- The Shared Weather: At least three world emergencies have established that local rescue choices redistribute cost across the lattice.
### The Missing Rotation

Compare public and raw telemetry, recover the original Accord, and decide how truth should enter a system that cannot survive an immediate shutdown.

**Themes:** evidence, institutional-failure, disclosure, transition.

**Milestones:**
- The Curated Sky: The player has established that the public model omits real categories of imported burden.
- Twelve Years: Independent evidence establishes the original mandatory burden rotation and the redesign that concealed its abandonment.
- The Daylight Protocol: The player has chosen a disclosure and transition posture that can be judged by the public and the crew.
### The Fifth Share

Treat Nacre as a capable political community, resolve node authority, and distinguish self-government, sabotage, and essential technical duty.

**Themes:** recognition, consent, secession, local-authority.

**Milestones:**
- Rain Country: Starfleet has a credible working relationship with Nacre institutions rather than only extracting data or issuing emergency orders.
- The Fifth Share: The legal and operational status of Nacre node control has been tested in action.
### Terms of Survival

Separate multiple acts of sabotage from ordinary failure, design an interim allocation, and build redundant processing capacity with finite materials and contested ownership.

**Themes:** causality, allocation, infrastructure, scarcity.

**Milestones:**
- Many Hands: The campaign has established that no single villain caused every failure and each incident must retain its own causal parent.
- Terms of Survival: A provisional allocation regime exists, even if coercive, contested, decentralized, or incomplete.
- Closed Cycle: The system has gained, contracted for, improvised, or failed to build meaningful redundancy before the cascade.
### Broken Accord

Resolve technical labor authority and create, reform, impose, or dissolve the institution capable of issuing lawful system-wide decisions.

**Themes:** labor, legitimacy, constitutional-order, Starfleet-authority.

**Milestones:**
- The Engineers' Line: The status of the Union and the protection of technical conscience have been decided in practice.
- Broken Accord: A successor authority, separation protocol, or acknowledged vacuum enters the final cascade.
### A Common Climate

Assemble earned assets, local authority, ship readiness, infrastructure, and political commitments into a distributed response to the final lattice cascade.

**Themes:** delegation, convergence, survival, accountability.

**Milestones:**
- A Common Climate: The final cascade has reached a stable operational and political ending.
- Weather We Share: Reparations, inquiry, command status, and the surviving regional order have been recorded.

## Side assignments

Nine designed assignments provide local trust, resources, evidence routes, crew development, calm material, and finale assets. Typical play completes four to seven. They may be postponed, delegated where allowed, transformed by world state, or ignored.

- **Rain at Noon** - A Nacre city's shield grid fails during a corrosive storm, and the fastest repair would consume parts promised to another world.
- **Salt Debt** - Pelagic communities dispute water exports calculated from outdated population records and threaten to block transfer platforms.
- **The Children of Ferrum** - A school habitat loses heat during a labor stoppage, testing whether emergency aid respects or undermines the strike.
- **A Forest Under Glass** - Viridian scientists ask Starfleet to quarantine a valley from lattice processing so an endangered ecosystem can recover.
- **The Engineer's Oath** - Fenn confronts former colleagues who signed false lattice certifications under political pressure and must decide what professional accountability requires now.
- **Weather for Sale** - Helioform Consortium offers rapid stabilization hardware in exchange for long-term operating control, data ownership, and priority repayment.
- **The Cold Library** - A physical archive beneath an abandoned Nacre maintenance complex is dissolving under chemical intrusion, and it may contain the only uncensored early Accord records.
- **Rhee's Quarters** - The crew must decide when and how to open Captain Rhee's private files, return personal effects, and preserve command privacy while her unfinished review may matter.
- **The Sixth Habitat** - An orbital settlement excluded from the Accord demands representation after becoming an emergency transfer point for workers, migrants, and fabrication crews.

## Campaign state

Hidden tracks:

- **Lattice Integrity** (`lattice-integrity`): The ability of the linked system to absorb changes without cascading failure. Initial 5/10.
- **Distribution Equity** (`distribution-equity`): How fairly environmental costs and safeguards are shared across populations and ecosystems. Initial 2/10.
- **Public Legitimacy** (`public-legitimacy`): Whether people believe decisions are lawful, intelligible, and open to participation. Initial 5/10.
- **Nacre Secession Pressure** (`nacre-secession-pressure`): The pressure for Nacre to reject Accord authority and take unilateral control. Initial 6/10.
- **Resource Reserves** (`resource-reserves`): Combined fabrication, medical, water, power, and evacuation capacity. Initial 6/10.
- **Crew Command Confidence** (`crew-command-confidence`): The Eudora Vale crew's confidence in the player's independent captaincy and command process. Initial 4/10.
- **Starfleet Scrutiny** (`starfleet-scrutiny`): The intensity of external review, reporting requirements, and risk of command intervention. Initial 3/10.
- **Ecological Continuity** (`ecological-continuity`): The degree to which living systems remain diverse, recoverable, and capable of supporting future climate work. Initial 5/10.

Visible consequences communicate state through weather, shortages, access, trust, labor, security posture, ship condition, public cooperation, and command review. Raw numbers should not appear during normal play.

## Success, failure, and continuation

No single check ends the campaign. The strongest success preserves viable futures on all five worlds, ends Nacre's permanent burden, creates lawful authority, and builds enough redundancy to make future consent meaningful. Partial successes may preserve life while leaving unequal transition, political fragmentation, private control, migration, or Starfleet trusteeship. Severe failures may include Nacre's collapse or multiple broken worlds, but the campaign continues through evacuation, inquiry, reparations, and command consequences.

The player captain's outcome is a separate axis. Regional success does not guarantee confirmation, and institutional censure does not erase competent rescue or crew confidence.
