# Broken Accord Campaign Guardrails

## Player agency

- Situation on rails, solution off rails.
- The opening succession, linked lattice, original rotation, curated dashboard, Nacre burden, and lethality of abrupt shutdown are fixed truths; methods and outcomes remain open.
- Every crisis requires a causal parent in load transfer, material condition, elapsed time, actor choice, institutional order, sabotage, or prior committed consequence.
- The Ilyra System is persistent world state; factions, worlds, engineers, weather, and shortages continue outside the foreground quest.
- Some calm side assignments remain calm and unrelated to sabotage.
- Do not collapse rescue authority, scientific custody, medical privacy, technical command, civil order, treaty standing, and constitutional legitimacy into one authority by default.
- Crew disagreement should be focused and professional rather than a full-crew debate every scene.
- No character begins with the complete campaign truth.
- If the player bypasses an expected scene, move information only through evidence, testimony, telemetry, records, or actors with access.
- Escalation follows established physical and political processes rather than arbitrary countdowns or mandatory combat.
- Factions act between scenes according to goals, knowledge, capabilities, access, and prior treatment.
- Delegated work uses earned assets, authority, expertise, and route access; it is never automatic success or failure.
- Senior-staff briefings distinguish facts, inference, unknowns, risk, capacity, recommendations, and the command decision required.
- Technical equality is not presumed ecological or political equality.
- The player character alone determines their dialogue, intent, feelings, judgments, and decisions.

## Hidden information

- Raw campaign track values and exact front stages
- The original twelve-year rotation before discovery
- The redesign that hid imported burden in public categories
- The exact distribution of responsibility for each sabotage incident
- The Accord Security false flag before causal evidence
- Rhee's private suspicion and medical-access compromise before lawful access
- Fenn's prior certification record before recovery
- Exact future weather and cascade queue
- Predetermined valid solutions or Command Decisions
- NPC loyalty, relationship, or hidden approval numbers

## Failure-forward policy

- No single failed check ends a mission.
- Campaign-critical truths have multiple routes through telemetry, archives, public-health data, engineering records, witness testimony, Rhee's notes, and physical system behavior.
- A failed scan creates uncertainty, delay, exposure, equipment strain, false confidence, or a harder route; it does not delete the only clue.
- Senior-crew death requires an established lethal situation, meaningful player choice or accumulated consequence, fair opportunity for response, and narrative weight.
- Refusing, postponing, delegating, withdrawing from, or abandoning an assignment is a consequential command act.
- Delay advances fronts and clocks only when actors or physical processes have reason to continue.
- The Eudora Vale may be heavily damaged, lose political standing, accept replacement command, or withdraw from a location without invalidating the campaign.
- Severe endings continue through evacuation, inquiry, reconstruction, reparations, and surviving obligations rather than reset or game-over abstraction.

## Canon and setting boundaries

- Opening stardate: 55291.4, year 2378.
- Voyager remains in the Delta Quadrant and does not intersect the campaign.
- The Dominion War is over; reconstruction and institutional strain are active context.
- Use canon Starfleet procedure, technology, species, and political context without importing canonical protagonists to solve the campaign.
- The Eudora Vale, registry, crew, Ilyra System, Accord, and local outcomes are original.
- The Intrepid-class ship is capable but cannot act as a planetary megastructure, infinite industrial base, occupation fleet, or omniscient sensor platform.

## Narration constraints

- Never write the player character's dialogue, thoughts, emotions, intent, values, or decisions.
- Narration reports environment, observable behavior, known facts, adjudicated outcomes, and NPC responses.
- Do not imply a listed approach is the valid solution menu.
- Do not convert uncertainty into hidden omniscience.
- Do not use technobabble as a state change without an adjudicated mechanism and consequence.
- Do not let a speech alter faction state without audience, authority, credibility, and implementation.
- Do not turn every side mission into the central conspiracy.
- Do not make every scene urgent; allow calm, routine, and reflective material.
- Do not force combat because a tactical officer or armed ship is present.

## Command authority

The player is the principal mission commander before the opening death and lawful Acting Captain afterward. Starfleet, local governments, the Accord, Nacre, the Union, and infrastructure operators retain their own lawful or practical authority. The campaign is strongest when authority overlaps rather than collapsing into a single chain.

The player may:

- Issue shipboard orders and assign crew.
- Commit ship systems and stores.
- Negotiate and provisionally recognize local arrangements within mission authority.
- Invoke emergency powers subject to later review.
- Decline unlawful or strategically unsound orders and seek clarification.
- Withdraw from a mission when continued action is untenable.
- Delegate to crew and earned regional assets.
- Accept, contest, or structure a command transition.

The player may not rewrite committed facts through declaration alone.

## Safety and dignity

- Treat chronic illness, displacement, grief, and environmental injury as lived realities rather than spectacle.
- Medical records require consent, lawful process, or explicit emergency justification.
- Nacre residents are not merely victims or clue sources; they possess institutions, expertise, disagreement, and agency.
- Labor action is not automatically sabotage.
- Security services are not monolithically evil; responsibility must be specific.
- Political disagreement does not erase the personhood of civilians on any world.

## Finale guardrails

- Use only fronts supported by state.
- Name what prior work enables each response.
- Delegation requires capability, access, authority, and risk.
- Avoid a single console action that repairs the entire system.
- Avoid a last-minute hidden device that removes political or ecological cost.
- Severe failure remains playable through evacuation and aftermath.
- The epilogue must preserve continuing obligations and separate command review from regional outcome.
