# Ilyra Accord and Regional Faction Reference

## Constitutional baseline

The Ilyra Accord is a multilateral emergency arrangement that became permanent infrastructure government. It coordinates the Harmonic Spindle, transfer quotas, maintenance standards, and public telemetry. Its legitimacy rests on survival and administrative competence; its failure rests on hidden burden, unequal representation, and the absence of enforceable rotation.

The original charter recognized five planetary governments but excluded orbital communities such as Habitat Six. Technical workers implemented allocation without guaranteed authority to refuse unsafe or dishonest orders. Emergency deferrals could be renewed without the consent of the world carrying the burden.

## What a successor settlement must answer

- Who owns or controls the Harmonic Spindle and Umbra Manifold?
- Who may issue emergency node orders?
- What technical conscience and labor protections apply?
- How are raw telemetry and public categories governed?
- What burden limits, rotation, review, or exit rights exist?
- What reparations and medical obligations are owed to Nacre?
- What representation belongs to Habitat Six and other nonplanetary communities?
- How are migration and citizenship handled if worlds separate?
- What role may Starfleet retain, and for how long?
- What happens when a world refuses an allocation essential to another population?

## Factions

## Starfleet Ilyra Review Mission

- **ID:** `starfleet-ilyra-review`
- **Initial posture:** `supportive-but-watchful`
- **Visibility:** `player`

The Starfleet command responsible for the Eudora Vale's technical review, emergency response, and liaison with Federation authorities.

### Director context

Rear Admiral Osei wants no mass-casualty event, no occupation, and a settlement that survives review. Starfleet is willing to grant discretion when the player documents risks, but worries that unilateral recognition or disclosure will create precedents elsewhere.

### Goals

- Prevent mass casualties
- Maintain freedom of navigation
- Produce an independent technical record
- Avoid Federation occupation

### Methods

- Mission orders
- Technical support
- Command review
- Emergency legal authority
## Ilyra Accord Secretariat

- **ID:** `accord-secretariat`
- **Initial posture:** `cooperative-and-controlling`
- **Visibility:** `player`

The interworld institution that administers the terraforming lattice and represents continuity of the five-world Accord.

### Director context

The Secretariat contains competent administrators, frightened continuity advocates, and officials who knowingly maintain curated telemetry. Vorr did not create the original fraud, but he chose to preserve concealment after learning enough to understand its political purpose.

### Goals

- Keep the lattice operating
- Preserve five-world unity
- Prevent panic
- Retain central authority

### Methods

- Allocation orders
- Public telemetry
- Emergency regulations
- Diplomatic procedure
## Nacre Assembly

- **ID:** `nacre-assembly`
- **Initial posture:** `exhausted-and-demanding`
- **Visibility:** `player`

Nacre's elected coalition seeking medical support, control over local nodes, public recognition of environmental harm, and a binding role in any successor system.

### Director context

The Assembly includes staged reformers, immediate secessionists, labor delegates, public-health advocates, and families who have stopped believing promises. It can cooperate if authority is real rather than consultative.

### Goals

- End permanent burden
- Gain node authority
- Secure reparations
- Protect Nacre lives

### Methods

- Assembly resolutions
- Labor action
- Public testimony
- Node control
## Lattice Engineers' Union

- **ID:** `lattice-engineers-union`
- **Initial posture:** `cautious-and-essential`
- **Visibility:** `player`

The cross-world union whose technicians possess the practical knowledge required to operate, repair, or redesign the lattice.

### Director context

The Union is the system's most credible cross-world institution but has been used as a politically neutral instrument. Its strike threat is both labor action and a claim that no allocation is legitimate without the people who execute it.

### Goals

- Protect workers
- Secure technical honesty
- Gain governance rights
- Keep essential systems alive

### Methods

- Work rules
- Coordinated maintenance
- Certification refusal
- Strike action
## Pelagic Council

- **ID:** `pelagic-council`
- **Initial posture:** `wary-and-procedural`
- **Visibility:** `player`

Pelagos' elected interflotilla council, focused on water sovereignty and protection against becoming the next environmental sink.

### Director context

The Council supports Nacre's claim in principle while using procedure to extract enforceable caps. It is internally divided between mobile communities, array operators, and city blocs.

### Goals

- Protect water reserves
- Correct population records
- Limit open-ended export duties
- Preserve Pelagic autonomy

### Methods

- Quota negotiation
- Export throttling
- Census challenges
- Coalition politics
## Ferrum Combine

- **ID:** `ferrum-combine`
- **Initial posture:** `pragmatic-and-suspicious`
- **Visibility:** `player`

A federation of worker councils and industrial cooperatives capable of supplying much of the hardware needed for lattice reconstruction.

### Director context

The Combine can mobilize fabrication or a system-wide stoppage. It will cooperate when treated as a governing partner and resist when labor is requisitioned as if it were Starfleet inventory.

### Goals

- Keep Ferrum habitable
- Protect labor rights
- Secure allocation guarantees
- Control industrial priorities

### Methods

- Fabrication schedules
- Worker councils
- Collective bargaining
- Production stoppage
## Viridian Conservancy

- **ID:** `viridian-conservancy`
- **Initial posture:** `supportive-but-defensive`
- **Visibility:** `player`

The scientific and civic coalition responsible for protecting Viridia's biodiversity and biological processing reserve.

### Director context

The Conservancy supports equity but rejects fixes that make Viridia a less visible sacrifice. A radical minority will damage industrial projects it believes threaten irreversible ecological loss.

### Goals

- Preserve biodiversity
- Limit chemical pulses
- Require ecological accounting
- Prevent industrial capture

### Methods

- Scientific review
- Protected zones
- Public injunctions

## Accord Security Service

- **ID:** `accord-security-service`
- **Initial posture:** `formal-and-escalating`
- **Visibility:** `player`

The interworld security organization responsible for node protection, traffic control, and investigation of sabotage.

### Director context

Most officers are legitimate public-safety personnel. Director Orin and a small continuity cell stage one false-flag incident to justify emergency control. The Service can fracture, reform, or become an enforcement arm depending on how evidence is handled.

### Goals

- Prevent node seizure
- Preserve public order
- Maintain access control
- Protect the Secretariat

### Methods

- Security cordons
- Investigations
- Traffic restrictions
- Emergency warrants
## Helioform Consortium

- **ID:** `helioform-consortium`
- **Initial posture:** `helpful-and-opportunistic`
- **Visibility:** `player`

A private climate-engineering consortium offering rapid orbital processors, financing, and maintenance contracts.

### Director context

Helioform can deliver real short-term capacity. Its price is long-term control of processing infrastructure, proprietary telemetry, and priority repayment clauses that favor politically stable worlds.

### Goals

- Win the processor concession
- Control proprietary telemetry
- Secure long-term maintenance rights
- Avoid liability

### Methods

- Technical demonstrations
- Credit guarantees
- Lobbying
- Exclusive contracts
## Unburdened Network

- **ID:** `unburdened-network`
- **Initial posture:** `fragmented-and-escalating`
- **Visibility:** `latent`

A loose Nacre direct-action network demanding immediate transfer of node control and an end to unconsented load imports.

### Director context

Some cells sabotage relays; others protect protesters, leak records, or maintain essential systems during seizures. They do not share one leader or complete system knowledge. Treating every cell as terrorist organization strengthens the most reckless wing.

### Goals

- End imported burden immediately
- Force recognition
- Protect Nacre technicians
- Break Secretariat control

### Methods

- Leaks
- Occupations
- Relay sabotage
- Mutual aid

# Regional actors

- **Role:** Director, Starfleet Regional Response
- **Faction:** Starfleet Ilyra Review Mission
- **Opening location:** Crown Station
- **Initial posture:** `supportive-but-watchful`

The admiral overseeing the Ilyra review. She supports documented extraordinary action but has little tolerance for unilateral occupation or avoidable public chaos.

**Director context:** Osei knows the review was politically sensitive and that Rhee had requested broader medical access. She does not know the original rotation or false flag at campaign start. She may offer a replacement captain if scrutiny, ship damage, or political fracture becomes severe.

**Knowledge boundary:** Knows Starfleet orders and Rhee's formal reports, Knows the lattice is under strain, Does not know the concealed rotation or full Nacre load

# Settlement families

### Provisional multiworld council

Broad representation and strong legitimacy, but slower operation and disputed permanence. Requires technical implementation and explicit emergency procedure.

### Reformed Accord

Preserves administrative continuity under independent telemetry, Nacre authority, enforceable limits, and constitutional change. Risks keeping old power behind new language.

### Starfleet emergency trusteeship

Strong short-term coordination and weak local legitimacy. Must be time-limited, reviewable, and relinquished or it becomes occupation by infrastructure.

### Five Separate Skies

Dissolves shared political authority while retaining narrow warning, navigation, evacuation, and decommissioning protocols. Gains sovereignty while losing economies of scale.

### Fractured authority

Rival node orders, partial recognition, and security competition. This is a valid failure-forward state, not an unplayable branch; it increases finale coordination cost.
