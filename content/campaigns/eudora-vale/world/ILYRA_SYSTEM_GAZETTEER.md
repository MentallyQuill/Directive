# Ilyra System Gazetteer

This is a Director-facing location reference. It supports repeat visits, side assignments, travel consequences, local scenes, and finale assembly.

## Regional scale

Principal destinations are hours apart. Crown Station and the Harmonic Spindle are central hubs, but the campaign should not make every mission begin there. Each world includes local communities, institutions, and terrain that continue beyond its main quest.

## U.S.S. Eudora Vale

- **ID:** `eudora-vale`
- **Kind:** `starship`
- **Known at opening:** yes
- **Tags:** ship, command, crew, science

### Player-facing orientation

The Intrepid-class ship assigned to review the Ilyra Accord, coordinate emergency response, and provide independent scientific and diplomatic support.

### Director use

The campaign's mobile command center. Its advanced sensors and laboratories can understand the lattice, but its damaged main deflector and limited fabrication stores prevent it from replacing regional infrastructure. Rhee's death turns every ready-room, bridge-watch, and personnel decision into part of the player's new captaincy.

### Everyday life

Crew move around a sealed captain's ready room, environmental specialists maintain rolling load plots in astrometrics, and the industrial replicator ledger is read at every senior briefing.

## Crown Station

- **ID:** `crown-station`
- **Kind:** `regional-hub`
- **Known at opening:** yes
- **Tags:** hub, diplomacy, telemetry, traffic

### Player-facing orientation

The Accord's public control center and diplomatic hub, positioned between Aurelia and Pelagos.

### Director use

Crown Station hosts traffic control, the Accord Secretariat, public telemetry, hearings, and visiting delegations. Its displays omit several categories of burden by design. It is both essential infrastructure and the stage on which continuity presents itself as transparency.

### Everyday life

School groups watch weather maps in the public gallery, engineers pass through access gates beneath political banners, and every delegation maintains a separate room full of private calculations.

## Aurelia

- **ID:** `aurelia`
- **Kind:** `inhabited-world`
- **Known at opening:** yes
- **Tags:** world, agriculture, politics, population

### Player-facing orientation

The Accord's temperate political and agricultural center, with the system's largest population and most visible public institutions.

### Director use

Aurelia imports water vapor, exports waste heat, and benefits from the appearance that its temperate climate is the natural center of the system. Its population is not uniformly privileged: food workers, dry-belt settlements, and urban ration districts bear real risk when allocations change.

### Everyday life

Weather bulletins are civic ritual, market prices respond to humidity forecasts, and children are taught that every loaf of bread is a gift from five worlds.

## Solis Terraces

- **ID:** `solis-terraces`
- **Kind:** `agricultural-complex`
- **Known at opening:** yes
- **Tags:** aurelia, agriculture, climate, food

### Player-facing orientation

A continent-scale agricultural district on Aurelia whose climate towers feed much of the Ilyra System.

### Director use

The opening surge exposes how tightly Aurelia's food security depends on imported Pelagic moisture and exported heat. Repairing the terraces without changing allocation perpetuates the old system; allowing losses creates immediate hunger and political backlash.

### Everyday life

Cooperative crews ride maintenance skiffs between climate towers, harvest forecasts are announced like election results, and emergency shelters double as seed banks.

## Pelagos

- **ID:** `pelagos`
- **Kind:** `inhabited-world`
- **Known at opening:** yes
- **Tags:** world, water, floating-cities, sovereignty

### Player-facing orientation

An ocean world of floating cities, desalination platforms, and deep-water ecosystems that supplies water vapor and biological catalysts to the lattice.

### Director use

Pelagos considers water a sovereign contribution, not an infinite reservoir. Its official population model excludes seasonal and mobile communities, making export obligations larger than leaders admit. Reformers support Nacre while fearing Pelagos will become the next sacrifice world.

### Everyday life

Communities move with storm lanes, public assemblies meet on linked flotillas, and families track water-export quotas beside tide predictions.

## Caldera Array

- **ID:** `caldera-array`
- **Kind:** `water-processing-complex`
- **Known at opening:** yes
- **Tags:** pelagos, water, infrastructure, census

### Player-facing orientation

A deep-water desalination and vapor-transfer complex that controls a large share of Pelagos' contribution to the lattice.

The Array can buffer exports, but doing so changes sea level and storm behavior. It stores old census assumptions in operational code. Pelagic technicians can help rewrite the model if protected from political blame.

## Viridia

- **ID:** `viridia`
- **Kind:** `inhabited-world`
- **Known at opening:** yes
- **Tags:** world, ecology, biodiversity, processing

### Player-facing orientation

A forested world with exceptional biodiversity and a small population that supplies much of the lattice's biological processing capacity.

### Director use

Viridia acts as the system's living scrubber. Its conservation culture supports equity but rejects fixes that industrialize ecosystems or treat biodiversity as consumable infrastructure. Some conservators will sabotage projects they believe cross irreversible thresholds.

### Everyday life

Settlements are built around migration corridors, local weather reports include pollinator counts, and visitors are expected to justify every sealed road and cleared landing area.

A protected Viridian valley containing rare biomes used as a reference baseline for the entire planet.

Oros Valley can be quarantined from lattice processing, preserving ecological continuity while shifting chemical load elsewhere. It is the clearest place to demonstrate that equal tonnage is not equal harm.

## Ferrum

- **ID:** `ferrum`
- **Kind:** `inhabited-world`
- **Known at opening:** yes
- **Tags:** world, industry, labor, heat

### Player-facing orientation

A cold, metal-rich industrial world that receives heat and oxygen in exchange for components needed across the system.

### Director use

Ferrum's worker councils know the lattice makes their lives possible and gives their fabrication labor political leverage. They distrust comfortable-world environmental rhetoric that treats Ferrum machinery as emergency inventory while excluding workers from decisions.

### Everyday life

Shift assemblies are broadcast from factory floors, heat credits appear on household accounts, and every civic building contains an emergency oxygen room.

## Nacre

- **ID:** `nacre`
- **Kind:** `inhabited-world`
- **Known at opening:** yes
- **Tags:** world, pollution, health, maintenance

### Player-facing orientation

A clouded world of sealed cities, corrosive rain, and expanding toxic seas whose maintenance culture keeps difficult environmental systems operating.

### Director use

Nacre has absorbed imported pollutants for generations. Its people possess the system's deepest practical maintenance knowledge and the least political power. The Nacre Assembly contains moderates, secessionists, and direct-action cells; no one group speaks for the whole world.

### Everyday life

Children identify rain chemistry by sound on shield roofs, public art is etched into replaceable corrosion panels, and maintenance shifts are treated as civic service.

## Harmonic Spindle

- **ID:** `harmonic-spindle`
- **Kind:** `lattice-node`
- **Known at opening:** yes
- **Tags:** infrastructure, energy, control, security

### Player-facing orientation

The primary energy and atmospheric-distribution node linking Viridia, Ferrum, and Nacre.

### Director use

The Spindle can reroute heat, gases, and atmospheric mass rapidly, but every emergency adjustment creates delayed consequences. Whoever controls its authorization keys can make temporary decisions with system-wide political force.

### Everyday life

Multiplanet crews work in mixed teams, every control surface displays three competing safety margins, and access corridors are lined with the names of technicians killed keeping the node online.

## Umbra Manifold

- **ID:** `umbra-manifold`
- **Kind:** `processing-complex`
- **Known at opening:** yes
- **Tags:** nacre, processing, archive, hazard

### Player-facing orientation

A deep-orbit complex near Nacre that compresses toxic compounds and exports stabilized material.

### Director use

Umbra contains obsolete processors, dangerous maintenance zones, original Accord records, and the best opportunity to build a genuine closed-cycle replacement. Some physical archives survived because Nacre technicians never trusted central databases.

### Everyday life

Crews sleep in rotating clean rooms, labels are engraved rather than printed, and maintenance apprentices memorize evacuation routes before they learn the control software.

## Ilyra Habitat Six

- **ID:** `sixth-habitat`
- **Kind:** `orbital-settlement`
- **Known at opening:** no
- **Tags:** habitat, representation, evacuation, neutral

### Player-facing orientation

A growing orbital settlement outside the five-world Accord that becomes an emergency transfer and fabrication point.

### Director use

Habitat Six began as a temporary construction camp and was excluded from the Accord's representation formulas. It can provide evacuation capacity and neutral meeting space, but recognition raises the question of whether the successor system has five members or six.

### Everyday life

Residents reuse construction modules as homes, communal kitchens face the system map, and every civic debate begins with whether temporary people are allowed permanent rights.

# Travel practice

Travel advances stardate and may trigger reaction rules. A route may be stable, conditional, hazardous, or temporarily closed. Conditions should arise from weather, node load, debris, traffic control, political access, or engineering limits. Reopening a route is a meaningful local success and potential finale asset.
