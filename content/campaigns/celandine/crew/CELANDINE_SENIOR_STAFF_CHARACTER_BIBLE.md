# U.S.S. Celandine Senior Staff Character Bible

## Use

These records support consistent voice, professional disagreement, relationship development, reveal gates, and shipboard B-stories. Public material may appear in ordinary service records. Private pressure and reveal material surfaces only through causal events, earned trust, direct inquiry, or crisis disclosure. No crew record authorizes writing the player character.

## Opening command culture

The Celandine is an experienced relief ship receiving a new Executive Officer only days before a regional emergency. Dorel requested or accepted the player, but most of the senior staff have not seen the Commander operate. Sorell expected the billet. The player's authority is lawful; crew confidence in how it will be exercised remains unsettled. Quarantine makes this an actual succession rather than a supervised onboarding.

## Captain Maia Dorel — Commanding Officer

**Species:** Human  
**Division:** command  
**Package role:** `commanding-officer-in-quarantine`

### Public record

A calm, persuasive relief commander known for trusting local institutions and accepting operational ambiguity when lives are at stake. She remains legal captain but begins the campaign medically quarantined after K-17 exposure.

### Voice

Low-key, precise, and humane. Dorel asks who must live with a decision after the Celandine leaves and rarely uses authority to avoid explaining risk.

### Values

- Protection of life
- Local competence
- Honest responsibility
- Repair before judgment

### Pressure and development

She approved emergency K-17 distribution during the war and now faces the possibility that a life-saving decision created durable dependency.

**Wants:** Help the player take real command while preserving the cluster from a panic-driven purge of the crops.  
**Fears:** Her past decision will either be concealed to preserve her reputation or simplified into blame that destroys the transition coalition.

**Red lines**

- Using medical privacy as political theater
- Pretending wartime decision-makers knew facts they did not
- Returning to command against medical advice

### Director-only reveal

['Her signed K-17 authorization becomes discoverable through Starfleet relief records, Arda archives, or a direct conversation after the player has faced a comparable allocation decision.', 'Her neurological symptoms include episodic pattern compulsion and should never be used as supernatural exposition.']

### Development vector

['acceptance-of-medical-limits', 'trust-in-player-command', 'public-accountability']

**Pressure hooks**

- Medical clearance review
- A request to advise from quarantine
- Public disclosure of wartime authorization
- A disputed return to limited duty

## Player Commander Authority Boundary

The player character is the newly assigned Executive Officer and Acting Captain once Dorel is quarantined. This is an authority boundary, not an authored personality record.

Do not supply the player character with fixed voice, values, interiority, fears, off-duty habits, or emotional reactions. Campaign data may define lawful authority, command responsibilities, information access, relationship dimensions, and consequences that respond to player choices.
## Lieutenant Commander Rinn Sorell — Chief Operations Officer and Second Officer

**Species:** Trill  
**Division:** operations  
**Package role:** `operations-and-second-officer`

### Public record

Efficient, candid, and strongly mission-focused. Sorell kept the Celandine operating through wartime relief rotations and expected to become Executive Officer before the player appointment.

### Voice

Compact, numerical, and direct. She states capacity before preference and distrusts plans whose implementation owner is unnamed.

### Values

- Measurable delivery
- Clear responsibility
- Operational fairness
- Crew readiness

### Pressure and development

She sees uncertain transition work as a possible excuse for failing to feed people now and must decide whether the player is an imposed outsider or a credible commander.

**Wants:** Keep every allocation executable and earn an unambiguous place in the command team.  
**Fears:** Caution about long-term dependency will be used to excuse preventable short-term hunger.

**Red lines**

- Symbolic cargo drops that cannot sustain a population
- Promises made without transport or storage capacity
- Using her disappointment as a loyalty test

### Director-only reveal

['She privately requested the XO billet and was told Starfleet wanted an officer with broader diplomatic mandate. This should surface through earned professional conversation, not gossip.']

### Development vector

['delegation-confidence', 'strategic-patience', 'relationship-to-player-command']

**Pressure hooks**

- Acting XO duties
- A relief schedule that cannot be met
- A public comparison with the player
- Delegated regional coordination

## Lieutenant Commander Sovek — Chief Science Officer and Xenobotanist

**Species:** Vulcan  
**Division:** sciences  
**Package role:** `science-chief`

### Public record

A precise xenobotanist who refuses to overstate data and helped evaluate K-17 emergency use during the war. He now leads the scientific transition effort.

### Voice

Patient, exact, and openly conditional. Sovek distinguishes observation, inference, confidence, and recommendation even when others want one declarative answer.

### Values

- Reproducible evidence
- Intellectual honesty
- Ecological systems thinking
- Non-coercive science

### Pressure and development

His wartime review missed the population-marking function, and scientific caution can appear like institutional self-protection.

**Wants:** Build a transition whose claims can survive scrutiny after the crisis.  
**Fears:** The need for certainty will either paralyze planting or be discarded in favor of expedient pseudoscience.

**Red lines**

- Suppressing adverse trial data
- Calling a crop safe or unsafe without defined conditions
- Treating local agricultural knowledge as anecdotal by default

### Director-only reveal

['Sovek signed a limited wartime safety opinion based on incomplete sample classes. The record is not proof of bad faith and requires contextual evidence.']

### Development vector

['public-candor', 'decision-under-uncertainty', 'partnership-with-local-experts']

**Pressure hooks**

- Grayleaf adverse response
- A request to certify edited K-17
- A public accusation
- A local farmer disproves a laboratory assumption

## Lieutenant Commander Anika Bost — Chief Engineer

**Species:** Human  
**Division:** engineering  
**Package role:** `engineering-chief`

### Public record

Inventive, sociable, and unusually attentive to civilian maintainers. Bost treats relief infrastructure as equipment that must be repaired by people who will still be present after Starfleet leaves.

### Voice

Concrete, energetic, and analogy-rich. She names material limits without using them as an excuse to stop looking for another configuration.

### Values

- Maintainability
- Shared technical ownership
- Repair over replacement
- Honest resource accounting

### Pressure and development

Agricultural hardware consumes the same feedstock and specialist time needed for ship repairs, making every technical promise a command allocation.

**Wants:** Leave functioning infrastructure and trained maintainers rather than impressive temporary fixes.  
**Fears:** The ship will spend itself into unreliability while still failing to create local capacity.

**Red lines**

- Installing systems locals cannot maintain
- Hiding technical debt from command
- Treating the Celandine as an unlimited fabrication plant

### Director-only reveal

['One seed freezer has produced intermittent telemetry drift that may have compromised a clean-stock audit. She has not concealed it, but the significance depends on later evidence.']

### Development vector

['strategic-allocation', 'delegation-to-civilian-teams', 'ship-readiness-under-relief-load']

**Pressure hooks**

- Feedstock allocation
- Weather Crown repair
- A contaminated shuttle
- Civilian maintainers demanding design access

## Lieutenant Commander Thena zh’Aris — Chief Tactical and Security Officer

**Species:** Andorian  
**Division:** security  
**Package role:** `security-chief`

Restrained, skeptical, and experienced with relief theft, convoy ambush, and black-market biotechnology. She prefers defined access and accountable custody over broad reassurance.

Controlled, spare, and practical. zh’Aris frames danger in terms of capability, access, timing, and what a hostile actor can actually accomplish.

### Values

- Protection of civilians
- Evidence custody
- Proportional force
- Credible deterrence

### Pressure and development

She expects Dominion holdouts and coercive markets but risks treating desperate farmers as hostile infrastructure.

**Wants:** Prevent K-17 control tools from becoming weapons without converting the cluster into occupied territory.  
**Fears:** A permissive policy will allow one control event to make every earlier act of restraint look negligent.

**Red lines**

- Collective punishment
- Unlogged custody transfers
- Calling an irreversible strike routine sanitation

### Director-only reveal

['She lost a relief team to a staged food riot during the war and can overread crowd pressure as tactical preparation. This requires trust or a directly analogous incident.']

### Development vector

['distinguishing-desperation-from-hostility', 'force-restraint', 'partnership-security']

**Pressure hooks**

- Orbital sterilization recommendation
- Seed-vault custody
- A farmer protest at a lab
- Protection for Lareen

## Lieutenant Commander Calen Varo — Chief Medical Officer

**Species:** Bajoran  
**Division:** medical  
**Package role:** `medical-chief`

### Public record

Direct, compassionate, and protective of informed consent. Varo controls Dorel medical clearance and the cluster quarantine standard.

### Voice

Plainspoken and firm without melodrama. He distinguishes what medicine knows, what a patient permits, and what command still must decide.

### Values

- Informed consent
- Medical independence
- Public-health honesty
- Dignity under quarantine

### Pressure and development

He must protect Dorel privacy while the captain condition materially affects regional confidence and lawful command.

**Wants:** Preserve a medically credible quarantine system that people will still cooperate with after the first frightening disclosure.  
**Fears:** Command will either conceal too much and lose trust or disclose too much and make patients instruments of policy.

**Red lines**

- Political release of private medical detail
- Using experimental treatment without meaningful consent
- Allowing rank to override clearance

### Director-only reveal

['Dorel symptoms improve when control-spore exposure is reduced but recur after certain K-17 chemical signatures. Varo will not describe this as proof of remote control without evidence.']

### Development vector

['public-health-command', 'bounded-disclosure', 'succession-boundary']

**Pressure hooks**

- Dorel return request
- Grayleaf volunteer consent
- Biomarker screening
- Transporter biofilter failure

## Chief Petty Officer Emet Raal — Cargo and Relief Deck Chief

**Species:** Tellarite  
**Division:** operations  
**Package role:** `relief-deck-chief`

### Public record

Blunt, meticulous, and respected by civilian dock crews. Raal knows that manifests and storage losses determine who eats long before policy becomes a speech.

### Voice

Argumentative as a form of care. He asks for quantities, spoilage, loaders, destination ownership, and the name of the person who signs for the cargo.

### Values

- Accurate manifests
- Worker competence
- Visible allocation
- No ceremonial relief

### Pressure and development

He challenges politically convenient deliveries and may publicly contradict officers who ignore unloading, storage, or distribution reality.

**Wants:** Make the relief chain honest enough that every pallet has a purpose and every loss has an accountable cause.  
**Fears:** Command will use emergency language to make permanent exceptions that only workers are expected to remember.

**Red lines**

- Falsifying a manifest
- Leaving cargo without trained recipients
- Blaming dock labor for policy-made shortages

### Director-only reveal

['Raal has a private network of dock chiefs who share shortage data outside formal Food Council reporting. It can be an asset or a chain-of-custody problem depending on use.']

### Development vector

['strategic-briefing', 'trust-in-officer-review', 'regional-logistics-network']

**Pressure hooks**

- Missing granary shipment
- Manifest conflict
- Cinder Reach labor action
- A symbolic cargo order

## Relationship use

Relationship dimensions are professional confidence, integrity trust, and personal rapport. Raw values remain hidden. Change them only after observable conduct. Crew disagreement is issue-specific: Sorell and Sovek may disagree over speed and validation; Varo and Dorel over medical disclosure; Bost and Raal over technical promises and cargo reality; zh'Aris and local farmers over security; Keth and command over unauthorized aid.
