# Cyradon Relief Cluster Gazetteer

## Regional character

Cyradon is a compact network of agricultural worlds and orbital infrastructure recovering from war. Travel is measured in hours, but every movement requires cargo preparation, quarantine, local clearance, and labor. The cluster should feel inhabited: farms, ration offices, kitchens, markets, clinics, seed exchanges, processing plants, union halls, wetlands, and repair scaffolds continue between crises.

## Locations

### U.S.S. Celandine

**ID:** `uss-celandine`  
**Kind:** starship  
**Initially known:** Yes

The Norway-class regional relief vessel serving as the player operational base, mobile laboratory, quarantine platform, and limited convoy escort.

**Director direction:** The ship begins with clean seed, laboratory, and relief capacity but cannot replace regional agriculture. Its quarantine routines, freezer reliability, shuttle availability, and crew trust are persistent state.

**Everyday life:** Shift changes overlap with field teams returning through decontamination. The relief deck smells of nutrient gel, soil sealant, and sterilizer catalyst; crew meals increasingly include local produce when biosecurity permits.

**Services:** medical isolation; seed storage; laboratories; cargo transport; diplomatic conference.

**Hazards:** K-17 contamination; finite stores; succession pressure.

### Verdant Station

**ID:** `verdant-station`  
**Kind:** orbital-station  
**Initially known:** Yes

The cluster coordination hub for Starfleet Relief Command, the Cyradon Food Council, clinics, seed laboratories, hearings, and interworld cargo transfer.

**Director direction:** Verdant turns hunger into schedules, reserve figures, and political claims. Its reports are necessary but lag local markets and can be shaped by emergency-power incentives.

**Everyday life:** Dock workers trade weather reports before manifests arrive. Families queue at clinic kiosks, seed auditors carry sealed sample cases, and every public hearing spills into food stalls along the cargo ring.

**Services:** public hearings; cargo transfer; medical clinics; seed testing; conference chambers.

**Hazards:** crowding; rumors; political pressure.

### Arda II

**ID:** `arda-ii`  
**Kind:** agricultural-world  
**Initially known:** Yes

The cluster largest population and grain producer. Vast K-17 terraces feed neighboring worlds, and the opening processing-tower exposure occurs here.

**Director direction:** Arda has the best storage and export infrastructure but the deepest monoculture. A control signal, targeted blight, or prolonged quarantine can turn its scale from strength into regional catastrophe.

**Everyday life:** Terrace trains run through the night during harvest. Markets sell a hundred preparations of the same staple grain, while older residents remember foods no longer grown at commercial scale.

**Services:** grain storage; processing towers; field hospitals; heavy cargo pads.

**Hazards:** control-spore exposure; monoculture blight; storm damage.

### Khepri

**ID:** `khepri`  
**Kind:** agricultural-world  
**Initially known:** Yes

A dry world of cooperative farms, subterranean reservoirs, and strong seed-saving traditions. It preserved fragments of pre-K-17 crop lines.

**Director direction:** Khepri can anchor clean-seed recovery, but drought, low yields, and political pressure make every heritage plot vulnerable to requisition or romantic overstatement.

**Everyday life:** Farm councils meet beside reservoir gauges. Seed exchanges are social events with songs, legal witnesses, and handwritten lineage books kept beside modern genomic records.

**Services:** seed libraries; reservoir access; dryland trial plots; farmer councils.

**Hazards:** drought; low yield; seed theft.

### Sorell

**ID:** `sorell`  
**Kind:** agricultural-moon  
**Initially known:** Yes

A humid moon whose K-17 protein vines grow through reclaimed industrial zones. Harvest authorities tightly control seed and ration access.

**Director direction:** Sorell prevented famine through centralized allocation, but the same system ties food, citizenship, employment, and political compliance together.

**Everyday life:** Ration halls double as neighborhood councils and employment offices. Vine bridges cross old factories, and residents track allocation changes with the same attention other worlds give weather.

**Services:** protein processing; ration registry; urban farms; industrial workshops.

**Hazards:** political rationing; crowd unrest; registry abuse.

### Vela Nacre

**ID:** `vela-nacre`  
**Kind:** coastal-world  
**Initially known:** Yes

A coastal world where K-17 root systems stabilized poisoned soils and now hold entire wetlands together.

**Director direction:** Removing K-17 too quickly can release toxins, collapse fisheries, and erase settlements. Vela makes ecological transition slower than political promises.

**Everyday life:** Fishing crews exchange salinity readings with root tenders. Homes stand on restored reed platforms, and children learn which shorelines are safe by the color of microbial mats.

**Services:** marine labs; wetland transport; toxin monitoring; fishery cooperatives.

**Hazards:** toxin release; storm surge; wetland collapse.

### Lysa's Moon

**ID:** `lysas-moon`  
**Kind:** agricultural-moon  
**Initially known:** Yes

A small farming moon used for refugee cooperatives, clean-soil multiplication, and independent crop trials.

**Director direction:** Its limited population and weak political status make it useful as a transition laboratory and easy for larger worlds to treat as expendable.

**Everyday life:** Temporary shelters sit beside carefully measured test plots. Volunteer farmers work short shifts around school schedules, and every successful seed lot is celebrated before being sealed for export.

**Services:** clean soil; trial fields; refugee housing; seed multiplication.

**Hazards:** trial contamination; immune reactions; political neglect.

### Cinder Reach

**ID:** `cinder-reach`  
**Kind:** asteroid-belt  
**Initially known:** Yes

An industrial belt producing fertilizer, irrigation parts, storage housings, and replicator feedstock. It is also the main route for restricted seed and Dominion biotechnology.

**Director direction:** Labor, insurance, shipping, and repair decisions here can affect every harvest without any field being attacked. The belt contains crime but is not itself a criminal faction.

**Everyday life:** Shift sirens mark life more than days. Dock canteens post fertilizer prices beside injury notices, and crews argue about whether sealed seed crates are cargo, medicine, or weapons.

**Services:** fabrication; cargo brokerage; fertilizer processing; repair yards.

**Hazards:** industrial accidents; smuggling; labor conflict.

### Weather Crown Array

**ID:** `weather-crown`  
**Kind:** orbital-installation  
**Initially known:** Yes

A distributed orbital system that moderates rainfall, storm tracks, and temperature across the inner cluster.

**Director direction:** Its data can save crops, predict control-spore dispersal, or create unequal weather advantages. Maintenance access and forecast transparency are political resources.

**Everyday life:** Small crews rotate through lonely control nodes, trading recipes and sensor anomalies over an always-open maintenance channel.

**Services:** weather control; atmospheric telemetry; spore modeling.

**Hazards:** control failure; data capture; storm amplification.

### Morrow Granary

**ID:** `morrow-granary`  
**Kind:** orbital-depot  
**Initially known:** Yes

A rotating reserve cylinder where emergency grain, rations, seed lots, and fertilizer are pooled before interworld distribution.

**Director direction:** The granary is a logistical buffer and a political symbol. Small accounting errors become hunger, while secret diversions can be justified as emergency discretion.

**Everyday life:** Cargo crews sleep in hot-bunk quarters during planting season. Every storage ring has a memorial panel for convoys lost during the war.

**Services:** bulk storage; ration packing; inspection bays; convoy staging.

**Hazards:** spoilage; manifest fraud; contamination.

### The Cardassian Orchard

**ID:** `cardassian-orchard`  
**Kind:** remediation-site  
**Initially known:** Yes

A joint Federation-Cardassian remediation site where damaged soil is rebuilt through fungi, mineral exchange, and mixed orchards rather than K-17 monoculture.

**Director direction:** The site produces valuable evidence that transition can work, but its Cardassian staff face retaliation and its methods require more labor than emergency authorities prefer.

**Everyday life:** Workers eat together beneath young orchard frames while security patrols stay deliberately distant. The first local fruit is preserved rather than sold.

**Services:** soil remediation; training; mixed crop trials; Cardassian records.

**Hazards:** retaliation; sabotage; political hostility.

### Briar Vault

**ID:** `briar-vault`  
**Kind:** dominion-seed-repository  
**Initially known:** No

A sealed Dominion seed repository believed to contain parent strains, control chemistry, growth records, and substitute organisms.

**Director direction:** The vault is hazardous and politically explosive, but not a single-answer dungeon. Its contents can support treatment, transition, edited K-17, or coercive control depending on custody and interpretation.

**Everyday life:** There is no ordinary civilian life inside the sealed repository. Outside, temporary camps form whenever rumors suggest the vault may open.

**Services:** parent strains; occupation records; control chemistry.

**Hazards:** automated defenses; biohazard; custody conflict.

## Regional actors

### Commodore Elias Brand

**Role:** Starfleet Relief Command regional commander  
**Species:** Human  
**Faction:** `starfleet-relief-command`  
**Opening location:** `verdant-station`  
**Posture:** formal and risk-sensitive

Wants a controlled phaseout that prevents famine and can survive Federation review.

**Director direction:** Measured, institutional, and explicit about orders. Brand respects results but dislikes unbounded local exceptions.

**Bounded knowledge:** fact.k17-dominion-origin; fact.dorel-authorized-war-distribution.

### Commissioner Lysa Voren

**Role:** Cyradon Food Council commissioner  
**Species:** Human  
**Faction:** `cyradon-food-council`  
**Opening location:** `verdant-station`  
**Posture:** competent and protective

Wants to prevent panic, preserve distribution, and retain enough emergency authority to get through the next planting.

**Director direction:** Fast, data-literate, and politically careful. Voren speaks in reserves and deadlines before discussing legitimacy.

**Bounded knowledge:** fact.cluster-dependence; fact.morrow-reserve-gap.

### Governor Ilyan Kes

**Role:** Governor of Arda II  
**Species:** Rigelian  
**Faction:** `arda-harvest-directorate`  
**Opening location:** `arda-ii`  
**Posture:** defensive but practical

Wants Arda harvest preserved and its wartime recovery treated as a legitimate achievement.

**Director direction:** Publicly reassuring, privately blunt about export math and domestic political fragility.

**Bounded knowledge:** fact.arda-monoculture; fact.processing-spore-residue.

### Sila Marr

**Role:** Khepri agronomist and Free Seed League spokesperson  
**Species:** Human  
**Faction:** `free-seed-league`  
**Opening location:** `khepri`  
**Posture:** credible reformer

Wants open testing, farmer representation, and protection of heritage stock from requisition or patent.

**Director direction:** Direct, historically grounded, and skeptical of any solution that leaves farmers dependent on sealed expertise.

**Bounded knowledge:** fact.khepri-heritage-lines; fact.free-seed-militant-split.

### Toma Rey

**Role:** Speaker of the Khepri Reservoir Council  
**Species:** Bajoran  
**Faction:** `khepri-seed-cooperatives`  
**Opening location:** `khepri`  
**Posture:** cautious localist

Wants water and seed decisions to remain answerable to communities carrying the risk.

**Director direction:** Slow, courteous, and relentless about implementation details and consent.

**Bounded knowledge:** fact.khepri-water-limit; fact.clean-acre-location.

### Prefect Nera Voss

**Role:** Director of the Sorell Allocation Authority  
**Species:** Bolian  
**Faction:** `sorell-allocation-authority`  
**Opening location:** `sorell`  
**Posture:** controlled and defensive

Wants ration continuity and believes centralized seed control is the only realistic barrier to chaos.

**Director direction:** Polished and procedural. Voss frames coercive systems as neutral administration and will provide real evidence for their benefits.

**Bounded knowledge:** fact.sorell-ration-patronage; fact.protein-vine-dependence.

### Omala Ren

**Faction:** `vela-wetland-stewardship`  
**Opening location:** `vela-nacre`  
**Posture:** patient and ecological

Wants the right to retain selected K-17 systems until wetland replacement is proven locally.

**Director direction:** Quiet, sensory, and specific about ecological sequences rather than political slogans.

**Bounded knowledge:** fact.vela-toxin-lock; fact.root-system-mutation.

**Species:** Cardassian

**Posture:** indispensable and wary

Wants remediation work judged on evidence while refusing to become a convenient symbol of absolution.

**Bounded knowledge:** fact.cardassian-occupation-records; fact.briar-chemical-index.

### Lareen

**Faction:** `dominion-biotech-holdouts`

**Director direction:** Courteous, analytical, and faintly amused by decentralized systems. She sees centralized management as morally neutral unless challenged by concrete outcomes.

**Bounded knowledge:** fact.k17-control-family; fact.briar-access-sequence; fact.marker-purpose.
