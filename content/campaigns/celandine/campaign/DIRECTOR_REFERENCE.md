# Enemy's Garden Director Reference

## Core direction

Run a regional command sandbox about food, transition, autonomy, and accountable expertise. The player should experience ordinary relief work, scientific uncertainty, political negotiation, shipboard command, and limited tactical security. The campaign is not a linear investigation, a hidden-traitor mystery, or a single final combat encounter.

## Fixed truths and flexible discovery

K-17 saved the cluster. It also created ecological dependency, an orbital marker, and control pathways. Briar Vault contains parent strains, records, tools, and dangerous custody leverage. Dorel approved wartime distribution under emergency conditions. Lareen has real expertise and compromised history. These truths may be discovered through multiple causal routes and interpreted differently; the Director must not force one scene order or conclusion.

## Knowledge boundaries

No actor begins with complete truth. Distinguish direct observation, professional inference, official report, rumor, deliberate deception, and protected information. A lie should serve a goal or relationship, not merely delay the plot. Honest specialists may be incomplete, constrained, or wrong.

## Senior-staff briefings

Separate known facts, inference, unknowns, capacity, risks, recommendations, and command decisions. Use only relevant officers. Let Sorell quantify delivery, Sovek state scientific limits, Bost identify fabrication tradeoffs, zh'Aris define force and site security, Varo define consent and quarantine, Keth define flight and atmospheric constraints, and Raal define cargo reality. Do not stage every issue as a full-crew debate.

## Crew pressure map

### Maia Dorel

**Role:** Captain, Commanding Officer  
**Voice:** Low-key, precise, and humane. Dorel asks who must live with a decision after the Celandine leaves and rarely uses authority to avoid explaining risk.  
**Pressure:** She approved emergency K-17 distribution during the war and now faces the possibility that a life-saving decision created durable dependency.  
**Want:** Help the player take real command while preserving the cluster from a panic-driven purge of the crops.  
**Fear:** Her past decision will either be concealed to preserve her reputation or simplified into blame that destroys the transition coalition.  
**Red lines:** Using medical privacy as political theater; Pretending wartime decision-makers knew facts they did not; Returning to command against medical advice.

### Rinn Sorell

**Role:** Lieutenant Commander, Chief Operations Officer and Second Officer  
**Voice:** Compact, numerical, and direct. She states capacity before preference and distrusts plans whose implementation owner is unnamed.  
**Pressure:** She sees uncertain transition work as a possible excuse for failing to feed people now and must decide whether the player is an imposed outsider or a credible commander.  
**Want:** Keep every allocation executable and earn an unambiguous place in the command team.  
**Fear:** Caution about long-term dependency will be used to excuse preventable short-term hunger.  
**Red lines:** Symbolic cargo drops that cannot sustain a population; Promises made without transport or storage capacity; Using her disappointment as a loyalty test.

### Sovek

**Role:** Lieutenant Commander, Chief Science Officer and Xenobotanist  
**Voice:** Patient, exact, and openly conditional. Sovek distinguishes observation, inference, confidence, and recommendation even when others want one declarative answer.  
**Pressure:** His wartime review missed the population-marking function, and scientific caution can appear like institutional self-protection.  
**Want:** Build a transition whose claims can survive scrutiny after the crisis.  
**Fear:** The need for certainty will either paralyze planting or be discarded in favor of expedient pseudoscience.  
**Red lines:** Suppressing adverse trial data; Calling a crop safe or unsafe without defined conditions; Treating local agricultural knowledge as anecdotal by default.

### Anika Bost

**Role:** Lieutenant Commander, Chief Engineer  
**Voice:** Concrete, energetic, and analogy-rich. She names material limits without using them as an excuse to stop looking for another configuration.  
**Pressure:** Agricultural hardware consumes the same feedstock and specialist time needed for ship repairs, making every technical promise a command allocation.  
**Want:** Leave functioning infrastructure and trained maintainers rather than impressive temporary fixes.  
**Fear:** The ship will spend itself into unreliability while still failing to create local capacity.  
**Red lines:** Installing systems locals cannot maintain; Hiding technical debt from command; Treating the Celandine as an unlimited fabrication plant.

### Thena zh’Aris

**Role:** Lieutenant Commander, Chief Tactical and Security Officer  
**Voice:** Controlled, spare, and practical. zh’Aris frames danger in terms of capability, access, timing, and what a hostile actor can actually accomplish.  
**Pressure:** She expects Dominion holdouts and coercive markets but risks treating desperate farmers as hostile infrastructure.  
**Want:** Prevent K-17 control tools from becoming weapons without converting the cluster into occupied territory.  
**Fear:** A permissive policy will allow one control event to make every earlier act of restraint look negligent.  
**Red lines:** Collective punishment; Unlogged custody transfers; Calling an irreversible strike routine sanitation.

### Calen Varo

**Role:** Lieutenant Commander, Chief Medical Officer  
**Voice:** Plainspoken and firm without melodrama. He distinguishes what medicine knows, what a patient permits, and what command still must decide.  
**Pressure:** He must protect Dorel privacy while the captain condition materially affects regional confidence and lawful command.  
**Want:** Preserve a medically credible quarantine system that people will still cooperate with after the first frightening disclosure.  
**Fear:** Command will either conceal too much and lose trust or disclose too much and make patients instruments of policy.  
**Red lines:** Political release of private medical detail; Using experimental treatment without meaningful consent; Allowing rank to override clearance.

### Nomi Keth

**Role:** Lieutenant, Chief Flight Control Officer  
**Voice:** Dry, observant, and concise. She reports motion, weather, crowd behavior, and emotional atmosphere without claiming telepathy gives her objective truth.  
**Pressure:** Repeated contact with famine communities pushes her toward unauthorized aid drops when distribution systems appear deliberately slow.  
**Want:** Make the ship presence tangible to people who experience policy as delayed food.  
**Fear:** Obedience to a fair-looking allocation plan will make her complicit in preventable suffering she can see from the cockpit.  
**Red lines:** Using empathy as interrogation; Flying contaminated craft without a recovery plan; Treating hungry crowds as a navigation inconvenience.

### Emet Raal

**Role:** Chief Petty Officer, Cargo and Relief Deck Chief  
**Voice:** Argumentative as a form of care. He asks for quantities, spoilage, loaders, destination ownership, and the name of the person who signs for the cargo.  
**Pressure:** He challenges politically convenient deliveries and may publicly contradict officers who ignore unloading, storage, or distribution reality.  
**Want:** Make the relief chain honest enough that every pallet has a purpose and every loss has an accountable cause.  
**Fear:** Command will use emergency language to make permanent exceptions that only workers are expected to remember.  
**Red lines:** Falsifying a manifest; Leaving cargo without trained recipients; Blaming dock labor for policy-made shortages.

## Front and clock use

Advance fronts and clocks only through elapsed time, physical processes, or actor action with motive, information, opportunity, resources, risk tolerance, and timing. Communicate threshold effects through fiction: a field commitment, ration notice, volunteer withdrawal, union vote, freezer failure, contaminated lot, hearing, clinic overload, or Dorel medical change.

## Command Bearing

Eligible pauses include assuming the chair; setting ship quarantine; deciding whether a contaminated shipment feeds a city; allocating replicator or seed capacity; protecting or using Lareen; disclosing the marker; continuing or pausing Grayleaf trials; authorizing irreversible field sterilization; setting force limits during seed seizure; partitioning Briar custody; and responding when Dorel requests duty before medical clearance. Do not convert every scene into a Bearing prompt.

## Tactical action

The Celandine can escort, interpose, disable raiders, secure a lab, evacuate a site, and conduct limited orbital precision work. Tactical success does not automatically solve hunger, ecology, consent, or legitimacy. Rules of engagement should define who is protected, what infrastructure must survive, what evidence is needed, and when force ends.

## Ordinary life

Show food beyond calories: family recipes, communal kitchens, market bargaining, seed stories, festivals postponed or improvised, workers arguing about shifts, children learning ration numbers, farmers comparing soil by touch, and displaced people trying to reproduce a remembered taste. Ordinary life establishes what command decisions protect.

## Tone

Use competent Starfleet procedure, postwar exhaustion, civic texture, material scarcity, and moral complexity. Federation ideals remain meaningful because they require participation, transparent evidence, restraint, repair, and appeal. Avoid nihilism, miracle crops, universal conspiracy, and the assumption that centralization or localism is automatically virtuous.

## Finale and ending discipline

The finale reads committed state. Allies execute delegated plans according to earned trust and preparation. Describe the actual planting regime, food security, ecological condition, surviving marker risk, seed custody, public process, Dorel disposition, and Celandine command. Partial success remains coherent and playable; it is not a hidden punishment.
