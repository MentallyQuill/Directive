# Enemy's Garden

## Campaign promise

Several worlds in the Cyradon Relief Cluster survived the final years of the Dominion War by adopting K-17, a family of Dominion-engineered crops that grows in damaged soil, tolerates unstable weather, and produces exceptional yields. The harvest prevented famine. It also displaced local seed lines, altered microbial ecologies, and embedded biological markers that make exposed fields and populations identifiable from orbit.

The U.S.S. Celandine arrives to audit the crop system, deliver agricultural support, and prepare the cluster for independent recovery. During the opening response at Arda II, Captain Maia Dorel is exposed to dormant control spores and placed in medical quarantine. The newly assigned Executive Officer assumes the conn, becomes Acting Captain, and inherits responsibility for food distribution, bioscience, quarantine, diplomacy, transport, security, and the legitimacy of a transition no world can survive alone.

The broad K-17 danger is not a late twist. It becomes apparent early. The campaign engine is transition: planting windows, finite clean stock, soil recovery, public disclosure, seed custody, labor, transport, scientific trials, sabotage, and the competing interests of worlds that depend on different K-17 strains. Every journey and command decision changes what can be planted next.

## Player role and authority

The player is a Starfleet Commander newly assigned as Executive Officer. Captain Dorel requested or accepted the appointment because the mission requires an officer able to integrate ship command with relief, science, engineering, medicine, security, logistics, and diplomacy. Character creation supports multiple professional backgrounds; it does not require a scientist.

Dorel's quarantine makes the player Acting Captain under standing orders and Starfleet confirmation. The player holds operational authority over the ship and principal mission authority throughout the Cyradon deployment. Dorel remains legal captain until lawfully relieved, transferred, retired, or returned to duty. Sickbay is not a remote bridge: Varo controls medical clearance, Dorel may advise only within established access and privacy rules, and any return to limited duty preserves the player's established regional command agency.

The Director never writes the player's dialogue, thoughts, emotions, motives, intent, or decisions. Senior officers supply routine competence, options, risks, and recommendations without deciding the command question.

## Ship baseline and mission fit

The Norway-class Celandine is a compact diplomatic and regional-response vessel refitted with an agricultural relief module. It carries xenobotany and soil laboratories, seed freezers, expanded cargo transporters, hydroponic capacity, two agricultural shuttles, a conference suite, and enough tactical power for convoy escort and relief-site defense. It cannot feed the entire cluster through replicators, replace planetary agriculture, quarantine every exposed population, or impose a solution by force.

Three limitations recur throughout play:

1. Clean seed stock is finite and must be multiplied over several growing cycles.
2. Standard transporter biofilters do not reliably detect dormant K-17 control spores.
3. Sterilization that destroys K-17 also damages beneficial soil organisms and can remove acreage from production for a season.

These limits keep farmers, local scientists, transport unions, Cardassian reconstruction specialists, clinics, and elected or emergency authorities central to success.

## Starting directives

1. Protect civilian life and prevent mass famine in the Cyradon Relief Cluster.
2. Audit K-17 crop, soil, water, processing, and biomarker risks without destroying essential food systems by default.
3. Support credible crop alternatives, ecological recovery, and local technical capacity.
4. Protect lawful medical privacy, informed consent, seed custody, and public access to consequential evidence.
5. Secure Dominion control material and parent strains under accountable custody.
6. Avoid assuming permanent civil or agricultural authority where legitimate local institutions can function.
7. Maintain lawful Celandine command continuity while Captain Dorel is medically unavailable.

## Four-arc structure

### Arc I: The Harvest That Won the War

The opening succession, Arda exposure, four world-level dependency cases, and early capacity work establish why K-17 cannot simply be destroyed. The player builds an initial doctrine for quarantine, food continuity, and clean-stock allocation while learning that each world depends on a different crop, infrastructure, and political bargain.

### Arc II: Fields of Identification

Medical and environmental evidence confirm that K-17 exposure links people, fields, water, and livestock to an orbital signature. Public disclosure, consent, data custody, Briar Vault access, and parent-strain research become political and operational questions. The vault supplies tools and dangerous knowledge, not one mandatory answer.

### Arc III: Seed Sovereignty

Grayleaf trials, heritage lines, processing hardware, local registries, controlled K-17 reserves, and seed law determine whether the cluster can maintain alternatives without becoming dependent on the Celandine or a single regional authority. Sabotage, false clean stock, and emergency seizure test whether enforcement protects transition or replaces it with occupation.

### Arc IV: Enemy's Garden

The assembled planting crisis reads actual state: food reserves, crop varieties, transition capacity, ecological condition, public trust, transport security, Briar custody, faction posture, Celandine resources, Dorel's health, and delegated side work. The player cannot stabilize every field personally. Earned institutions and allies act according to preparation, authority, trust, and the orders they receive.

## Persistent campaign model

The campaign tracks famine pressure, biomarker spread, alternative readiness, seed sovereignty, sabotage and retaliation, public trust, ecological health, crew command confidence, Starfleet scrutiny, and captain condition. Raw values remain hidden. They appear through ration changes, migration, clinic load, market prices, field behavior, hearing access, protest, convoy reliability, crew candor, Dorel's medical limits, and the willingness of local actors to execute delegated plans.

Eight fronts operate independently: harvest deadlines, biological integration, transition capacity, emergency power, sabotage and retaliation, ecological lock-in, Briar custody, and command succession. They advance only through elapsed time, physical processes, or actors with motive, knowledge, opportunity, resources, risk tolerance, and timing.

## Main questline

The package contains 26 quest templates: fourteen core quests, nine designed side assignments, two standing operational frames, and one emergent bad-batch template. The first three authored missions use tactical graphs. Later quests use the open-world contract and inherit current world, crew, ship, faction, and campaign state.

Quest availability is state-based. The four initial world cases can be pursued in different orders. The marker mission opens after sufficient regional evidence rather than a single compulsory clue. Disclosure, Briar access, parent-strain work, Grayleaf transition, seed governance, and control-bloom response unlock through committed facts and outcomes. The finale assembles current state instead of selecting a fixed branch.

## Failure and recovery

No single failed check ends a mission. Failure produces cost: lost time, crop damage, medical risk, witness refusal, reduced evidence quality, contaminated stock, damaged trust, ration pressure, crew strain, or a harder decision. A ship loss, death, command removal, severe famine, ecological collapse, or captured seed authority may create a terminal candidate, but the player receives checkpoint and continuation options defined by the end-condition contract.
