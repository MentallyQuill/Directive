# Enemy's Garden: Endings and Epilogue

## Principle

Strong success is a credible transition that people can maintain. It is not the instantaneous removal of every Dominion organism. Campaign resolution evaluates independent axes and records the actual institutions, stocks, ecology, rights, and command relationships produced during play.

## Ending axes

### Food Security

Whether the cluster avoids mass famine and possesses credible stores, production, processing, transport, and future harvest capacity.

### Biological Autonomy

Whether marker, control-spore, and control-chemistry risk is reduced, contained, monitored, or left exploitable.

### Ecological Health

Whether soil, water, wetlands, pollinators, fisheries, and mixed-crop systems can survive the transition.

### Seed Sovereignty

Whether seed, parent stock, testing, reserves, and planting authority are legitimate, plural, reviewable, and maintainable.

### Justice and Public Trust

Whether disclosure, medical rights, wartime decisions, sabotage, specialist accountability, and reparations receive an honest public process.

### Celandine Command Continuity

Whether Dorel, the player, Sorell, Varo, and the crew emerge with lawful roles and a command culture capable of continuing the work.

## Result bands

### A Free Harvest

**Band:** `Great Success`

No world suffers mass famine, control risk is contained, multiple alternatives are viable, seed authority is legitimate, and dangerous knowledge remains under accountable custody.

### Two Harvests More

**Band:** `Success`

The region retains monitored K-17 use under credible exit milestones while alternatives, reserves, and institutions mature.

### A Thousand Private Gardens

**Band:** `Partial Success`

Local seed sovereignty and innovation survive, but common testing, contamination control, and regional enforcement remain uneven.

### The Managed Garden

**Band:** `Partial Failure`

Food security is preserved through centralized seed and data authority that communities cannot meaningfully challenge.

### Clean Fields, Empty Stores

**Band:** `Failure`

Control risk falls sharply, but premature eradication causes severe shortage, displacement, and preventable death.

### Enemy's Garden

**Band:** `Great Failure`

Control failure, blight, violence, or transport collapse destroys several harvests and leaves only evacuation, ration enforcement, and later recovery.

## Authored end conditions

### What We Plant Next Resolved

**Family:** `authoredCompletion`  
**Severity:** `completion`  
**Default band:** Success

The epilogue resolves the next harvest, public record, seed settlement, and Celandine command outcome.

### Player Character Death

**Family:** `playerDeath`  
**Severity:** `terminalCandidate`  
**Default band:** Great Failure

The player dies before the transition is settled; replay, keep the branch, or continue only through a plausible altered role.

Continuation frames: `medical-survival-and-command-gap`, `retired-but-testifying`.

### Permanent Command Removal

**Family:** `commandRemoval`  
**Severity:** `terminalCandidate`  
**Default band:** Partial Failure

The player loses durable ship command before the transition and can continue only through inquiry, advice, or another lawful frame.

Continuation frames: `court-martial-and-inquiry`, `relieved-but-advising`.

### Celandine Destroyed and Relief Objective Failed

**Family:** `shipOrBaseLoss`  
**Severity:** `terminalCandidate`  
**Default band:** Great Failure

The Celandine is destroyed before food, seed, evacuation, or transition capacity survives through regional partners.

Continuation frames: `survivors-after-celandine-loss`, `aftermath-rescue-and-food-accounting`.

### Celandine Destroyed but Relief Objective Preserved

**Family:** `shipOrBaseLoss`  
**Severity:** `terminalCandidate`  
**Default band:** Partial Success

The ship is lost, but food, seed, records, and transition work survive through earned institutions and assets.

Continuation frames: `survivors-after-celandine-loss`, `allied-relief-command`.

### Mass Famine

**Family:** `objectiveCollapse`  
**Severity:** `terminalCandidate`  
**Default band:** Great Failure

Regional food systems can no longer prevent mass mortality and displacement; remaining play centers rescue, rationing, and accountability.

Continuation frames: `aftermath-rescue-and-food-accounting`, `allied-relief-command`.

### Control Bloom Catastrophe

**Family:** `objectiveCollapse`  
**Severity:** `terminalCandidate`  
**Default band:** Great Failure

A regional K-17 control event compromises multiple crops and populations faster than normal transition work can contain.

Continuation frames: `aftermath-rescue-and-food-accounting`, `allied-relief-command`.

### Ecological Collapse

**Family:** `objectiveCollapse`  
**Severity:** `terminalCandidate`  
**Default band:** Failure

Soil, water, wetland, pollinator, or processing ecology fails at regional scale, replacing transition with long recovery and relocation.

Continuation frames: `aftermath-rescue-and-food-accounting`, `allied-relief-command`.

### Transition Capacity Collapse

**Family:** `objectiveCollapse`  
**Severity:** `terminalCandidate`  
**Default band:** Failure

Clean seed, trials, processing, labor, and stores cannot support an exit from K-17 while hunger intensifies.

Continuation frames: `aftermath-rescue-and-food-accounting`, `allied-relief-command`.

### Public Legitimacy Collapse

**Family:** `objectiveCollapse`  
**Severity:** `terminalCandidate`  
**Default band:** Partial Failure

Technically viable plans lose enough legitimacy that compliance, security, and distribution fragment across the cluster.

Continuation frames: `court-martial-and-inquiry`, `allied-relief-command`, `aftermath-rescue-and-food-accounting`.

### Seed Authority Captured

**Family:** `objectiveCollapse`  
**Severity:** `terminalCandidate`  
**Default band:** Partial Failure

Seed, test, and parent-stock authority becomes unchallengeable even if immediate food security survives.

Continuation frames: `court-martial-and-inquiry`, `retired-but-testifying`.

### Player Resignation or Transfer

**Family:** `playerChoice`  
**Severity:** `terminalCandidate`  
**Default band:** Partial Success

The player leaves the command role and can continue only through testimony, advice, or a narrowed position.

Continuation frames: `retired-but-testifying`, `relieved-but-advising`.

### Player Chooses to Conclude

**Family:** `playerChoice`  
**Severity:** `terminal`  
**Default band:** Success

The player intentionally concludes the campaign at the current transition point.

## Continuation frames

### Court-Martial and Inquiry

Play continues through investigation, testimony, evidence custody, relief accounting, and constrained regional duties.

### Relieved but Advising

The player is formally relieved while the Celandine or a successor command continues to request regional expertise.

### Survivors After Celandine Loss

Surviving crew and regional partners continue evacuation, seed preservation, medical response, and food coordination after the ship is lost.

### Allied Relief Command

The player continues through Verdant, local governments, cooperatives, or transport unions after Starfleet command capacity is reduced.

### Aftermath Rescue and Food Accounting

A severe harvest failure becomes an aftermath campaign of evacuation, rationing, seed salvage, missing-person work, memorials, and public accounting.

### Medical Survival and Command Gap

The player survives a terminal injury but cannot immediately resume command, leaving succession and recovery active.

### Retired but Testifying

The player leaves active Starfleet service but continues through testimony, advocacy, research review, or local public work.

## Epilogue procedure

`What We Plant Next` records each world planting regime, current stores, transition milestones, unresolved K-17 use, Briar custody, public reporting, reparations, food law, ecological obligations, and the officers or civilian institutions responsible for maintenance. The epilogue may include hearings, field visits, memorials, seed exchanges, a meal, reassignment, confirmation, censure, retirement, or continuing relief operations. It must not erase cost.

## Command outcomes

Dorel may resume full command, accept a limited or shore role, retire medically, face review for wartime decisions, or remain a protected witness and advisor. The player may remain XO with expanded mission authority, be confirmed as captain, receive censure while retaining local legitimacy, accept a relief or diplomatic posting, or request reassignment. These outcomes follow medical reality, lawful conduct, crew confidence, candor, Starfleet review, and operational need.
