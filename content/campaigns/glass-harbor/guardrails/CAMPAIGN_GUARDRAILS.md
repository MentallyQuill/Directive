# Campaign Guardrails

## Player agency

- Never write the player character’s thoughts, feelings, speech, or decisions.
- Supply routine Starfleet competence, established facts, procedural options, and consequences; stop before judgment.
- The player may refuse, postpone, delegate, withdraw, surrender, negotiate, or pursue an unanticipated approach.
- Prewritten approaches are examples, not a menu of allowed solutions.

## Command authority

- Captain Rhos retains final legal command while physically present in the opening.
- After Rhos disappears, lawful succession gives the player operational command as Acting Captain.
- Senior officers preserve domain authority and may document objections, recommend alternatives, or invoke lawful safety limits.
- Crew disagreement is not a vote. The player decides after receiving relevant advice.
- Rhos’s return creates a command problem to resolve through play; it does not automatically revoke player agency.

## Hidden truth

- Raw campaign track values
- Secret faction clocks
- Exact Shepherd node count before discovery
- Captain Rhos’s survival and location
- Jorad Pel’s route leaks
- The Crown’s partial Shepherd control key
- Predetermined valid solutions or Command Decisions
- NPC loyalty or relationship numbers

## Failure forward

- No single failed check ends a mission.
- Campaign-critical survival preserves meaningful continuation after major loss, refusal, delay, or withdrawal.
- A failed scan creates uncertainty, false confidence, delay, equipment strain, exposure, or a harder evidence route; it does not delete the only clue.
- Senior-crew death requires an established lethal situation, meaningful player choice or accumulated consequence, fair opportunity for command response, and narrative weight.
- Refusing an assignment is a consequential command act, not an out-of-bounds input.
- Delay advances clocks only when actors or physical processes have a reason to continue.
- The Glass Harbor may be heavily damaged, forced to withdraw, or lose regional leverage without invalidating the campaign.

## Open-world causality

- Every offscreen action identifies an actor, goal, knowledge basis, capability, and causal opportunity.
- Every physical escalation identifies the process advancing it.
- Travel and rest advance time; they do not advance every front automatically.
- Quests inherit current location, faction, route, ship, crew, and knowledge state.
- The finale reads actual campaign state and cannot reset ignored or failed obligations.

## Tone

- Competent Starfleet professionals operating under uncertainty.
- Moral and political complexity without nihilism.
- Rescue, repair, ordinary gratitude, humor, and domestic continuity remain present.
- Violence is possible but rarely sufficient to solve chart custody, legitimacy, or infrastructure problems.
- The Reef is dangerous because it is physical, inhabited, and politically contested—not because every local actor is deceptive.
