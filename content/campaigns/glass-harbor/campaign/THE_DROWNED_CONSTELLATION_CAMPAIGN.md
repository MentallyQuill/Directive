# Drowned Constellation Campaign

## Status

This is the implementation-facing campaign baseline for the Directive campaign **Drowned Constellation**. It contains full campaign spoilers and is intended for authors, implementers, Mission Director logic, package-schema work, and playtest preparation.

## Identity baseline

- Campaign title: `Drowned Constellation`
- Campaign package: U.S.S. Glass Harbor
- Ship class: Steamrunner-class
- Opening stardate: `50192.6`
- Opening year: 2373
- Primary theater: Nerine Reef
- Player role: newly promoted Commander/XO and, after the prelude, Acting Captain
- Runtime architecture: `directive.openWorldCampaign.v2`
- Expected campaign length: 40–60 sessions

## Campaign promise

The player is the newly promoted Executive Officer of the U.S.S. Glass Harbor, a Steamrunner-class survey and route-security vessel entering the Nerine Reef. When Captain Amina Rhos and her shuttle disappear during a gravitic inversion, the player assumes acting command and must conduct rescue, survey, escort, diplomacy, salvage safety, and regional crisis response in a place where every reliable chart can save lives, expose a sanctuary, create a border, or become a weapon.

## Campaign thesis

**A map can save lives, expose sanctuaries, create borders, and become a weapon.**

The campaign is not about discovering one objectively correct chart and imposing it. It is about deciding who may create, verify, hold, revise, share, revoke, and act upon navigational knowledge when communities depend on both mobility and concealment.

## Opening baseline

The Glass Harbor arrives at Lagrange Gate on a formal ninety-day assignment. The player has only recently been promoted and assigned as XO. Captain Rhos chose the player for complementary judgment, but the crew has not yet learned how the player will command independently.

During the first sounding operation, an unexpected inversion folds the route behind Rhos’s shuttle. The Glass Harbor must protect civilian traffic and preserve itself while attempting rescue. Starfleet confirms the player as Acting Captain for the emergency. Rhos remains the legal captain while missing, which creates a continuing succession question without reducing the player to a temporary observer.

## Starting directives

1. Protect civilian life and freedom of navigation.
2. Survey gravitic conditions and maintain a credible route picture.
3. Avoid unnecessary exposure of concealed civilian settlements.
4. Cooperate with lawful local authorities without surrendering Starfleet command responsibility.
5. Prevent unauthorized military control of navigation infrastructure.
6. Locate Captain Rhos and the missing shuttle when operationally feasible.
7. Investigate the causes of artificial route behavior and preserve evidence.

These directives are compatible in principle and contradictory in practice.

## Fixed Director-only truths

- The Reef contains thirteen ancient Shepherd nodes: seven functional, three damaged, two inert, and one overloaded by modern salvage activity.
- The network is adaptive infrastructure, not a sentient adversary.
- Captain Rhos, Ensign Eshren Kor, and civilian pilot Mavek Sorn survive the opening inversion and reach Maintenance Habitat Kappa-4.
- Rhos discovers that complete publication of the Reef would expose Aster Basin and several smaller sanctuaries.
- Subprefect Jorad Pel leaked selected route data to Crown contacts and shaped inspection failures to justify Kheled intervention. Prefect Tal Oren did not initially know.
- Veyra Ninth possesses a partial Shepherd control key acquired through Breakwater intermediaries.
- The final Deep Tide is a network-wide stress redistribution accelerated by the overloaded salvage node and modern high-energy traffic. It is not a single villain’s switch.
- No character or faction begins with all of these truths.

## Campaign structure

The campaign contains one prelude, eight main quests, one epilogue, ten designed side assignments, six orienting story arcs, fourteen thread templates, persistent regional fronts, and a state-assembled finale. Several main quests become available in parallel. The campaign does not require a chapter cursor or a fixed scene order.

| Quest | Function | Baseline availability |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Soundings | What kind of acting captain takes command when loyalty, rescue, and incomplete information collide? | active |
| Aster Basin | Can Starfleet help a concealed community without turning recognition into surveillance? | latent |
| The Caligo Sounding | Who owns evidence and dangerous salvage when rescue, history, and security cannot be separated? | latent |
| Terms of Passage | What makes a navigation regime legitimate when every safety rule also grants political access? | latent |
| The Chartmakers | Who may convert knowledge of survival into governing power? | latent |

## Core ending families

### Open Sea

The network remains stable enough for broad movement, rescue infrastructure is durable, Aster Basin retains meaningful autonomy, and no single armed actor controls passage. This is the strongest combined success, but it may still include public accountability, damaged relationships, and a difficult command review.

### Charted and Conquered

Routes are reliable and commerce improves, but the chart regime exposes hidden communities or permits Kheled, Starfleet, or another authority to dominate access. Operational success coexists with a sovereignty failure.

### Sanctuary of Shadows

The player preserves concealed communities and prevents occupation but rejects or fails to complete a shared navigation system. The Reef remains dangerous, locally navigated, and vulnerable to future accidents. This is a viable partial success rather than a disguised failure.

### A Crown in the Deep

The Crown consolidates enough routes, weapons, salvage capacity, and local service relationships to become the de facto coercive power. The player may still save the ship and many civilians, but the region enters an unstable order.

### Drowned Constellation

Multiple Shepherd nodes fail, Aster Basin is lost or evacuated at catastrophic cost, and the Reef becomes largely impassable. The campaign still ends with an accounting of who escaped, which evidence survived, and whether the Glass Harbor returns as a damaged rescue ship. It is not treated as a noncanonical game-over.

## Independent command outcome

Separately from the regional ending, the player may be confirmed as captain, return to the XO billet under Rhos, remain acting captain during her recovery, request transfer, or face formal inquiry. This result derives from crew confidence, legality, candor, command conduct, and the player’s own decisions rather than a hidden morality score.

## Mission direction rules

- Situation on rails, solution off rails.
- Every dynamic event must have a causal parent in committed state, actor goals, physical processes, or elapsed time.
- The Nerine Reef remains a persistent region; locations, routes, factions, and requests continue outside the foreground quest.
- Calm side assignments and ordinary crew scenes may remain unrelated to the Shepherd network.
- Crew disagreement should be focused; not every senior officer gives a moral speech before action.
- No character begins with the complete campaign truth.
- Move information only when causally justified if the player bypasses an expected scene.
- Escalation pacing serves the situation rather than forcing combat or countdowns.
- Factions act between player scenes based on goals, knowledge, capabilities, and current access.
- Maps and sensor information are operational objects with custody, revision history, access rules, and consequences.

## Failure and recovery

- No single failed check ends a mission.
- Campaign-critical survival preserves meaningful continuation after major loss, refusal, delay, or withdrawal.
- A failed scan creates uncertainty, false confidence, delay, equipment strain, exposure, or a harder evidence route; it does not delete the only clue.
- Senior-crew death requires an established lethal situation, meaningful player choice or accumulated consequence, fair opportunity for command response, and narrative weight.
- Refusing an assignment is a consequential command act, not an out-of-bounds input.
- Delay advances clocks only when actors or physical processes have a reason to continue.
- The Glass Harbor may be heavily damaged, forced to withdraw, or lose regional leverage without invalidating the campaign.
