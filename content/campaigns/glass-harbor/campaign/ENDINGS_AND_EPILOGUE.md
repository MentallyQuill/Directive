# Endings and Epilogue Assembly

## Ending method

The campaign ending is assembled from committed campaign state. It is not selected from a hidden morality score and should not be reduced to one binary success flag.

## Ending axes

| Axis | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Mobility and Lives | How many people, routes, habitats, and rescue systems survive the Deep Tide. |
| Sanctuary and Sovereignty | Whether hidden communities retain meaningful autonomy or become exposed dependencies. |
| Regional Governance | Which institutions can lawfully govern routes, security, and emergency coordination. |
| Truth and Accountability | What becomes known about the Sunken Fleet, Kheled route leaks, Crown conduct, salvage hazards, and Starfleet decisions. |
| Ship and Crew Continuity | The condition of the Glass Harbor and the senior crew after sustained open-world operations. |
| Command Succession | Whether the player is confirmed as captain, returns to the XO role, transfers, faces inquiry, or retains acting command. |

## Operational bands

### Strong operational success

Most inhabited locations survive, several independent routes remain usable, rescue capacity is not centralized in one faction, and the Shepherd network is stable enough for controlled operations. The Glass Harbor may still be damaged, and political outcomes may remain compromised.

### Partial operational success

The inversion is contained locally or through sacrifice. Important habitats survive, but the Reef loses routes, infrastructure, a node, or access to entire districts. This band supports both Sanctuary of Shadows and Charted and Conquered outcomes.

### Severe operational failure

Multiple nodes fail, routes collapse, Aster Basin is evacuated or destroyed at catastrophic cost, and the ship returns damaged or stranded. The epilogue still records survivors, evidence, command conduct, and future obligations.

## Political and sovereignty bands

- Shared charter: route authority is distributed among recognized civilian, Starfleet, Kheled, and technical custodians with real veto and review mechanisms.
- Sanctuary compact: concealed communities remain autonomous and accept limited mutual-aid obligations without publishing full coordinates.
- Administrative control: Starfleet or Kheled authority stabilizes passage but marginalizes local governance.
- Fragmentation: no durable regional agreement exists; local pilots and bilateral bargains remain essential.
- Coercive consolidation: Crown or another armed actor controls enough services and route access to dominate the region.

## Truth and accountability bands

- Public chain: evidence concerning Pel, Crown acquisition, salvage overload, and command decisions survives in a reviewable record.
- Protected disclosure: enough truth is disclosed for accountability while sanctuary-specific information remains sealed.
- Strategic concealment: critical facts are withheld to preserve order, creating future leverage and institutional risk.
- Lost record: evidence is destroyed, discredited, or traded away; surviving actors write incompatible histories.

## Command succession outcomes

### Confirmed Captain

The player is offered or confirmed in permanent command because the crew, Starfleet, and the final record support their judgment and candor. Rhos may endorse, challenge, or be unavailable for this outcome.

### Return to XO under Rhos

Rhos recovers and resumes command through a negotiated transition. The player remains principal mission commander for the conclusion and may define a changed command relationship rather than simply surrendering the chair.

### Continuing Acting Captain

Rhos survives but cannot immediately return, or Starfleet delays permanent disposition. The player retains command with an explicit review date and unresolved succession pressure.

### Transfer or New Command

The player requests transfer, accepts another command, or concludes that the ship requires a different arrangement. This may be a success rather than punishment.

### Inquiry

A formal review examines unlawful action, concealed risk, preventable loss, abuse of chart authority, or conflict with Starfleet orders. Inquiry does not imply automatic disgrace and may expose institutional contradictions.

## Epilogue scene families

- Public memorial and survivor accounting.
- Chart custody hearing and map-charter ratification or collapse.
- Rhos reunion, medical recovery, funeral, or unresolved absence.
- Crew succession conversation in the ready room.
- Quiet return to a location transformed by earlier side work.
- Command review with preserved evidence, incomplete evidence, or competing records.
- Final route sounding showing what the region can now safely reach—and what remains deliberately absent from the map.
