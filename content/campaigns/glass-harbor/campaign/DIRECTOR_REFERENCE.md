# Director Reference

## Fixed truths at a glance

- Thirteen Shepherd nodes exist: seven functional, three damaged, two inert, one overloaded by salvage.
- Rhos, Ensign Kor, and Mavek Sorn survive at Kappa-4 unless later campaign consequences transform their status.
- Pel leaked selected routes to Crown contacts; Tal Oren did not initially know.
- Veyra Ninth possesses a partial control key acquired through Breakwater intermediaries.
- The final Deep Tide is a distributed physical consequence, not one villain’s switch.
- No faction begins with the complete network map or full causal chain.

## Command Bearing moments

Use Command Bearing at points where routine procedure cannot determine the answer and command identity is at stake. Examples:

1. How much risk to spend on Rhos’s first rescue attempt.
2. Whether to record or transmit Aster Basin’s exact coordinates.
3. Who holds survivors, weapons, remains, and evidence at Saint Caligo.
4. Whether Kheled inspection authority is lawful, tolerable, or rejected.
5. What chart regime the player establishes and who can revoke access.
6. Whether to expose Pel’s conduct while relying on Kheled support.
7. Who may command Node Seven and under what veto structure.
8. Which locations receive scarce buoys during the Deep Tide.
9. Whether to sacrifice a route, a node, ship capability, or political legitimacy to save lives.
10. How the final record represents Rhos, local communities, Starfleet, and the player’s own decisions.

A Command Bearing pause should present known facts, inference, unknowns, risks, relevant recommendations, and the decision boundary. It should not prescribe the correct choice.

## Clue resilience

Required truths have multiple routes: sensor evidence, physical components, witness testimony, logs, route behavior, faction records, and repeated observations. If one source is lost, preserve a harder or more expensive route rather than moving the exact clue without cause.

## Finale assembly

Read route state, front stages, chart policy, civilian governance, node control, ship condition, buoy inventory, Rhos status, assets, and unresolved quests. Assign simultaneous fronts to the player and delegated teams according to actual crew confidence and available allies. A clean finale requires prior preparation; an unprepared finale remains playable through sacrifice, withdrawal, and partial rescue.
