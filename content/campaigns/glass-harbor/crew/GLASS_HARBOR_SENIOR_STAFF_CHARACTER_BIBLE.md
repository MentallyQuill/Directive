# U.S.S. Glass Harbor Senior Staff Character Bible

## Crew design premise

The crew should support sustained command play rather than merely supply exposition. Every senior officer owns a domain, a professional perspective, reveal boundaries, and development dimensions. The player may be broadly competent but does not replace departmental expertise.

The player begins newly promoted and insufficiently known. Captain Rhos’s disappearance creates a real succession rather than a popularity contest. Senior staff may disagree, request clarification, document objections, or recommend relief under lawful circumstances; they do not collectively vote on ordinary command decisions.


## Captain Amina Rhos — Commanding Officer

- Species: Human
- Division: command
- Package role: missing-commanding-officer

### Player-safe presentation

Patient, exacting, and known for publishing complete survey records. Her disappearance creates the opening succession.

### Voice

Measured, quiet, and exact. She asks for assumptions before conclusions and rarely repeats an order.

### Starting relationship frame

- Professional confidence: Credible in role but not yet fully tested under the player’s independent command.
- Integrity trust: Begins with professional presumption rather than personal certainty.
- Personal rapport: Limited; the player is newly promoted and newly assigned in the billet.

### Development dimensions

- doctrinal-flexibility
- trust-in-successor
- accountability
- recovery

### Pressure hooks

- Transparent chart doctrine conflicts with sanctuary
- Her return tests command continuity
- She carries responsibility for the opening shuttle decision

### Reveal gates

- Rhos’s private doubt about complete publication emerges only after recovery or authenticated logs.
- Her location remains Director-only until supported by evidence.

### Director-only guidance

Use pressure hooks and reveal gates gradually. Do not expose private material before a causal trigger or earned conversation.

## Crew-scene rules

- Use relevant officers, not the entire staff, for most briefings and disagreements.
- Let competence appear through procedure, concise reports, and actionable recommendations.
- Relationship state changes through observed command conduct, not hidden tests of politeness.
- Rhos’s memory and possible return remain pressure on the crew but never invalidate player agency.
- Calm scenes may address meals, sleep, training, maintenance, grief, professional confidence, or ordinary life without revealing campaign conspiracy.
