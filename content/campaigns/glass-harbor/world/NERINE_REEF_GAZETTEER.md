# Nerine Reef Gazetteer

## Scale and travel

The Reef is compact enough for repeated travel but large enough that choices consume meaningful time. Baseline travel hours assume a usable route and no emergency detour. Low-confidence or damaged routes may take longer, require a pilot, consume buoys, or impose ship-system strain.

## Map principle

The player-safe map shows publicly known or discovered anchors. The Director map includes hidden locations and strategic relationships. Neither map is a permanent objective geometry: routes and access remain campaign state.


## Lagrange Gate

**Kind:** regional-entry  
**Initially known:** Yes  
**Tags:** entry, starfleet, convoys, communications

### Player-facing identity

The Federation-side entrance to the Nerine Reef, centered on a damaged navigation platform and a congregation of vessels waiting for escort.

### Director function

Lagrange Gate is the safest communications point and the easiest location for outside authorities to project power. Crowding, inspection delays, and route scarcity turn routine traffic control into political pressure.

### Everyday life

Courier crews barter current soundings in station corridors; families sleep aboard ships waiting for safe passage; repair teams work continuously on the navigation platform.

## Kheled Watch

**Kind:** patrol-station  
**Initially known:** Yes  
**Tags:** kheled, security, diplomacy, repairs

### Player-facing identity

A compact Kheled patrol station controlling the Protectorate’s most reliable approach to the Reef.

### Director function

Kheled Watch can supply repair stores, military pilots, and disciplined patrol support, but it treats navigation access as a security concession. Prefect Tal Oren is pragmatic; Subprefect Jorad Pel is actively manufacturing evidence for intervention.

### Everyday life

The station is austere and heavily scheduled. Registered merchants receive predictable service; unregistered habitats and improvised craft experience delays and scrutiny.

## Quiet Shelf

**Kind:** stable-basin  
**Initially known:** Yes  
**Tags:** calm, science, agriculture, crew

### Player-facing identity

A relatively stable basin containing a listening array, an agricultural habitat, and enough clear space for sensor calibration and flight training.

### Director function

Quiet Shelf supports calm content, repairs, crew conversations, and calibration. Time spent here is safe but allows other fronts to advance. It is not secretly a villain base.

### Everyday life

The agricultural habitat runs on communal shifts and treats visiting crews to fresh produce. The listening array’s technicians keep meticulous handwritten route weather logs.

## Aster Basin

**Kind:** civilian-capital  
**Initially known:** No  
**Tags:** drift-concord, civilians, sanctuary, politics

### Player-facing identity

The largest civilian concentration in the Reef and the informal center of the Drift Concord, assembled from habitats, converted freighters, and tethered platforms around a dim brown dwarf.

### Director function

Aster Basin survives through concealment, shared pilots, and mutual aid. Recording or transmitting its exact coordinates changes Chart Exposure. The basin can become a trusted ally, a resentful neutral, an occupied dependency, or a mass-casualty site.

### Everyday life

Markets move between docked hulls, classrooms rotate through ships with available pressure, and public meetings are held by narrowband relay so no single habitat becomes a permanent capital.

### Connected routes

- Lagrange Gate to Aster Basin: 4.0 hours baseline, conditional
- Kheled Watch to Aster Basin: 2.5 hours baseline, restricted

## Saint Caligo Wreckfield

**Kind:** wreckfield  
**Initially known:** Yes  
**Tags:** wrecks, salvage, history, hazard

### Player-facing identity

Hundreds of wrecks from several conflicts orbit a compact gravitic knot, many stripped, some armed, and a few still sealed.

### Director function

Saint Caligo supports repeated visits. It contains survivor recoveries, cultural claims, weapons hazards, Shepherd components, and evidence from the Kheled-Crown border crisis. No single expedition exhausts the location.

### Everyday life

Breakwater divers mark safe hulls with amber lamps; memorial transmitters recite names on old emergency bands; salvage families live aboard tugs that rarely leave the field.

## Crown Shoal

**Kind:** raider-haven  
**Initially known:** No  
**Tags:** raiders, black-market, security, trade

### Player-facing identity

A maze of dense fragments and sensor shadows intermittently controlled by the Crown of Embers.

### Director function

Crown Shoal contains illicit docks, hostage exchanges, black-market chart data, and communities paying protection because no other force reaches them. Direct assault can scatter raiders without replacing the services they monopolize.

### Everyday life

Markets open only when lookout buoys signal clear approaches. Protection fees are recorded as repair subscriptions, and several captains maintain genuine rescue crews alongside coercive boarding parties.

## Shepherd Node Seven

**Kind:** ancient-installation  
**Initially known:** No  
**Tags:** shepherd, ancient, science, strategic

### Player-facing identity

A regular asteroid formation suspected to contain a buried gravitic installation.

### Director function

Node Seven is one of thirteen ancient regulators. It can stabilize nearby routes, but any operational controller can turn it into a chokepoint. Access requires evidence from independent sources or a risky blind survey.

### Everyday life

There is no current settlement. Maintenance chambers contain dormant repair drones, mineralized control conduits, and signs that prior visitors removed components without understanding them.

### Connected routes

## Deep Tide Trench

**Kind:** extreme-hazard  
**Initially known:** No  
**Tags:** finale, hazard, science, rescue

### Player-facing identity

The most unstable mapped region of the Reef, where transit lanes compress, reverse, or disappear.

### Director function

Deep Tide is the likely convergence zone for the final inversion but remains accessible earlier for proportionate high-risk operations. It should offer exceptional leverage, not arbitrary punishment.

### Everyday life

No permanent settlement survives here. Short-lived camps and sensor buoys appear after calm intervals and are abandoned when the geometry begins to sing through hull plating.

## Bellweather Anchorage

**Kind:** mobile-anchorage  
**Initially known:** No  
**Tags:** breakwater, trade, tugs, fabrication

### Player-facing identity

A moving congregation of salvage tugs, clinic barges, and trading craft that relocates as routes change.

### Director function

Bellweather is the Breakwater Guild’s practical capital. Its mobility makes it resilient and difficult to regulate. It is the best source of fabrication, towing, and informal news.

### Everyday life

Ships dock in rotating clusters around a heavily modified tender. Disputes are settled at public tool benches where each party must contribute labor while arguing its case.

## Ossuary Loop

**Kind:** memorial-zone  
**Initially known:** No  
**Tags:** memorial, identity, history, law

### Player-facing identity

A slow orbit of exodus wrecks, memorial capsules, and disputed remains near the outer edge of Saint Caligo.

Ossuary Loop is where technical identification and cultural ownership diverge. It supports Twelve Names, A Ship Named Twice, and evidence concerning the old civilian exodus.

### Everyday life

Visitors approach under low power, transmit identity declarations, and attach small memory lights to designated spars. Salvage is taboo unless a memorial council grants permission.

## Lantern Span

**Kind:** navigation-corridor  
**Initially known:** No  
**Tags:** navigation, charts, infrastructure, policy

### Player-facing identity

A promising corridor between Quiet Shelf and Aster Basin where a buoy chain could create the Reef’s first broadly usable public route.

### Director function

Lantern Span makes chart policy tangible. Public beacons improve rescue and commerce; encrypted or locally keyed beacons preserve control; central control creates dependence.

### Everyday life

When open, civilian pilots leave route notes and spare parts at each beacon. When closed, the span becomes an empty stretch of static and abandoned marker lights.

### Connected routes

## Maintenance Habitat Kappa-4

**Kind:** hidden-habitat  
**Initially known:** No  
**Tags:** hidden, captain-rhos, shepherd, survival

### Player-facing identity

A concealed pre-Federation maintenance habitat whose location is not known at campaign start.

### Director function

Kappa-4 shelters Captain Rhos, navigator Ensign Eshren Kor, and civilian pilot Mavek Sorn after the opening disaster. Their condition depends on the Rhos Survival clock. The habitat also contains partial Shepherd maintenance records.

### Everyday life

The habitat is mostly dormant. Emergency lighting, fungal air scrubbers, and ancient gravity baffles keep a few chambers habitable while systems fail in uneven cycles.
