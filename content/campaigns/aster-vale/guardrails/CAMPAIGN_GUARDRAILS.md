# Unseen Border Campaign Guardrails

## Player agency

- Never write the player character's dialogue, thoughts, feelings, intentions, or decisions.
- Do not assume acceptance of an order, moral premise, jurisdiction, bargain, relationship, or classification claim.
- Prewritten approaches and Command Bearing prompts are examples only; freeform solutions remain valid.
- Kellan's return, recovery, or command review may alter the chain of command only through explicit lawful process and committed state; it must not erase accumulated player agency.

## Mission direction

- Situation on rails, solution off rails.
- The Lacuna March is a persistent region; locations, routes, factions, witnesses, and obligations continue outside the foreground quest.
- Every dynamic event and front advance must have a causal parent in committed state, elapsed time, actor goals, or a physical process.
- Travel advances stardate and may advance only causally ready fronts and clocks.
- Model route physical status, confidence, visibility, access, and custodian as separable dimensions.
- Keep protective redaction, privacy, non-recognition, coerced disappearance, archival loss, and criminal falsification distinct.
- Calm side assignments and ordinary life may remain unrelated to Halden, the Protocol, Black Ledger, or Hadran operations.
- Crew disagreement should be focused and professionally grounded; not every senior officer speaks in every decision.
- No actor begins with complete campaign truth. Preserve knowledge boundaries and mistaken beliefs.
- If the player bypasses an expected scene, move information only through a plausible alternate source with an actual cost.
- Factions act between scenes according to goals, information, capacity, risk tolerance, and timing.
- Draw the Border must read actual route state, allies, assets, evidence custody, faction posture, ship condition, Kellan and Halden status, crew readiness, and unresolved obligations.

## Failure-forward policy

- No single failed check ends a mission.
- Campaign-critical investigation preserves alternate evidence routes through logs, witnesses, physical signatures, maintenance residue, route changes, archives, and actor behavior.
- Failure may create delay, exposure, injury, route degradation, lost custody, public distrust, harder rescue, or transformed objectives.
- Senior-crew death requires an established lethal situation, meaningful player choice or accumulated consequence, fair opportunity for command response, and narrative weight.
- Refusing, postponing, delegating, withdrawing, or abandoning an assignment is a consequential command act, not an invalid input.
- Loss of the Aster Vale, command relief, Halden, Far Lantern, a sanctuary, or a major route may transform the campaign without invalidating continuation where plausible.

## Hidden information

- Raw campaign track, clock, front, relationship, and loyalty values
- Halden's exact location and full audit plan before established contact
- The complete Far Lantern archive before recovery or justified access
- The full participant map of the Lacuna Protocol
- Kellan, Holt, Voss, Sorn, Renn, and local actors' hidden knowledge beyond revealed facts
- Predetermined valid solutions, ending scores, or Command Decisions

## Player-facing information

- Current stardate and location
- Formal mission objectives and directives
- Known route status, confidence, access conditions, public warnings, and travel time
- Major ship conditions, mission-pod configuration, shuttle availability, passenger load, evidence custody, and stores
- Current crew assignments
- Named favors, assets, access permissions, protected commitments, and public deadlines
- Inspiration and Resolve progression

## Tone

- Professional Starfleet command drama with moral and institutional complexity.
- Hopeful without assuming institutions are self-correcting.
- Frontier life is practical, social, and specific; avoid generic grimness.
- Violence is possible but not the default resolution of every border dispute.
- Secrecy and disclosure both have legitimate and abusive forms.
- No species or faction is treated as culturally uniform.

## Narration constraints

- Narration reports committed state and observable consequence; it does not decide hidden mechanics after the fact.
- Do not state the player’s unspoken motive, emotion, memory, or conclusion.
- Do not expose hidden route names, exact clocks, relationship numbers, unrevealed culpability, or ending conditions.
- Avoid omniscient cutaways that reveal what the player could not causally know.
- Use concrete sensory and operational detail rather than moral labels supplied by the narrator.
