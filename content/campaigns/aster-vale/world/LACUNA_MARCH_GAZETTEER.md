# Lacuna March Gazetteer

## Regional character

The Lacuna March is a frontier of short travel times and uneven visibility. Settlements are not distant enough to be isolated by physics alone; they are isolated by charts, permissions, patrol patterns, damaged beacons, family law, debt, fear, and the risk that being found will invite the wrong authority. Everyday life should remain visible: repair shifts, school transfers, market arrivals, funerals, ration allocations, pilot gossip, watch schedules, civic meetings, and the practical cost of a delayed ship.

## Locations

### U.S.S. Aster Vale

**ID:** `aster-vale`  
**Kind:** mobile-operational-base  
**Opening visibility:** known

The ship is the campaign's mobile command base. Its location, passenger load, brig use, chart custody, shuttle availability, and mission-pod status must remain persistent. It can control one crisis at a time and cannot replace local institutions.

**Player-safe:** A New Orleans-class frontier frigate serving as patrol ship, charting platform, diplomatic venue, and temporary refuge.

**Everyday life:** Watch rotations overlap with route reviews, temporary passengers occupy training spaces, and the wardroom becomes a courtroom, planning cell, or family meeting room depending on the day.

**Services**

- medical bay
- brig
- diplomatic suite
- long-range shuttles
- chart lab

**Hazards**

- No exceptional fixed hazard; current state may add one.

**Factions:** starfleet-border-service  
**Actors:** none

### Meridian Beacon

**ID:** `meridian-beacon`  
**Kind:** regional-hub  
**Opening visibility:** known

The safest repair and resupply point and the most documented. Border Service, inquiry officers, customs archives, and Hadran liaison staff share a platform whose public map omits traffic its controllers privately manage.

**Player-safe:** A Starfleet traffic-control platform marking the official western entrance to the Lacuna March.

**Everyday life:** Controllers maintain two route boards: the public display and a handwritten shift book of callsigns that do not officially exist. Families wait beside customs desks for destination rulings.

**Services**

- repair
- resupply
- customs
- legal review
- refugee processing

**Hazards**

- No exceptional fixed hazard; current state may add one.

**Factions:** starfleet-border-service  
**Actors:** rear-admiral-caris-holt

### Meridian Refugee Annex

**ID:** `meridian-refugee-annex`  
**Kind:** administrative-subhub  
**Opening visibility:** known

The Annex can protect people briefly but cannot become a permanent destination. Its records are vulnerable to lawful requests that may still endanger applicants. Conditions here expose whether shipboard policy produces safety, delay, or unaccountable confinement.

**Player-safe:** A converted cargo spindle used for temporary shelter, interviews, medical screening, and asylum review.

**Everyday life:** Volunteers post meal times in four languages, children trade ship-recognition cards, and advocates keep paper copies because digital files can be frozen by competing jurisdictions.

**Services**

- temporary housing
- medical screening
- asylum interviews
- family tracing

**Hazards**

- No exceptional fixed hazard; current state may add one.

**Factions:** starfleet-border-service, free-passage-network  
**Actors:** captain-jori-ven

### Halloway Colony

**ID:** `halloway-colony`  
**Kind:** federation-colony  
**Opening visibility:** known

Halloway leaders demand chart restoration, but cooperatives have hidden refugee laborers, provisioned Free Passage ships, and routed taxes from erased settlements. The colony can become a legitimate logistical ally or a political engine for closure.

**Player-safe:** A prosperous Federation agricultural colony whose exports depend on predictable routes and reliable inspection rules.

**Everyday life:** Grain elevators share landing fields with family-run shuttles. Cooperative boards publish harvest schedules, while labor rosters are kept deliberately vague in districts that remember earlier purges.

**Services**

- food supply
- civilian repair
- housing placement
- colonial records

**Hazards**

- No exceptional fixed hazard; current state may add one.

**Factions:** ilyran-colonial-council  
**Actors:** governor-lise-talar

### Gravesend Veil

**ID:** `gravesend-veil`  
**Kind:** hazardous-navigation-zone  
**Opening visibility:** known

Radiation pockets, old mines, reflected subspace returns, and changing shear make automated navigation unreliable. Local pilots preserve route knowledge through practice, oral teaching, and physical marker sequences. The Veil responds to probes, beacon work, and accumulated data; it is dangerous, not supernatural.

**Player-safe:** A sensor-distorting nebular fold used for concealed crossings between Meridian, Sable, and protected settlements.

**Everyday life:** No permanent settlement exists, but pilots leave coded memorial tags on stable rocks and emergency caches in shielded hollows. Route knowledge is taught in pairs so no single arrest erases it.

**Services**

- None reliable.

**Hazards**

- sensor distortion
- radiation pockets
- legacy mines
- false subspace returns

**Factions:** free-passage-network, lacuna-protocol-network  
**Actors:** none

### Sable Crossing

**ID:** `sable-crossing`  
**Kind:** civic-market-hub  
**Opening visibility:** known

The March's market, customs office, rumor exchange, and informal court. Its residents include Federation colonists, Hadran expatriates, couriers, and people with no recognized citizenship. Whether Sable develops accountable civic authority or competing checkpoints is a central campaign question.

**Player-safe:** A cluster of habitats and cargo platforms where official and concealed routes meet.

**Everyday life:** Dock numbers are auctioned at dawn, disputes are heard in a converted cargo theater, and merchants accept three legal identities plus a local witness seal.

**Services**

- trade
- repair
- mediation
- pilot hire
- temporary berthing

**Hazards**

- No exceptional fixed hazard; current state may add one.

**Factions:** sable-civic-assembly, free-passage-network, black-ledger-syndicate  
**Actors:** magistrate-rina-esh, lio-marek, nyra-voss

### Nysa's Lantern

**ID:** `nysas-lantern`  
**Kind:** concealed-sanctuary  
**Opening visibility:** concealed

The largest sanctuary in the March and the place most endangered by full chart publication. It has schools, workshops, elections, dissent, security problems, and residents who do not agree on whether recognition is protection or exposure.

**Player-safe:** A hidden habitat built from a disabled survey station and linked refugee transports.

**Everyday life:** Morning bells mark school shifts rather than religious observance. Hull sections are named for the ships that carried families to safety, and council sessions are broadcast internally unless a witness requests protection.

**Services**

- housing
- school
- workshops
- local government
- protected witness care

**Hazards**

- No exceptional fixed hazard; current state may add one.

**Factions:** nysas-lantern-council, free-passage-network  
**Actors:** councilor-eme-saar, commander-senka-halden

### Varin's Rest

**ID:** `varins-rest`  
**Kind:** neutral-sanctuary  
**Opening visibility:** known

A practical neutral site for mediation, patient transfer, funerals, and witness meetings. Free Passage and Hadran officers historically respected its sanctuary, but the custom has no treaty status and can erode under pressure.

**Player-safe:** A small moon settlement centered on a monastery, clinic, and memorial field for travelers who died unnamed.

**Everyday life:** Clinicians work beside monastics, visitors add names to basalt walls when identification becomes possible, and armed crews are expected to leave targeting systems powered down.

**Services**

- clinic
- mediation
- funerals
- protected meetings

**Hazards**

- No exceptional fixed hazard; current state may add one.

**Factions:** free-passage-network  
**Actors:** prior-ael-varin

### Kestrel System

**ID:** `kestrel-system`  
**Kind:** debris-and-cache-system  
**Opening visibility:** known

Halden's last verified telemetry points here. Black Ledger uses the debris field for exchanges, but the system also supports unregistered homesteads and routine survival stories unrelated to the missing officer.

**Player-safe:** A nominally empty system containing an abandoned listening array, hidden fuel caches, and a dense debris field.

**Everyday life:** Homestead shuttles travel dark to conserve power and avoid attention. Salvagers leave color-coded scrap markers that mean safety, claim, warning, or debt depending on who reads them.

**Services**

- limited fuel cache
- salvage
- sensor shelter

**Hazards**

- debris collisions
- spoofed transponders
- unmapped homestead traffic

**Factions:** black-ledger-syndicate, free-passage-network, lacuna-protocol-network  
**Actors:** none

### Kestrel Homesteads

**ID:** `kestrel-homesteads`  
**Kind:** erased-settlement  
**Opening visibility:** concealed

The settlement is proof that the blank region is a living civic system, not a database error. Residents include legal colonists, undocumented Hadran families, and people who fear both Starfleet recognition and Black Ledger control.

**Player-safe:** A dispersed settlement network omitted from current charts but still paying Federation taxes through Halloway intermediaries.

**Everyday life:** Residents maintain shared life-support depots, rotate teachers between rocks, and record property boundaries on physical plaques because official cadastral systems list the system as empty.

**Services**

- basic repair
- community stores
- local guides

**Hazards**

- No exceptional fixed hazard; current state may add one.

**Factions:** ilyran-colonial-council, free-passage-network  
**Actors:** none

### Far Lantern

**ID:** `far-lantern`  
**Kind:** archive-beacon  
**Opening visibility:** concealed

Its archive contains unfiltered traffic history from before and during the chart erasures. Restoring it could expose refugee routes, Hadran deployments, Black Ledger traffic, and Starfleet complicity. Editing or destroying it can protect people while compromising accountability.

**Player-safe:** An old navigation beacon beyond the recognized border, maintained by rotating civilian crews.

**Everyday life:** Keepers live in six-week rotations, maintain physical checksum books, and read unidentified distress bursts aloud before deleting duplicates.

**Services**

- navigation archive
- long-range communications
- sensor baseline

**Hazards**

- No exceptional fixed hazard; current state may add one.

**Factions:** lacuna-protocol-network, free-passage-network  
**Actors:** keeper-jalen-or

### Hadran Perimeter

**ID:** `hadran-perimeter`  
**Kind:** contested-border-zone  
**Opening visibility:** known

Colonel Sorn operates from a mobile patrol anchor while hardline intelligence units press for warrants, passenger data, and direct access to concealed routes. The zone can become a negotiated boundary, a blockade, or the staging area for a task group.

**Player-safe:** A patrol and inspection zone controlled by the Hadran Transitional Authority near the eastern edge of the March.

**Everyday life:** Patrol crews trade repair parts with civilian traffic between inspections, families wait for exit permits, and every vessel keeps separate manifests for military and civil authorities.

**Services**

- None reliable.

**Hazards**

- armed inspections
- warrant seizures
- civil-war traffic

**Factions:** hadran-transitional-authority  
**Actors:** colonel-varek-sorn, major-kael-renn

## Travel texture

Most major journeys take hours rather than weeks. That keeps obligations simultaneous: a medical delay at Nysa, an inspection dispute at Sable, and a Hadran contact near Kestrel can all remain live within one watch cycle. The Director should announce estimated travel, known confidence, access restrictions, and likely front movement before departure when the crew can know them.
