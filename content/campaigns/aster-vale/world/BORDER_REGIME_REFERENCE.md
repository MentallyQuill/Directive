# Lacuna March Border Regime Reference

## Purpose

The finale requires an operational answer to border governance. This reference describes institutional components the player can combine, reject, or redesign. It is not a menu of endings.

## Required functions

| Function | Questions a viable regime must answer |
| --- | --- |
| Navigation safety | Who surveys, validates, repairs, warns, and pays? |
| Access | Who may travel, inspect, refuse inspection, invoke emergency passage, or appeal denial? |
| Sanctuary | How can protected people receive rescue, medicine, family contact, and representation without general exposure? |
| Evidence | Who holds records, what is public, what is protected, and how is misuse audited? |
| Asylum and identity | Which records establish personhood, family, residence, and protection across jurisdictions? |
| Enforcement | Who may interdict, board, arrest, seize, disarm, escort, or use force? |
| Custody succession | What happens when a pilot, council, officer, or archive becomes unavailable or corrupt? |
| Review | Who can challenge a route classification, detention, exposure, disappearance, or use of credentials? |

## Institutional components

| Component | Strength | Failure mode |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Starfleet chart authority | Technical standards, rescue capacity, and broad accountability | Central access can expose protected traffic and marginalize non-Federation residents. |
| Local route councils | Consent, context, and durable community knowledge | Uneven rights, capture by local elites, and limited enforcement capacity. |
| Joint border board | Shared legitimacy and cross-border coordination | Deadlock, veto abuse, intelligence collection, and diplomatic fragility. |
| Neutral escrow | Auditable protected data and bounded release | High-value target; legitimacy depends on appointment and technical security. |
| Pilot guild custody | Practical navigation and distributed resilience | Debt, monopoly, inherited privilege, and informal coercion. |
| Public registry | Equal discoverability and easier rescue | Pattern exposure, extraction, pursuit, and commercial pressure. |
| Protected annex | Separates safety data from identity and destination data | Classification abuse and weak public oversight. |
| Emergency override | Fast rescue when ordinary consent is impossible | Exception can become ordinary rule. |

## Minimum settlement tests

- A medical distress can reach help without first resolving every status dispute.
- A protected community can challenge misuse without revealing itself to every authority.
- A route custodian can be replaced, reviewed, or sanctioned.
- A Hadran family can seek a missing person without gaining automatic access to every dissident refuge.
- Starfleet can prove why it acted without publishing every protected coordinate.
- A traveler cannot be disappeared merely because records conflict.
- Black Ledger or another carrier cannot monopolize indispensable passage through debt and violence.
- The system still functions after the Aster Vale departs.

## Failure signs

- All meaningful knowledge sits with one person or one ship.
- The regime requires permanent Starfleet emergency rule.
- Sanctuary depends entirely on false records that cannot be reviewed.
- Public charts expose people faster than enforcement can protect them.
- Private carriers can deny medical or family travel without appeal.
- Every restriction is permanent and every override is secret.
- The settlement describes moral principles but lacks maintenance, staffing, custody succession, or response times.
