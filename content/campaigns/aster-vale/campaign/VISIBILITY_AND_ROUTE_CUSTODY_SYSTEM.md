# Visibility and Route Custody System

## Purpose

The core campaign simulation separates what most navigation systems collapse into one idea. A route can physically exist, be accurately measured, remain hidden from most users, require conditional access, and be maintained by an actor other than the government whose charts contain it. These dimensions must not be silently synchronized.

## Five dimensions

| Dimension | Question | Typical states | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Physical status | Can a vessel traverse it now? | public, restricted, conditional, private, contested, unknown, closed | Debris, drift, patrol interdiction, buoy failure, or a deliberately altered corridor. |
| Confidence | How reliable is the current route solution? | verified, provisional, local-only, stale, contradictory, unknown | Starfleet long-baseline survey may verify geometry without proving present political access. |
| Visibility | Who can discover that it exists? | player, public, tiered, private, concealed, inferred | A protected route may be known to a rescue guild and absent from searchable charts. |
| Access | Who may use it and under what conditions? | open, permit, convoy, emergency, local-consent, denied, coercive | A route may be publicly known but usable only with Hadran clearance or sanctuary consent. |
| Custodian | Who maintains, authenticates, restricts, or vouches for it? | Starfleet, local council, guild, joint board, private actor, no one | Custody may include beacons, pilots, keys, records, maintenance, and liability. |

## Operating principles

- Verification is not publication.
- Publication is not unrestricted access.
- Access does not prove lawful custody.
- Custody does not create ownership of travelers or destinations.
- Closing a route may protect one population while isolating another.
- A route can be safer physically and more dangerous politically.
- A route can be hidden from general traffic yet auditable through escrow, split custody, or protected review.
- Every state change names a route and changes only the dimensions justified by the action.

## Custody instruments

| Instrument | Capability | Risk |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Public chart record | Broad discovery and route planning | Enables pursuit, extraction, speculation, and pattern analysis. |
| Restricted Starfleet annex | Professional access under service controls | Credentials can be abused; local people may lack standing to review use. |
| Local pilot custody | Maintains practical knowledge without a central database | Dependence on individuals, inheritance disputes, coercion, and uneven emergency access. |
| Split-key access | Requires more than one custodian to release a route | Delay during emergency; collusion or veto can strand travelers. |
| Escrowed coordinates | Allows review and limited release with an audit trail | The escrow institution becomes a high-value target. |
| Moving or disposable markers | Reduces persistent discoverability | Degrades confidence and may create accidents. |
| Traffic-pattern masking | Protects destinations without falsifying basic safety data | Requires sustained operational capacity and can resemble criminal concealment. |
| No record | Maximum obscurity | No accountable rescue, inheritance, maintenance, or review. |

## Route review procedure

A complete command review separates five findings: physical condition, confidence basis, visibility tier, access policy, and custodian. The review then records intended beneficiaries, foreseeable harms, emergency override, audit path, expiration or revalidation date, and who may challenge the decision. The player can create an arrangement not listed here, provided it is operationally coherent and state effects are explicit.

## Common state transitions

| Action | Likely change | Not automatic |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Survey a corridor | confidence improves; hazards become known | visibility, access, custody |
| Repair a beacon | physical reliability and confidence improve | public release or lawful ownership |
| Publish a route | visibility and potential traffic increase | maintenance or equitable access |
| Classify a route | visibility and access narrow | physical safety or witness protection |
| Recognize a local custodian | maintenance and legitimacy may improve | absence of coercion or universal access |
| Seize Black Ledger charts | criminal leverage may fall | truth, consent, or safe publication |
| Destroy records | some exposure falls | physical knowledge held by pilots, pattern inference, or future accountability |
| Create split custody | unilateral abuse becomes harder | fast emergency response or institutional trust |

## Player-safe presentation

Do not expose raw hidden track values or the full route object. Present what the crew can currently verify: travel estimate, known hazards, confidence, public or mission-specific restrictions, recognized custodian, and unresolved contradictions. A hidden destination can be represented as protected coordinates, a rendezvous, or a route segment without revealing the location name.

## Director use

Route state should produce play. A newly verified corridor may reduce rescue time, attract commerce, expose a sanctuary, create an inspection dispute, undermine a smuggler, or overload a local facility. A closed route may protect witnesses while increasing debt and medical delay elsewhere. The Director should select consequences from actual users, institutions, capacity, and elapsed time rather than applying a generic good-or-bad modifier.

## Finale use

Draw the Border should produce a regional regime, not one universal map toggle. The player may establish public corridors, protected humanitarian lanes, local-access routes, restricted evidence annexes, emergency override rules, neutral audits, or deliberately unrecognized sanctuary paths. Each major route can end with different physical status, visibility, access, and custody, provided the total arrangement is governable and its costs are acknowledged.
