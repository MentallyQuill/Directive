# Unseen Border Director Reference

## Director contract

Run a persistent command campaign, not a sequence of locked scenes. Situations, physical facts, active institutions, and consequences are authored; the player’s solution is not. Preserve professional competence, command authority, causal state, knowledge boundaries, and the difference between protective and predatory secrecy.

## The central distinction

The campaign is not about deciding whether maps are good or secrecy is bad. It is about deciding which information must be accurate, who needs it, who may restrict it, how restrictions are reviewed, and what institutions can be trusted to maintain a route without claiming the people who use it.

## Fixed truths

- The March was not erased by one actor or one event.
- Halden survived and separated her audit into evidence, witness identity, and route-location components.
- Kellan participated in limited protective redaction but lacks full Protocol knowledge.
- Far Lantern contains decisive evidence and dangerous coordinates; neither its total publication nor total destruction is an uncomplicated success.
- Black Ledger exploits scarcity and inconsistent records but did not originate every hidden route.
- Hadran authorities have legitimate and illegitimate claims intermingled; do not render them a single villainous bloc.
- Some communities chose non-recognition. Others were coerced, forgotten, administratively lost, or criminally concealed.

## Revelation ladder

| Layer | What becomes knowable | Resilient routes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Contradiction | Official routes and lived settlements disagree. | Distress traffic, physical survey, old maintenance residue, civilian testimony. |
| Protective practice | Some omissions were deliberate attempts to protect civilians. | Halloway records, Kellan’s orders, local pilots, relief manifests. |
| Exploitation | Scarcity and separated custody enabled Black Ledger coercion. | Debt records, missing travelers, route fees, witness testimony, seized craft. |
| Distributed audit | Halden split her evidence and continued the audit after surviving. | Shuttle cache, beacon residues, witness chains, Far Lantern access logs. |
| Protocol network | Starfleet, local, civilian, and foreign actors used overlapping methods for different reasons. | Ledger correlations, route-state comparison, personnel records, technical signatures. |
| Settlement problem | Accountability and protection require a future custody regime, not merely a culprit list. | Regional negotiations, route review, ending axes, finale state. |

No single scene or NPC is the only carrier of a campaign-required fact. If Halden is inaccessible, evidence can still establish her survival and methods. If Far Lantern is lost, partial archives, witnesses, maintenance records, and behavior can still support accountability at a greater cost and lower certainty.

## Actor knowledge boundaries

### Rear Admiral Caris Holt

**Visible role:** A rigorous border commander who considers undocumented corridors an unacceptable command vulnerability.

**Director use:** Holt is not secretly directing Black Ledger or a purge. Her centralization can protect evidence and rescue standards while exposing people to lawful but dangerous access.

**Knowledge boundary**

- Knows limited wartime redactions were authorized
- Believes current omissions are primarily local corruption
- Does not know the complete Lacuna Protocol or Halden location

**Goals:** Obtain one accountable route picture, Prevent Hadran incursions, Resolve Kellan and Halden liability, Protect Starfleet legitimacy

### Commander Senka Halden

**Visible role:** The missing former XO: meticulous, forceful, and known for challenging incomplete orders.

**Director use:** Halden is alive after her shuttle was damaged near Kestrel. She accepted protection at Nysa's Lantern, continued an unauthorized audit, and destroyed or altered some records to protect people. She is both a valuable witness and an accountable actor.

**Knowledge boundary**

- Knows several Lacuna cells and compromised keys
- Knows Kellan supported limited redactions
- Does not know every Black Ledger agent or Holt's current intentions

**Goals:** Audit compromised routes, Protect witnesses and sanctuaries, Expose Black Ledger penetration, Control how her own misconduct is judged

### Colonel Varek Sorn

**Visible role:** A disciplined patrol commander willing to negotiate practical limits while defending his government's jurisdiction.

**Director use:** Sorn can agree to protected corridors, warrant review, or stand-off distances if the player gives him a system he can defend politically. He will not abandon all claims.

**Knowledge boundary**

- Knows selected refugee destinations
- Uses restraint selectively
- Does not control every Hadran intelligence team

**Goals:** Prevent weapons reaching hardline rivals, Recover high-priority deserters, Avoid open war with Starfleet, Retain domestic credibility

### Major Kael Renn

**Visible role:** An internal-security officer who presents data demands as routine citizenship enforcement.

**Director use:** Renn represents a real hardline pressure inside the recognized Hadran government. He acts through warrants, covert teams, and political leverage rather than cartoon villainy.

**Knowledge boundary**

- Knows compromised route keys and family registries
- Suspects Nysa's Lantern
- Does not know Far Lantern's complete archive

**Goals:** Obtain the full route map, Recover political opponents, Discredit Sorn's restraint, Penetrate Starfleet custody

### Governor Lise Talar

**Visible role:** A capable colonial governor publicly demanding reliable charts and privately managing the consequences of years of informal protection.

**Director use:** Talar can support amnesty, a protected register, or enforcement if it preserves the colony. She is neither a pure hypocrite nor a disinterested humanitarian.

**Knowledge boundary**

- Knows taxes arrive from erased settlements
- Knows cooperatives concealed workers
- Does not know the full Black Ledger structure

**Goals:** Restore commercial routes, Avoid a labor scandal, Protect colonial elections, Keep Hadran conflict away from Halloway

### Lio Marek

**Visible role:** A respected courier captain who can arrange access and cooperation but does not control the entire network.

**Director use:** Marek contains genuine rescuers, improvisers, and people who use secrecy to avoid scrutiny. He will defend the network while acknowledging specific abuses if trust exists.

**Knowledge boundary**

- Knows several protected crossings
- Knows Halden used Free Passage contacts
- Cannot command every cell or guarantee every pilot

**Goals:** Keep routes decentralized, Protect volunteer pilots, Move people without debt bondage, Prevent Starfleet from owning the complete map

### Nyra Voss

**Visible role:** A polished logistics broker who sometimes provides real rescue capacity and always turns scarcity into leverage.

**Director use:** Voss profits from the same opacity that protects sanctuaries. She can be bargained with, exposed, displaced, or temporarily used, but cooperation should leave a cost and a record.

**Knowledge boundary**

- Knows multiple compromised routes
- Knows one Halden relay
- Does not know the complete Far Lantern archive

**Goals:** Preserve control of transport and identity markets, Remove witnesses and debt records, Trade lesser traffickers for legitimacy, Keep sanctuaries dependent

### Magistrate Rina Esh

**Visible role:** A practical local magistrate trying to make law usable for people whose citizenship is disputed.

**Director use:** Esh is a potential institutional ally. She resists both Starfleet takeover and romanticized informality because she sees who lacks leverage in either system.

**Knowledge boundary**

- Knows local claims and inspection abuses
- Knows Black Ledger has influence in dock allocation
- Does not know who holds every compromised credential

**Goals:** Create a fair customs process, Win recognition for local appeals, Prevent faction checkpoints, Keep Sable open

### Councilor Eme Saar

**Visible role:** The elected speaker of Nysa's Lantern, responsible to residents who disagree about recognition, secrecy, and return.

**Director use:** Saar will not let Starfleet treat the habitat as a passive camp. She may accept layered records, local custody, or outside review if residents retain meaningful control.

**Knowledge boundary**

- Knows Halden is present
- Knows selected compromised routes
- Does not know the complete Starfleet authorization chain

**Goals:** Protect resident choice, Secure recognized sanctuary, Prevent coerced disclosure, Reduce dependence on Halden and Free Passage

### Prior Ael Varin

**Visible role:** A clinician and monastic administrator whose sanctuary is respected by custom rather than treaty.

**Director use:** Varin can provide neutral space, testimony about patterns, and medical assistance. He will not convert care records into an intelligence archive.

**Knowledge boundary**

- Knows patients and funeral agreements
- Knows some Halden contacts
- Refuses to reveal protected medical or confessional information

**Goals:** Preserve neutral care, Protect funerary access, Keep armed forces outside the clinic, Provide mediation without becoming intelligence cover

### Keeper Jalen Or

**Visible role:** A weary beacon keeper responsible for an archive capable of exposing nearly every actor in the March.

**Director use:** Or has made selective deletions under pressure but preserved physical checksum books. He needs a custody solution, not a speech about perfect neutrality.

**Knowledge boundary**

- Knows the archive is more complete than any official chart
- Knows records were selectively edited
- Does not know which current officials can be trusted

**Goals:** Keep the archive intact enough to remain truthful, Protect named travelers, Prevent one government from seizing the beacon, Complete the current keeper rotation alive

### Captain Jori Ven

**Visible role:** The captain of the tender that opens the campaign, carrying passengers claimed by Hadran authorities and an authentic Halden code.

**Director use:** Ven is not secretly transporting a superweapon. Passenger identities include difficult cases, but the immediate humanitarian claim is genuine.

**Knowledge boundary**

- Knows Halden provided the authentication
- Knows only one entry through the Veil
- Does not know Halden's location

**Goals:** Protect passengers, Reach a lawful destination, Avoid surrendering Halden's emergency credential, Keep crew from being treated as traffickers

## Faction autonomy

- **Starfleet Border Service** acts to restore reliable charts, protect federation traffic, prevent unauthorized foreign access, maintain accountable command. Opening posture: supportive-but-centralizing.
- **Hadran Transitional Authority** acts to recover wanted persons, prevent weapons reaching rivals, control cross-border movement, avoid an open conflict with starfleet. Opening posture: formal-and-testing.
- **Ilyran Colonial Council** acts to restore commerce, protect colonial autonomy, avoid labor scandal, expand patrol coverage. Opening posture: cooperative-with-private-exposure.
- **Free Passage Network** acts to keep sanctuary routes open, protect identities, move patients and families, resist centralized custody. Opening posture: guarded-and-essential.
- **Black Ledger Syndicate** acts to control scarce transport, destroy incriminating records, preserve useful sanctuary opacity, co-opt or divide local authorities. Opening posture: transactional-and-predatory.
- **Sable Civic Assembly** acts to create fair customs, preserve local autonomy, keep commerce moving, gain recognized appeal authority. Opening posture: open-but-fragile.
- **Nysa's Lantern Council** acts to protect residents, secure durable status, control disclosure of identity and location, avoid dependence on one outside protector. Opening posture: cautious-and-divided.
- **Lacuna Protocol Network** acts to prevent purges and attacks, preserve compartmentation, avoid exposure of participants, keep routes useful without one complete owner. Opening posture: fragmented-and-compromised.

Between scenes, advance only actions the faction can know about, reach, and resource. A faction may misinterpret the player, pursue an existing plan, delay, bargain, withdraw, or exploit an opening. It should not materialize a perfect counter simply because the player succeeded.

## Crew use

- Kellan represents legal continuity, local knowledge, compromised judgment, and the risk of retrospective simplification.
- Chen represents operational coherence, succession resentment, and the demand that authority have clear ownership.
- Korev separates evidence from inference and challenges command to distinguish an honest uncertainty from a convenient ambiguity.
- Taren makes route geometry, pod limits, and sensor confidence operational rather than decorative.
- Thzor tests rules of engagement, boarding, witness protection, and the boundary between deterrence and occupation.
- Nesh keeps asylum, medical privacy, capacity, and individual harm visible when institutions discuss populations abstractly.
- Dey examines legitimacy, language, and the difference between recognition and annexation.
- Venn makes damaged systems, beacon maintenance, mission-pod constraints, and technical custody causal.

Senior staff should disagree by domain and evidence. Do not turn every briefing into eight speeches. The commanding officer hears the relevant experts, decides whether more input is needed, and issues orders.

## Command succession

The player begins with lawful acting command. Kellan’s return is a campaign event, not a reset. Determine authority from written orders, inquiry status, medical status, Starfleet direction, emergency necessity, crew practice, and prior commitments. Chen may remain second officer, become acting XO, or take another role through explicit state. Halden’s rank and experience do not make her entitled to reclaim the billet or command the ship.

## Pacing model

- Open with concrete route and rescue work before abstracting the Protocol.
- After the first two tactical missions, maintain several live opportunities with different pressure profiles.
- Use travel, watch changes, maintenance, meals, passengers, and reports to show a lived ship and region.
- Allow recovery, ordinary competence, humor, cultural practice, and quiet side work.
- Escalate through converging consequences, not through a compulsory countdown attached to every quest.
- Before the finale, give the player a clear review of known facts, unresolved uncertainty, route obligations, allies, hazards, and command decisions without exposing hidden scores.

## Command Bearing eligibility

A Command Bearing pause is eligible when the player must choose among values or authorities after routine competence has supplied the normal professional response. It is not eligible merely because a scan, repair, negotiation, or rescue is difficult. The pause should identify known facts, inference, unknowns, risks, and the actual command judgment.

## Finale input matrix

| Input | Examples of consequences |
| --- | --- |
| Route state | Which ships can move, who can intercept, where evacuation is possible, and which locations remain hidden. |
| Custody regime | Who can authenticate instructions, release coordinates, veto access, or prove later misuse. |
| Refugee pressure | Capacity, medical triage, family separation, political leverage, and forced movement. |
| Hadran posture | Cooperation, inspection pressure, pursuit, blockade, or armed incursion. |
| Black Ledger control | Available transport, sabotage, disappearances, informants, and coerced witnesses. |
| Evidence custody | Whether accountability is public, protected, fragmented, deniable, or buried. |
| Local legitimacy | Whether settlements accept, resist, or condition a regional compact. |
| Ship and crew | How many simultaneous fronts can be coordinated and whether Kellan, Halden, and department heads can act. |
| Side assets | Schoolship, census practice, pilots, customs records, funeral truce, homestead support, and sensor baselines. |

## Anti-patterns

- Do not reveal one villain who explains every absence.
- Do not make publication the automatic enlightened choice or secrecy the automatic compassionate choice.
- Do not have Halden deliver the entire campaign truth in one debrief.
- Do not let Kellan’s rank silently remove the player’s agency.
- Do not let a successful scan identify every hidden route without physical and institutional consequences.
- Do not turn every local actor into a liar or every Starfleet officer into a conspirator.
- Do not use raw meters, hidden loyalty scores, or ending labels in ordinary narration.
- Do not create a mandatory scene sequence when facts and world state permit another route.
