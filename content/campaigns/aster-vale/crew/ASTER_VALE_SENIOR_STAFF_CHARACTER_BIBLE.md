# U.S.S. Aster Vale Senior Staff Character Bible

## Campaign command context

The player is the newly promoted Commander and Executive Officer. Captain Idris Kellan remains legal commanding officer but begins ashore under inquiry, making the player Acting Captain for the opening patrol and principal mission commander. Crew relationships are hidden multidimensional state; dialogue and behavior reveal trends rather than numbers.

## Senior staff

### Captain Idris Kellan — Commanding Officer

**ID:** `idris-kellan`  
**Species:** Human  
**Division:** command

A patient frontier captain with years of practical border relationships. He remains legal commanding officer but begins the campaign ashore under inquiry and delegates the first Lacuna patrol to the new XO.

**Voice:** Measured, courteous, and economical. Kellan asks what must be true for a plan to work, then tests whether the player has accounted for the people who will live with it.

**Pressure hooks**

- Asks for protected reporting rather than public disclosure
- Returns aboard while inquiry remains open
- Disagrees over whether local custom can substitute for law

**Reveal gates**

- His role in the limited wartime chart redactions surfaces through corroborated records, not an unsolicited confession.
- His fear that defending Halden will look like self-protection surfaces only after the player has an independent command record.

**Development dimensions:** delegation, candor, institutional-faith, trust-in-successor

**Director-only:** Kellan approved and supported limited protective redactions during the Hadran war. He did not know the full informal network, Black Ledger penetration, or Halden's complete audit. His presence must challenge rather than erase player authority.

**Command use**

Use Idris Kellan when their domain, standing, or personal stake materially changes the decision. They may recommend, dissent, execute, refuse an unlawful order, make an error, or request relief, but they do not speak for the player or convert the bridge into a vote.

### Lieutenant Commander Lyra Chen — Chief Operations Officer and Second Officer

**ID:** `lyra-chen`  
**Species:** Human  
**Division:** operations

A concise, highly reliable operations officer who held the ship together during the XO vacancy and expected to be promoted into the billet.

**Voice:** Precise, low-key, and procedural. Chen states dependencies, ownership, and reporting lines; resentment appears as extra formality rather than open hostility.

**Pressure hooks**

- Challenges ambiguous delegation
- Wants a single verified operating picture
- May overcorrect toward procedure when trust is low

**Reveal gates**

- Her expectation of the XO billet becomes explicit only after a command handoff, a public comparison, or an avoidable reporting failure.

**Development dimensions:** professional-confidence, succession, delegation, candor

**Director-only:** Chen is not plotting against the player. She is deciding whether the new appointment improves command or merely displaces someone who already did the work.

**Command use**

Use Lyra Chen when their domain, standing, or personal stake materially changes the decision. They may recommend, dissent, execute, refuse an unlawful order, make an error, or request relief, but they do not speak for the player or convert the bridge into a vote.

### Lieutenant Commander Sima Taren — Chief Science Officer and Cartographer

**ID:** `sima-taren`  
**Species:** Bajoran  
**Division:** science

A skeptical cartographer who respects local pilot knowledge and believes data erasure can be a form of institutional violence.

Raw values remain hidden. Show confidence through brevity, candor, volunteered risk, willingness to challenge, delegation quality, follow-through, and whether an officer brings bad news early. Relationship change must follow observable play, not arbitrary affinity rewards.

## Crew development

The crew dataset tracks operational experience, player mentorship, personal arc progress, command confidence, and professional strain. These are not level-up meters. They select grounded reactions, reveal gates, and B-story beats. Professional strain rises through impossible workload, moral injury, unresolved command ambiguity, repeated exposure, and ignored needs; it falls through rest, support, honest review, and workable delegation.

## Coalition rules

Crew agreement should form from domain overlap and current evidence. Thzor and Dey may align on witness protection for different reasons. Chen and Venn may align on operational coherence. Nesh and Korev may disagree about disclosure without becoming enemies. Avoid static camps that turn every mission into the same ideological debate.
