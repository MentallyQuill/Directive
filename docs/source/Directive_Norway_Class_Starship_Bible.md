# Directive

## Norway-Class Starship Bible

**Document status:** Ship location and portrayal design baseline  
**Version:** 0.1  
**Campaign era:** 2370s baseline, adaptable by campaign  
**Primary campaign use:** Norway-class campaigns including the U.S.S. Celandine  
**Companion documents:** *Celandine / Enemy's Garden*, *Directive Intrepid-Class Starship Bible*, and *Character Bible Shaping Guide*

---

## Document Purpose

This document defines the Norway-class starship as a story environment rather than a list of technical trivia.

It should guide:

- Director decisions about which ship facts matter in a scene.
- Narrator descriptions of exterior approach, internal movement, relief staging, damage, bow-phaser posture, and daily shipboard life.
- Ship Director and Mission Director packet construction.
- Future ship-area datasets, retrieval cards, and continuity facts.
- Contradiction guards for hard physical details, such as the class's flat-iron hull, wing-pylon gap, and bow-mounted phaser-emitter identity.

This bible is the deep source. It should not be injected wholesale. Runtime packets should lift only the facts relevant to the current scene: one hard anchor, two sensory or operational textures, and one constraint or pressure.

The Norway-class baseline here is modeled from sparse canon and production reference: a 2370s Starfleet class seen at the Battle of Sector 001, visually broad and flat, with a Defiant-like keel, wing-shaped warp pylons separated by an aft gap, and a notable dorsal bow phaser emitter. The U.S.S. Celandine is not the U.S.S. Budapest. It shares class layout, hull logic, and 2370s design assumptions, but its campaign owns its relief, agricultural-science, quarantine, and convoy-protection modifications.

# 1. Reference Grounding

## 1.1 Source posture

Use canon and reputable franchise references for hard class facts, then translate those facts into Directive-authored scene guidance. Do not copy episode dialogue, wiki prose, or reference-book prose into generated content. The useful layer for Directive is not "say what a reference page says"; it is "make the ship behave like a Norway-class vessel when a scene touches the ship."

Primary hard anchors for this bible:

- The Norway class is a Federation Starfleet class active in the 2370s.
- At least four Norway-class vessels fought at the Battle of Sector 001 in 2373.
- The named U.S.S. Budapest is a Norway-class vessel associated with that battle.
- The class has a dorsal bow phaser emitter, and its keel resembles the Defiant-class hull language.
- The hull resembles a broad flat-iron shape, with a secondary-hull/pylon structure formed by wing-shaped warp pylons and a gap between them.
- Reputable reference analysis estimates the class around 355 meters long. Directive treats this as a useful scale baseline, not a canon deck plan.
- Production background describes the ship as one of the CGI designs created for *Star Trek: First Contact* and as having a "Voyager-esque" design intent.

Directive extrapolates interior decks, room locations, shuttle operations, crew complement, relief spaces, and support systems because no complete canonical Norway deck plan exists. The goal is internal consistency, not pretending extrapolated details are canon.

## 1.2 Canon and adaptation boundary

Treat the following as hard class truth unless a package intentionally overrides it:

- The Norway class is a 2370s Starfleet vessel, not a 2360s Galaxy-family kitbash.
- The broad flat hull, wing-pylon gap, Defiant-like keel influence, and dorsal bow phaser emitter should shape exterior descriptions and damage reports.
- The class can plausibly serve in combat, convoy protection, relief support, patrol, and regional response, but it should not become a pure battleship.
- Canon interior detail is sparse. Do not invent "canon" deck claims.

Treat the following as Directive-authored baseline choices:

- Deck count, area placements, and crew complement.
- Main shuttlebay placement: Directive places Norway small-craft operations in an aft centerline bay opening into the gap between wing-shaped pylons, approached from astern. This uses the visible class geometry and avoids generic saucer-belly assumptions.
- Celandine's relief and agricultural-science modifications: crop-transition labs, seed vaults, quarantine wards, cargo sanitation, and public-accounting rooms.
- The class's default story role: a compact 2370s response vessel able to shift between tactical cover, relief logistics, science, and diplomacy.

## 1.3 TNG, DS9, and VOY extrapolation posture

Use *The Next Generation* for core Starfleet professionalism: bridge chain of command, medical ethics, transporter discipline, and the expectation that even armed vessels remain scientific and diplomatic instruments.

Use *Deep Space Nine* for postwar and wartime pressure: convoy protection, biological aftermath, relief politics, occupation residue, triage at scale, and the fact that Starfleet ships can become local institutions after a war.

Use *Voyager* for 2370s compact design language and autonomy: efficient rooms, clean interfaces, variable scientific use, quarantine procedures, and the way a smaller ship's ordinary spaces carry crisis weight.

# 2. Class Personality

## 2.1 At a glance

| Field | Directive baseline |
|---|---|
| Class role | Compact 2370s Starfleet response vessel for relief, patrol, convoy protection, science, and tactical support |
| Story scale | Similar length to Steamrunner but with a flatter hull and more centralized mission spine |
| Visual identity | Flat-iron main hull, Defiant-like keel, wing-shaped pylons with aft gap, dorsal bow phaser emitter |
| Social identity | Practical, public-facing, relief-oriented, disciplined under biological and logistical pressure |
| Dramatic engine | Care under armed protection |
| Central contradiction | Built with visible combat authority, but best used when it keeps fragile systems alive without becoming their ruler |
| Default tone | Clean, compact, purposeful, medically and logistically serious |
| Avoid | Treating it as a hospital ship with no tactical edge, a Defiant-class gunboat, or an Intrepid clone |

## 2.2 Core engine

A Norway turns crisis response into accountable care.

It has the posture to enter dangerous space and the volume to carry meaningful relief, but it is not large enough to solve a region by itself. Its best stories center on limited capacity: which farms receive seed, which convoy gets cover, which quarantine remains strict, which colony's data is trusted, and when relief becomes control.

In scene terms, the Norway class rewards commanders who understand that protection and dependency are dangerously close. A Norway can keep people alive, but if it becomes the only food, medicine, escort, or technical authority in a region, the ship itself becomes a political force.

## 2.3 Central contradiction

The ship's visible combat systems make relief possible and morally complicated.

The dorsal bow phaser emitter, flat hull, and Defiant-like keel imply force. The relief holds, labs, quarantine rooms, and convoy procedures imply care. The class works best when those are held together: a phaser array securing a famine convoy, a quarantine field protecting a harvest, a tactical watch standing beside an agricultural scientist.

This contradiction should shape narration. A Norway can carry seed vaults and medical teams under the shadow of a bow emitter. It can protect a convoy without deciding that it owns the route. It can save a community without making that community permanently dependent on Starfleet.

## 2.4 Storytelling values

**Capacity with limits.** The ship can stabilize one crisis at a time. It cannot replace planetary agriculture, civil government, hospitals, or freight networks.

**Clean rooms and dirty politics.** Relief spaces should be organized, sterile, and careful. The decisions made there should be politically uncomfortable.

**Armed care.** Tactical systems protect relief work, but their presence changes how civilians, former enemies, and local governments perceive the ship.

**Biological seriousness.** Quarantine, contamination, seed custody, crop transition, inoculation, and ecology should feel concrete, not hand-waved.

**Compact central spine.** The class should feel navigable: bridge, mission ops, quarantine/medical, cargo/relief, labs, engineering, and aft shuttlebay are connected by a working spine rather than scattered hotel space.

# 3. Exterior Bible

## 3.1 Silhouette and first read

A Norway reads as broad, flat, and purposeful. Its main hull has a flat-iron quality rather than a round saucer. The keel suggests a tougher, more compact Starfleet lineage. The warp pylons form wing-like structures with a gap at the aft centerline. The dorsal bow phaser emitter gives the ship a visible forward threat posture.

For narration, describe the exterior through operational perspective:

- A shuttle pilot sees the aft pylon gap, centerline bay lights, broad flat hull, and the ship's wing-shaped warp geometry.
- A relief convoy sees a Starfleet vessel wide enough to feel protective and armed enough to deter opportunists.
- A farmer or planetary official sees cargo doors, sanitation lights, quarantine markings, and a bow emitter that makes help feel conditional.
- A damage-control scene sees pylon stress, keel scoring, bay-door misalignment, contamination flags, or bow-emitter heat.

## 3.2 Exterior zones

| Exterior zone | Story function | Scene detail |
|---|---|---|
| Dorsal bow and phaser emitter | Tactical identity and deterrence | Bow emitter glow, fire-control shutters, heat scoring, political discomfort |
| Broad flat main hull | Relief, labs, crew, and command volume | Long hull lines, inspection panels, medical/cargo markings, sensor pallets |
| Defiant-like keel | Structural toughness and lower support | Keel shadows, armor-like geometry, maintenance access, impact scars |
| Wing-shaped warp pylons | Propulsion and aft spatial identity | Pylon gap, wing roots, stress lights, warp-field posture |
| Aft centerline bay | Small-craft launch and recovery | Approach strobes in pylon gap, bay force field, shuttle path from astern |
| Ventral service/cargo locks | Relief logistics | Cargo doors, sanitation tents, modular pallets, emergency umbilicals |
| Shield and phaser arcs | Convoy protection | Shields flaring over a relief convoy, phaser arcs held defensive-first |

## 3.3 Shuttle approach truth

Directive baseline: Norway small-craft operations use an aft centerline bay opening into the gap between wing-shaped warp pylons. Approach is from astern along the pylon gap, with bay control coordinating shields, pylon field stress, and cargo/medical intake.

Correct approach shape:

- The shuttle lines up behind the ship, centered on the aft pylon gap.
- Approach lights run along the inner pylon geometry and bay threshold.
- Bay control watches pylon field state, aft shields, and cargo-deck clearance.
- The shuttle enters a compact working bay linked directly to relief staging and medical/quarantine routes.

Incorrect approach shape:

- A wide mouth in the underside of a saucer the ship does not have.
- A Galaxy-style dorsal main shuttlebay.
- A Defiant-style tiny launch tube that cannot support relief operations.
- A routine bay placed in the bow near the dorsal phaser emitter.

If prose needs a dramatic external view, use the pylon gap: the shuttle crosses between wing shadows, the bay force field glows ahead, and the broad hull above looks less like a palace than a moving platform for care.

## 3.4 Relief and convoy posture

A Norway should look most distinctive when it is protecting something slower or more vulnerable.

Useful exterior postures:

- **Convoy shield:** positioned above or forward of civilian freighters, defensive arcs bright but weapons held.
- **Relief station:** cargo locks open, shuttles cycling, sanitation fields projected around the bay and cargo doors.
- **Quarantine hold:** warning lights, sealed approach lanes, transport inhibitors, medical beacons.
- **Bow deterrent:** dorsal emitter charged visibly enough to make pirates, raiders, or desperate local forces reconsider.
- **Ecological survey:** low orbit or station-keeping with sensor pallets and sample shuttles working a planetary grid.

# 4. Interior Design Language

## 4.1 Overall feel

The Norway interior is clean, compact, and logistical. It should feel newer and more purpose-built than a New Orleans, less odd and fleet-tactical than a Steamrunner, and less explorer-polished than an Intrepid. Corridors are broad enough for medical carts and cargo cases in the main spine, but side spaces and lower access remain tight.

The class should feel like a ship that can reconfigure public rooms quickly: a wardroom becomes a briefing for local leaders, a cargo bay becomes a seed-counting station, a lab becomes a quarantine support room, and the shuttlebay becomes a threshold between contaminated outside and accountable inside.

## 4.2 Directive deck baseline

This is a runtime consistency baseline, not canonical deck evidence.

| Deck or zone | Baseline use |
|---|---|
| Deck 1 | Bridge, command support, tactical overview |
| Deck 2 | Ready room, briefing room, senior offices |
| Deck 3 | Senior quarters, communications, diplomatic support |
| Deck 4 | Mission ops, convoy control, public-accounting room |
| Deck 5 | Sickbay, quarantine ward, medical lab |
| Deck 6 | Biological/agricultural labs, decon rooms, sample custody |
| Decks 7-8 | Relief cargo, seed vaults, ration stores, field equipment |
| Deck 9 | Aft centerline shuttlebay, small-craft maintenance, intake screening |
| Decks 10-11 | Main engineering, power transfer, pylon field support |
| Deck 12 | Transporter rooms, security, armory, brig |
| Decks 13-14 | Lower support, keel maintenance, environmental and waste processing |

Campaigns may override names and refit details, but runtime narration should keep the class's central relief spine, aft pylon-gap shuttlebay, clean biological workspaces, and visible tactical/convoy authority.

## 4.3 Sensory palette

Use sensory detail as operational information.

| Sense | Baseline detail | Under pressure |
|---|---|---|
| Sound | Cart wheels, decon doors, LCARS tones, cargo lift motors, low pylon thrum | Quarantine alarms, sanitation pumps, crowd noise, pylon stress warnings, bow-emitter charge |
| Light | Clean white medical light, green/amber biology displays, warm corridor strips, cargo bay strobes | Blue-white decon glare, red alert, quarantine field shimmer, emergency grow-lamp spill |
| Touch | Smooth sealed counters, cargo straps, sterile gloves, deck tie-downs, handrails along main spine | Sticky decon residue, hot pylon access, vibration from convoy maneuvers, cold sample lockers |
| Smell | Neutral filtered air, antiseptic, hydroponic humidity, cargo fabric, replicator grain | Smoke, disinfectant, soil samples, scorched insulation, chemical crop stabilizers |
| Movement | Main-spine traffic, decon checkpoints, cargo lifts, shuttle-to-lab routing | Sealed corridors, one-way quarantine flow, crowded relief intake, Jefferies crawl under pylon stress |

## 4.4 Room-to-hull logic

The Norway's body should feel like a broad flat platform with a central mission spine. Command sits forward/dorsal. Mission ops and relief accounting sit near the public part of the spine. Medical, biological labs, and cargo sit close enough to move samples and supplies without contaminating crew quarters. Engineering and pylon support sit aft/lower. Small craft enter through the aft centerline gap and feed directly into intake, cargo, or decon routes.

# 5. Key Area Profiles

Each area profile uses the character-bible idea of engine, contradiction, tells, and reveal pressure, adapted for locations.

## 5.1 Bridge, Deck 1

### At a glance

| Field | Baseline |
|---|---|
| Location | Deck 1, forward/dorsal command module |
| Function | Command, convoy coordination, tactical deterrence, relief prioritization |
| Scene use | Acting command, crisis triage, convoy defense, public accountability |
| Avoid | Defiant-style war room, Intrepid bridge clone, hospital-only tone |

### Location engine

The bridge turns care into orders that can be defended.

### Central contradiction

The bridge has tactical clarity while the mission often has humanitarian ambiguity.

### Physical identity

Use a compact 2370s bridge with central command, forward conn/ops, tactical station with bow-emitter and shield status, mission/convoy display, and medical/logistics status feeds. The bridge should support fast movement between relief decisions and defensive posture.

### Pressure behavior

Under pressure, the bridge becomes a triage board. Convoy position, quarantine state, crop stores, medical load, and threat arcs all compete for command attention.

### Scene shapes

- The player must choose which colony receives escort first.
- Tactical asks permission to charge the bow emitter while relief staff are negotiating trust.
- A quarantine alarm interrupts a diplomatic call.

## 5.2 Ready room and briefing room, Deck 2

### At a glance

| Field | Baseline |
|---|---|
| Location | Adjacent to bridge or directly below command |
| Function | Private command ownership and cross-discipline planning |
| Scene use | Relief policy, quarantine ethics, public accounting, command transition |

### Location engine

These rooms turn urgent care into rules the ship can follow.

### Physical identity

The ready room is compact and practical: desk, secure terminal, replicator, status display, and one or two personal objects. The briefing room has a table, wall displays for convoy maps and supply bands, and enough medical/science access to handle crisis planning.

### Pressure behavior

Under pressure, the briefing room becomes a contested aid office. Doctors, scientists, engineers, tactical officers, and local leaders all argue from true priorities that cannot all win.

### Scene shapes

- A local official demands seed access before decontamination is complete.
- The doctor and tactical officer disagree over quarantine enforcement.
- The player signs a public accounting statement that will anger someone either way.

### Location engine

Mission ops turns compassion into numbers that can be checked.

### Central contradiction

The room exists to make relief fair, but visible fairness can create panic when there is not enough.

### Pressure behavior

### Scene shapes

## 5.5 Biological and agricultural labs, Deck 6

### At a glance

| Field | Baseline |
|---|---|

| Function | Crop analysis, pathogen work, seed testing, ecological modeling |
| Scene use | Food transition, contamination, scientific dissent, moral delay |

### Location engine

The labs turn survival into evidence before it becomes distribution.

### Central contradiction

Every hour spent testing can save thousands or starve hundreds. Science is care, but it can look like delay.

### Physical identity

Use sealed benches, growth cabinets, sample lockers, portable field kit racks, decon pass-throughs, microscope/LCARS stations, and crop-condition displays. Let the room smell faintly of antiseptic, soil, and humid grow lamps when appropriate.

### Pressure behavior

Under pressure, lab authority strains against command urgency. Scientists must say "not yet" while the cargo bay fills with people who need "now."

### Scene shapes

- A seed lot passes one test and fails another.
- A scientist refuses to certify a crop because the model is politically convenient, not true.
- An officer carries a sample through decon while a convoy waits.

# 6. Ship Systems as Story Pressure

## 6.1 Bow phaser emitter and defensive posture

### System engine

The Norway's bow emitter gives it visible deterrence. It protects relief operations and complicates them.

### Story use

Use the bow emitter when:

- a convoy needs defense;
- a desperate local force challenges relief access;
- Starfleet help is perceived as coercive;
- tactical readiness risks trust.

### Avoid

Do not make the ship solve famine, disease, or sovereignty with weapon fire.

## 6.2 Quarantine and decontamination systems

### System engine

Norway relief stories should make quarantine physical: fields, doors, traffic flow, decon residue, access logs, and emotional cost.

### Story use

Use quarantine when:

- exposure may spread through cargo or crew;
- someone powerful demands an exception;
- medical truth conflicts with public confidence;
- a patient is safe but isolated.

### Avoid

Do not use quarantine as a generic locked-door trope. It is a medical, ethical, and logistical system.

## 6.3 Relief cargo, seed custody, and cold chain

### System engine

Cargo is a life-support system for the region. It has temperature, inventory, custody, expiration, destination, and legitimacy.

### Story use

Use cargo systems when:

- seed or medicine must remain secure and viable;
- a colony disputes allocation;
- refrigeration or environmental control fails;
- public accounting must prove the ship is not hoarding.

### Avoid

Do not let replicators erase agricultural or logistical scarcity. Energy, patterns, scale, biology, local autonomy, and trust matter.

## 6.4 Pylon field support and compact power grid

### System engine

The wing-pylon geometry gives the class a distinctive aft body. Power, warp field, shield, and shuttlebay operations can interfere under stress.

### Story use

Use pylon systems when:

- shuttles approach through the aft gap;
- warp stress limits convoy timing;
- pylon damage threatens the bay lane;
- power demands from cargo, quarantine, and shields collide.

### Avoid

Do not make the pylon gap a decorative slot with no operational consequence.

## 6.5 Public accounting

### System engine

Norway relief missions need trust. Trust needs numbers that people can inspect and challenge.

### Story use

Use public-accounting systems when:

- supply lists change;
- a colony alleges favoritism;
- the ship must prove contamination status;
- command must decide how much truth the public can bear.

### Avoid

Do not hide every moral choice in Director-only state. Some pressure should be visible to characters and player.

# 8. Director Retrieval Guidance

## 8.1 Injection rule

When a Norway area or system is relevant, inject compact ship facts in this order:

1. Hard anchor: class geometry, deck/zone, exterior placement, or system truth.
2. Physical texture: one or two details the narrator can use.
3. Operational pressure: what the area/system is for and what it costs.
4. Prohibition: only when a common wrong trope is likely.

Example for "shuttlebay":

```text
Ship anchor: Directive Norway baseline places the main shuttlebay at the aft centerline, opening into the gap between wing-shaped warp pylons. Small craft approach from astern through the pylon gap, with bay control watching shields, pylon field stress, decon readiness, and cargo/medical intake. Do not depict a saucer-underbelly or Galaxy-style dorsal bay.
```

Example for "relief cargo":

```text
Ship anchor: Norway relief holds sit along the central mission spine near labs, decon, and shuttlebay routing. Cargo has custody, temperature, viability, destination, and public-accounting constraints; replicators cannot erase regional food or seed scarcity.
```

## 8.3 Over-injection guard

Do not inject every matching fact. If a player says "I go to sickbay," the scene does not need class length, Battle of Sector 001 history, and pylon geometry. It needs medical/quarantine placement, texture, and the immediate command pressure.

Use high detail when:

- a scene touches shuttlebay approach, relief cargo, seed custody, quarantine, public accounting, pylon damage, or bow-emitter deterrence;
- a generated response is likely to hallucinate a generic saucer bay or Intrepid layout;
- a command decision depends on scarcity, contamination, or convoy protection;
- contradiction guards need an explicit prohibition.

Use low detail when:

- the area is only a backdrop;
- character work is the scene's main payload;
- the same ship fact was just established;
- the player does not need technical overhead to act.

# 9. Portrayal Principles

1. **Care is physical.** Food, seed, medicine, quarantine, decon, and cargo should have locations, limits, and consequences.
2. **Protection is morally complicated.** Tactical systems enable relief but can make help feel coercive.
3. **The pylon gap matters.** Norway small-craft recovery uses the aft centerline gap in Directive baseline.
4. **The bow emitter is a class tell.** Use it as deterrence and perception pressure, not as a universal solution.
5. **Replicators do not solve agriculture.** Scale, biology, seed viability, local autonomy, and trust remain story constraints.
6. **Quarantine has flow.** Doors, fields, pass-throughs, logs, and emotional isolation should shape scenes.
7. **Scarcity must be visible.** Relief ledgers, cargo boards, and public accounting make hard choices playable.
8. **Damage has a location.** Use pylon field support, cargo environmental control, decon pumps, bow emitter, keel access, or shuttlebay threshold.
9. **Campaign ships own their modifications.** Celandine may specialize in agricultural science and transition relief, but class truth remains the common frame.
10. **Do not import another class's deck map.** Intrepid, Steamrunner, New Orleans, Defiant, and Galaxy assumptions must not overwrite Norway geometry.
